# What Happens If the Tenant Doesn't Sign

**Summary:** If a tenant does not sign their lease, the unit stays available and you can resend the link, update the tenant's details, or cancel the lease and start fresh.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23

---

## How long does the tenant have?

Signing links do not expire automatically, but Klikk sends automatic reminder emails to unsigned tenants at these intervals:

- **24 hours** after the initial send
- **72 hours** after the initial send
- **7 days** after the initial send

You can always send a manual reminder from the Signing Progress panel at any time.

---

## What you can do

### Option 1 — Resend the signing link

If the tenant says they did not receive the email:

1. Ask them to check their spam or junk folder first.
2. Go to **Leases** → open the lease → **Signing Progress** panel.
3. Click **"Resend Link"** next to the tenant's name.
4. A new email is sent immediately.

> **Screenshot:** Signing Progress panel — "Resend Link" button next to the unsigned tenant row.

---

### Option 2 — Update the tenant's email address

If the email address is wrong:

1. Go to **Leases** → open the lease → **Tenants** section.
2. Click the pencil icon next to the tenant name.
3. Update the email address and click **"Save"**.
4. Go back to **Signing Progress** and click **"Resend Link"**.

> Note: You can only update the email while the lease is still in **Pending Signing** status. Once a party has signed, their details are locked.

---

### Option 3 — Cancel the lease and start over

If the tenant has pulled out or you need to make material changes to the lease:

1. Go to the lease detail page.
2. Click **"Cancel Lease"** in the top-right menu (the three-dot icon).
3. Confirm the cancellation. The lease moves to **Cancelled** status.
4. The unit status returns to **Available**.
5. You can create a new lease for the same property.

> **Screenshot:** Three-dot menu on the lease detail page — "Cancel Lease" option at the bottom in red.

---

## Does the unit stay occupied while waiting?

No. A unit is only marked **Occupied** once the lease is fully signed by all parties and the status is **Active**. While the lease is in **Pending Signing**, the unit stays **Available** in your reports and on the owner dashboard.

---

## What if the tenant signed but the agent hasn't?

The lease is not complete until the agent counter-signs. If you have not yet signed:

1. Go to **Leases** and look for leases with the **"Awaiting Agent Signature"** badge.
2. Click the lease and then click **"Sign Now"**.

Klikk also sends you a reminder email once all tenants have signed.
