# How to Generate a Lease

**Summary:** Use Klikk's AI lease builder to create a Rental Housing Act-compliant lease in minutes — just answer a few questions and the document is ready.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23

---

## Before you start

- The property must have a **signed mandate** on file. You cannot generate a lease without one.
- You need the agreed rental amount, deposit amount, lease start and end dates, and the names of all adult occupants.

---

## Steps

### 1. Open the property

Go to **Properties** and click the property you want to generate a lease for.

---

### 2. Start a new lease

Click the **Leases** tab, then click **"New Lease"**.

> **Screenshot:** Leases tab — empty state or existing leases list with a pink "New Lease" button in the top right corner.

---

### 3. Choose a lease template

Klikk shows a list of available lease templates. For most residential properties, select **"Standard Residential Lease (RHA 2023)"**.

If your agency has custom templates, they appear at the top of the list with your agency logo.

> **Screenshot:** Template selector — "Standard Residential Lease (RHA 2023)" highlighted.

---

### 4. Fill in the lease details

A step-by-step form collects the key lease terms:

| Step | Fields |
|---|---|
| **Property** | Address, unit number (pre-filled from property record) |
| **Parties** | Tenant name(s) and ID numbers, landlord/agency details |
| **Term** | Start date, end date, notice period |
| **Financials** | Monthly rental, deposit (typically 1–2 months), pro-rata for first month |
| **Occupants** | Names of all people who will live in the property |
| **Special conditions** | Pets, parking, garden maintenance (optional free-text) |

Click **"Next"** after each step. All fields marked with a red asterisk (*) are required under the RHA.

> **Screenshot:** Financials step — monthly rental, deposit, and pro-rata fields.

---

### 5. Review the AI-generated document

After you complete the form, Klikk generates the full lease document. Click **"Preview"** to read it. The AI fills in the standard clauses automatically.

If anything looks wrong, click **"Edit"** to go back to the relevant step and correct it.

> **Screenshot:** Lease preview — full document with merge fields highlighted in blue.

---

### 6. Add or remove clauses (optional)

Click **"Edit Document"** to open the clause editor. You can:

- Remove any optional clause by clicking the bin icon next to it.
- Add a clause from the library by clicking **"Add Clause"**.
- Type a custom clause directly into the editor.

> **Screenshot:** Clause editor — library panel open on the right, existing clauses listed on the left.

---

### 7. Save the lease

Click **"Save Lease"**. The lease moves to **Draft** status and appears under the Leases tab.

From here you can add the tenant (see [How to invite a tenant](how-to-invite-a-tenant.md)) and then send the lease for signing.

---

## Tips

- **Deposits:** Under the RHA, the deposit must be placed in an interest-bearing account. Klikk tracks the deposit amount but does not process the payment — record it in your trust account as usual.
- **Fixed vs. month-to-month:** Choose an end date for a fixed-term lease. Leave the end date blank for month-to-month, and set the notice period (typically 1 calendar month).
- **Multiple tenants:** You can add more than one tenant to a lease. All named tenants will receive signing links.
