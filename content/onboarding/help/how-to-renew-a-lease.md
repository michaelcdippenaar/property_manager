# How to Renew a Lease

**Summary:** When a fixed-term lease is approaching its end date, use the Klikk renewal flow to agree new terms, generate the renewal document, and get it signed — all without starting from scratch.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23

---

## When does Klikk remind me?

Klikk sends you an automatic reminder when a lease is **90 days** from its end date. You will see:

- An in-app notification
- An email summary with the lease details and a **"Start Renewal"** button

You do not have to wait for the reminder — you can start a renewal at any time.

---

## Steps

### 1. Open the lease

Go to **Leases** and find the lease you want to renew. It may show a **"Renewal Due"** badge if it is within the 90-day window.

> **Screenshot:** Lease list — a lease card with an amber "Renewal Due" badge and the end date shown below it.

---

### 2. Click "Renew Lease"

On the lease detail page, click **"Renew Lease"** in the top-right action area.

> **Screenshot:** Lease detail page — "Renew Lease" button highlighted, next to the "..." menu.

---

### 3. Choose renewal type

Select how you want to renew:

| Option | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Fixed term** | New end date — e.g. another 12 months |
| **Month-to-month** | Lease continues with no fixed end date |

> **Screenshot:** Renewal type selector — two radio buttons, "Fixed term" selected.

---

### 4. Update the terms

A pre-filled form shows the current lease terms. Update anything that is changing:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| New start date | Defaults to the day after the current end date |
| New end date | For fixed-term renewals |
| New monthly rental | Enter the new amount — Klikk notes the percentage increase |
| New deposit (if applicable) | Usually stays the same; update only if terms require it |
| Special conditions | Add, remove, or edit clauses |

> **Screenshot:** Renewal terms form — "New monthly rental" field showing the current amount with a note "R500 increase (8.3%) from current rent."

---

### 5. Preview and confirm the renewal document

Click **"Preview Renewal"**. Klikk generates a renewal addendum using the existing lease as the base document, with only the changed terms shown.

If everything looks right, click **"Confirm Terms"**.

> **Screenshot:** Renewal document preview — one-page addendum showing changed fields highlighted in blue.

---

### 6. Send for signing

Click **"Send for Signing"**. The renewal follows the same signing flow as a new lease:

1. Tenant(s) sign first.
2. You counter-sign.

The tenant receives an email with a new signing link. Remind them to sign before the current lease end date.

> **Screenshot:** Signing confirmation dialog — "Renewal sent to [tenant name] for signing."

---

### 7. Renewal activates automatically

Once both parties have signed, the renewal lease activates on the new start date. The old lease closes and the new one becomes the active record. Payment references update to reflect the new rent amount from the new start month.

> **Screenshot:** Lease detail page — status shows "Renewal Active from 1 June 2026."

---

## What if the tenant does not want to renew?

If the tenant is leaving, do not start a renewal. The lease will expire on its end date and the unit will change to **Available** automatically. You can then begin the process of finding a new tenant.

If the tenant wants to leave before the end date, that is a lease termination — contact support for guidance, as this may require a signed termination agreement under the RHA.
