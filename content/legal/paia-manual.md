## Part A: Introduction

### A1. Purpose

The Promotion of Access to Information Act 2 of 2000 ("PAIA") gives effect to the constitutional right of access to information held by private bodies, as protected under section 32 of the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa. This manual is compiled in terms of section 51 of PAIA and is intended to assist data subjects in exercising their rights.

## Part C: How to request access

### C1. Who may request

Any person (natural or juristic) may request access to records held by Klikk. Data subjects have the right to request their own PI. Third-party requests are subject to privacy protections.

### C2. Form 2 — Request for access to records

A formal PAIA request must be submitted using **Form 2** (prescribed under the PAIA Regulations).

**Download Form 2:** [PDF link — to be hosted at klikk.co.za/docs/paia-form-2.pdf]

Form 2 must be completed in full and submitted to:

- **Email:** info-officer@klikk.co.za (preferred — attach completed PDF)
- **Post:** Information Officer, Klikk (Pty) Ltd, PO Box [XXX], [City], [Postal Code]
- **Hand delivery:** [Physical address — to be inserted]

### C3. Request fee

A request fee of **R140.00** is payable before the request is processed, as prescribed by the PAIA Regulations (GN R187 of 15 February 2002, as amended). This fee may be waived at our discretion for requests relating to your own PI.

### C4. Access fee

Where access is granted, an access fee may be charged to recover the reasonable cost of reproduction. Fee schedule:

| Format | Fee |
|--------|-----|
| Printed copy (per page) | R2.00 |
| Electronic copy (email) | R0.00 for first 20 pages; R2.00 per page thereafter |
| CD/USB (per disc/drive) | R70.00 |
| Inspection of records (per hour or part thereof) | R145.00 |

Persons who can demonstrate that they cannot afford the fee may apply for a waiver (Form 2, Section 4).

### C5. Response time

Klikk will respond within **30 days** of receiving a properly completed Form 2 and any applicable request fee (PAIA s56). This period may be extended by a further 30 days where the request requires extensive searching or is for a large volume of records (PAIA s57).

### C6. Grounds for refusal

Access may be refused on grounds including (non-exhaustive):

- The record contains the PI of a third party who has not consented
- Disclosure would be unreasonably prejudicial to commercial interests
- The record is subject to legal professional privilege
- Disclosure could endanger the safety of a person
- The record does not exist or cannot be found after reasonable search

If access is refused, Klikk will provide written reasons. The requester may apply to the Information Regulator or court for review.
