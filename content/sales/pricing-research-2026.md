## Executive Summary

SA rental-platform pricing falls into two structural camps: **per-unit/month** (PayProp, WeConnectU, RentMaster, HouseME) and **flat monthly subscription** (Prop Data enterprise quote, Klikk flat-rate). Klikk's current pricing structure — flat R2,500/month Pro, flat R4,000/month base Enterprise — is well-positioned below the break-even unit counts vs per-unit competitors, but the top tier's internal name (`enterprise`) creates a segment mismatch: the features and price point align better with mid-market agencies (20–100 units) than with true enterprise buyers. The recommended tier name for the top self-serve tier is **"Agency"**, with rationale detailed in Section 4.

## 2. Willingness-to-Pay Analysis

### Methodology

SA-specific willingness-to-pay data is sparse — no published SA property industry WTP survey was found for rental management software specifically (as at April 2026). This analysis synthesises:

1. **Competitor pricing as revealed preference** — what agents are currently paying reveals their floor. WeConnectU's R150–200/unit market position and continued customer retention implies WTP at that level for a comprehensive feature set.
2. **Review and forum sentiment** — G2, Capterra, and SA property forums. Common themes: price sensitivity at small portfolio sizes; frustration with per-unit models that penalise growth; preference for predictable monthly costs.
3. **ICP interview proxies** — the ICP document (`content/sales/icp.md`) contains management fee estimates and revenue model data from which value anchors can be derived.
4. **Competitor free trial / freemium behaviour** — RentMaster's 30-day trial, Rentpost's freemium for 5 units, and the absence of freemium from WeConnectU and Prop Data suggests upper-market players expect paid commitment from the first conversation.

---

### 2.1 Small Portfolio (1–20 Units)

**Segment:** Independent landlord (T2 ICP) and very small boutique PM (T1 ICP, sub-20 units).

**Revenue context:** A portfolio of 10 units at an average Cape Town rental of R10,000/month generates R100,000/month in gross rent. A 7–10% management fee = R7,000–R10,000/month agency revenue. For a self-managing landlord, 10 × R10,000 = R100,000/month rental income.

**WTP benchmarks:**

| Metric | Low | Mid | High | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ZAR/unit/month | R30 | R50 | R80 | Competitor RentMaster R50–80/unit; self-managing landlords most price-sensitive |
| ZAR/month flat | R0–R300 | R500 | R1,000 | Spreadsheet/WhatsApp inertia; anything above R1,000/month requires strong value demonstration |
| % of management fee acceptable | 3–5% | 5–7% | 10% | Platform cost as % of agency revenue from portfolio |

**Insight:** This segment has the lowest absolute WTP but the largest volume. A free tier up to 5 properties (Klikk's current model) captures this segment and creates upgrade pathways. The ceiling for paid conversion is approximately R500–1,000/month before price becomes a conversion blocker. Klikk's Pro tier at R2,500/month is above the conversion ceiling for a 10-unit portfolio — the "Pro is right when you hit 20–25 units" messaging is correct. The free tier (0–5 units) bridges the gap.

**Klikk alignment:** Free tier (0–5 properties) is correctly priced at R0. Pro at R2,500/month becomes cost-effective vs RentMaster (~R1,250/month for 25 units at R50/unit) at exactly 25 units, and represents strong value over RentMaster at 50 units (R2,500–R4,000 vs R2,500 flat). The current pricing handles this segment well.

---

### 2.2 Mid Portfolio (21–100 Units)

**Segment:** Boutique PM firm (T1 ICP, primary) and smaller rental departments (T3 ICP, entry).

**Revenue context:** 50 units at R10,000/month average = R500,000/month gross rent. Management fee at 8% = R40,000/month agency revenue. 100 units = R80,000/month agency revenue.

**WTP benchmarks:**

| Metric | Low | Mid | High | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ZAR/unit/month | R40 | R60 | R100 | WeConnectU/Prop Data indicative; professional agencies expect to pay for capability |
| ZAR/month flat | R1,500 | R2,500 | R5,000 | Forum discussions; "sweet spot" for boutique agency willingness |
| % of management fee acceptable | 3–5% | 6–8% | 10% | Klikk at R2,500 = 6.25% of R40,000 mgmt fee (50 units) — within acceptable range |

**Insight:** This is Klikk's primary value zone. The flat R2,500/month Pro model is at the mid WTP for this segment. At 50 units it is cheaper than RentMaster. At 100 units it is dramatically cheaper than WeConnectU (R2,500 vs R15,000–R20,000). The per-unit derived price of R25/unit at 100 units is below any named competitor's published or indicative rate.

Forum sentiment consistently shows that agencies in this range are frustrated with WeConnectU pricing as their portfolio grows — "it gets expensive fast" is a common complaint. This is Klikk's strongest displacement vector.

**Klikk alignment:** Pro tier (R2,500/month, up to 100 units) is correctly positioned for this segment. The transition from Pro to the current Enterprise tier at 100+ units at R4,000/month base + R50/property above 100 is a reasonable step-up. However, see the naming concern in Section 4 — "Enterprise" may signal the wrong segment to this audience.

---

### 2.3 Large Portfolio (100+ Units)

**Segment:** Estate agency rental department (T3 ICP), larger PM companies, franchise branches.

**Revenue context:** 200 units at R12,000/month average = R2,400,000/month gross rent. Management fee at 8% = R192,000/month agency revenue. The platform cost at R4,000–R6,000/month represents 2–3% of revenue.

**WTP benchmarks:**

| Metric | Low | Mid | High | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ZAR/unit/month | R30 | R50 | R80 | WeConnectU/Prop Data dominant at this size; negotiated volume pricing expected |
| ZAR/month (200 units) | R5,000 | R8,000 | R15,000 | Prop Data and WeConnectU indicative; this segment expects customised pricing |
| % of management fee acceptable | 2–4% | 4–6% | 8% | Higher absolute revenue means lower acceptable % for software |

**Insight:** Large portfolios expect negotiation. A R4,000 base + R50/property model gives a 200-unit agency a bill of R4,000 + (100 × R50) = R9,000/month — well below the WeConnectU or Prop Data benchmark for equivalent unit counts. This is strong value, but the naming and framing needs to be "Agency" scale, not "Enterprise" scale (the latter implies a custom-quote enterprise deal, which creates wrong expectations).

For true enterprise (300+ units, franchise, institutional), a separate "Custom / Enterprise" tier at quoted pricing remains appropriate.

**Klikk alignment:** The overage model (R50/property above 100) is structurally sound. The price points are competitive. The tier naming is the main correction required — see Section 4.

## 4. Tier-Naming Recommendation

### 4.1 Background

The current `pricing.yaml` uses `enterprise` as the key for the top self-serve tier (R4,000/month base, 100+ units, white-label, SLA). The display name in pricing.yaml is `"Enterprise"`. DEC-016 asks whether this should be renamed to `Agency`, `Professional`, or another label.

This matters because:
1. The word "Enterprise" in SaaS signals "large corporation, custom quote, long sales cycle." Klikk's top self-serve tier is not that — it is for agencies managing 100–300 units, not Fortune 500 firms.
2. Competitors that SA agents know and compare against (WeConnectU, PayProp, Prop Data) use segment-based naming (`Professional`, `Premier`, agency-size language), not tech-company enterprise naming.
3. The ICP for this tier is a rental agency principal or department head. They do not self-identify as "enterprise" — they self-identify as "a mid-size agency" or "a growing property management company."

---

### 4.2 Options Evaluated

#### Option A: "Agency" (Recommended)

**Label:** Agency
**Subtitle suggestion:** "For rental departments and growing agencies."

**Pros:**
- Directly maps to the ICP segment — rental agents and agency principals understand the word
- SA property industry uses "agency" universally for this business type (PPRA registers "estate agencies")
- Signals that this tier was built for property professionals, not generic enterprise buyers
- Creates a clear naming ladder: Free → Pro → Agency → Custom
- Aligns with how WeConnectU, PayProp, and Prop Data describe their equivalent customer — the "agency" segment
- Avoids the "Enterprise" connotation of custom pricing and long sales cycles
- Short, memorable, and SA-market-native

**Cons:**
- "Agency" is generic — landlords managing properties through an agency structure may also qualify
- May imply the lower tiers are "non-agency" quality (mitigated by Pro's strong feature set)

**Verdict:** Strongest option. The word "Agency" resonates with Klikk's T3 ICP (estate agency rental department) and upper T1 ICP (boutique PM company), which together represent the addressable market for this tier.

---

#### Option B: "Professional" (Strong second option)

**Label:** Professional
**Subtitle suggestion:** "Full platform for professional property managers."

**Pros:**
- Common SaaS naming convention — widely recognised from Canva Pro / Professional, HubSpot Professional, etc.
- Signals advanced capability without implying enterprise-scale complexity or pricing
- Inclusive — applies to boutique PM firms, rental departments, and larger landlords who see themselves as "professional"
- Creates a clear ladder: Free → Pro → Professional → Custom (though "Pro" and "Professional" are close in meaning)

**Cons:**
- "Pro" and "Professional" are conceptually overlapping — the ladder loses clarity ("Pro" vs "Professional" is confusing)
- Less SA-market-specific than "Agency"
- Does not signal the specific agency/rental-department audience as directly

**Verdict:** Strong second option if the current `pro` tier is renamed (e.g. to "Growth" or "Standard"). If `pro` stays as `pro`, using "Professional" for the next tier up creates naming confusion. Only viable if the tier below is renamed simultaneously.

---

#### Option C: "Enterprise" (Current — Not Recommended for Display)

**Label:** Enterprise

**Pros:**
- Familiar enterprise SaaS terminology
- Signals top-tier, full-capability product
- No naming change required

**Cons:**
- "Enterprise" in SA property market implies corporate real estate funds or institutional portfolios — not the agency or PM firm that is Klikk's actual T3 ICP
- Creates wrong expectation of pricing (custom quote) vs Klikk's published flat-rate model
- In a sales conversation, "Enterprise tier" from an unknown brand sounds like a longer procurement process, not a self-serve upgrade
- Competitor naming at this segment (WeConnectU "Professional/Premium", Prop Data branch licence) does not use "enterprise" for the 100-unit segment
- Klikk already has a `custom` tier below this that serves true enterprise — having both "Enterprise" and "Custom" creates naming hierarchy confusion

**Verdict:** Retire as the display name for the top self-serve tier. Retain as the internal `yaml` key if desired to avoid breaking any downstream references, but update the display `name:` field.

---

#### Option D: "Premier" or "Premium"

**Label:** Premier / Premium

**Pros:**
- Signals top-of-range without enterprise connotations
- Used by several SA platforms (FNB Premier, Standard Bank Premium, Vodacom Premium) — familiar register

**Cons:**
- Vague — does not convey who this tier is for
- "Premium" is overused in SA SaaS and banking; signals added cost, not added fit
- Less differentiated than "Agency" in a property-management context

**Verdict:** Weaker than Agency or Professional. Not recommended.

---

#### Option E: "Scale"

**Label:** Scale

**Pros:**
- Modern SaaS convention (Linear, Notion, Vercel all use growth/scale tier names)
- Signals the use case: growing portfolio, scaling operations

**Cons:**
- No SA property industry precedent — would feel foreign in context
- Does not map to how agents self-describe their business
- Risk of tech-company aesthetic that alienates traditional agency principals

**Verdict:** Suitable for a tech-native audience; not recommended for SA rental agency market.

---

### 4.3 Recommendation

**Rename the top self-serve tier display name from "Enterprise" to "Agency".**

- Keep the internal YAML key as `enterprise` to avoid downstream code breakage, unless the engineering cost of renaming is acceptable.
- Update `name:` in `pricing.yaml` from `"Enterprise"` to `"Agency"`.
- Update `subtitle:` to reflect the segment: e.g. _"100+ properties. Your brand. Your infrastructure."_ (current subtitle is already reasonable — can be kept).
- The naming ladder becomes: **Free → Pro → Agency → Custom** — clean, intuitive, and SA-market-native.
- The `Custom` tier (POA) remains correctly named for bespoke / enterprise engagements.

## 5. Klikk Pricing Assessment vs Market

### 5.1 Is Klikk Pro (R2,500/month) correctly priced?

**Assessment: Yes, at the upper end of the value zone — price should stay.**

At R2,500/month flat for up to 100 units:
- Break-even vs RentMaster (~R50/unit): 50 units — Pro is cheaper at 50+ units
- Break-even vs WeConnectU (~R150/unit): 17 units — Pro is dramatically cheaper at 17+ units
- Derived unit price at full capacity (100 units): R25/unit — the lowest in the market for a comparable feature set

**Risk if price is lowered:** Cheapness signal. At R2,500/month, Pro is already considerably cheaper than the incumbents for mid-portfolio size. Lowering it further risks signalling a low-quality product before the brand is established. Klikk's AI differentiation supports the current price.

**Risk if price is raised:** Conversion friction for T1 boutique agencies (20–50 units) where the ICP has been managing on R50–R80/unit tools. R3,000+ monthly flat may face resistance at the low end of this segment. Current R2,500/month is the ceiling for this segment without a stronger trust base.

**Conclusion:** R2,500/month is correctly positioned. No change recommended.

---

### 5.2 Is Klikk Agency/Enterprise (R4,000/month + R50/property above 100) correctly priced?

**Assessment: Yes, structurally sound — minor overage rate consideration.**

At R4,000/month base + R50/property above 100:
- 200-unit portfolio: R4,000 + (100 × R50) = R9,000/month vs WeConnectU ~R30,000–R40,000/month
- 150-unit portfolio: R4,000 + (50 × R50) = R6,500/month
- Derived per-unit at 200 units: R45/unit — below all named competitors

**The R50/property overage rate** is aggressive. RentMaster charges R50–R80/unit; Klikk charges R50/property (not unit) above the base 100 properties. At multi-unit properties (a 20-unit complex = 1 property, 20 units), this is far lower than the per-unit competition. This is a significant selling point for agencies managing complexes.

**Potential concern:** The word "property" vs "unit" in the overage creates potential confusion (and potential underbilling if properties contain many units). DEC-016 should clarify this when tier naming is finalised.

**Conclusion:** Pricing is competitive and structurally sound. The overage mechanism (properties not units) is a differentiated selling point for complex management. Retain current pricing; clarify property vs unit definition in customer-facing copy.

---

### 5.3 Free tier (R0, 5 properties): correctly structured?

**Assessment: Yes.**

No SA competitor offers a genuine free tier. RentMaster offers a 30-day trial; Rentpost offers freemium (5 units, US market). Klikk's permanent free tier for up to 5 properties with a full AI suite (BYOK) is a unique market position. It serves:
- T2 independent landlords (who may never convert to paid — acceptable brand/referral value)
- Early trial / proof-of-concept for T1 boutique agencies before committing to Pro
- A BYOK AI model that means the free tier costs Klikk minimal LLM cost (user supplies their own key)

Retain free tier as-is.
