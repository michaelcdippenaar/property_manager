# Klikk Rentals — Objection Handling Guide

_Top 10 objections for rental agents, boutique property managers, and self-managing landlords. Responses are grounded in BUILT features only. Last updated: 2026-04-22._

---

## How to use this document

Read the objection category first to understand what the prospect is really saying beneath the surface. The **core response** is the scripted version. **Supporting points** give you material to go deeper if needed. **What not to say** flags the traps.

---

## Objection 1 — "We already use PayProp"

**What they really mean:** "We have a payment workflow we trust. Don't ask us to rip it out."

**Core response:**

> "Klikk doesn't replace PayProp — it sits alongside it. PayProp is excellent at what it does: collecting rent and disbursing to landlords. Klikk manages everything PayProp doesn't: AI lease generation, maintenance dispatch, tenant communication, owner reporting, and e-signing. Your payment workflow stays exactly as it is. We're building a direct integration so rent data flows across without manual entry."

**Supporting points:**
- Most WeConnectU users also keep PayProp. Same logic applies.
- Klikk's rent tracking uses unique payment references — this works with any payment method, including EFT into a PayProp trust account.
- The integration is on the roadmap (not yet built — do not imply it works today).

**What not to say:**
- Do not say "you won't need PayProp anymore" — that is not true and not the goal.
- Do not promise a timeline on the PayProp integration.

## Objection 2 — "I don't trust AI to write legal documents"

**What they really mean:** "I'm worried AI will produce something wrong and I'll be liable for it."

**Core response:**

> "Completely understandable — and you're right to ask. The AI drafts the lease from your template; you review and approve every word before it goes out. Think of it as a fast paralegal, not a replacement for your judgement. Every clause is editable in our rich text editor. You can build a library of pre-approved clauses so the AI only uses language you've already signed off on. The base templates are built for the Rental Housing Act — you start from a compliant foundation and customise from there."

**Supporting points:**
- The AI (Claude) does not invent clauses. It populates from your template and drafts from context you give it.
- You can lock any clause so the AI cannot modify it.
- The clause library means recurring clauses (pet policy, utilities, maintenance responsibility) are reused verbatim once you've approved them once.
- If the prospect is a lawyer or has a legal background: "You can upload your own lease template. The AI populates the merge fields and structures the document — your clauses, your wording."

**What not to say:**
- Do not say "the AI is always accurate" or imply legal review is unnecessary.
- Do not dismiss the concern — it is valid and shows sophistication.

## Objection 3 — "It's too expensive"

**What they really mean:** "I can't see the ROI yet, or I'm comparing to doing it manually (which feels free)."

**Core response:**

> "The free tier covers up to five properties — no credit card, no time limit. That's zero cost to start. For larger portfolios, Pro is R2,500 per month for up to 50 properties or 100 units. For a 20-unit portfolio, that's R125 per unit per month — less than a single hour of admin time per unit, per month. Most property managers save 5–10 admin hours per month just on lease creation and maintenance coordination. The question is not whether R2,500 is expensive — it's whether the time you save is worth more than that."

**Supporting points:**
- Compare to WeConnectU: R150–200/unit/month for a comparable portfolio. A 20-unit portfolio on WeConnectU costs R3,000–R4,000/month. Klikk Pro is R2,500.
- For T3 agencies displacing a part-time admin: "A part-time admin costs R6,000–R8,000/month. Klikk handles the same work at R2,500."
- Free tier removes all risk from the conversation. You cannot lose money trialling a free product.

**What not to say:**
- Do not say "it's cheap" — position it as good value, not cheap.
- Do not apologise for the price.

## Objection 4 — "We've never heard of you"

**What they really mean:** "I'm not sure this company will be around in 12 months. Am I taking a risk?"

**Core response:**

> "We're new and deliberately SA-focused. Klikk was built by a landlord who couldn't find a tool that actually understood South African rental law — the RHA, POPIA, the deposit rules, the way maintenance works here. We're not a US product localised for SA. Early adopters get the most support and the most input into the roadmap. And the free tier means you can try it with zero financial risk — your data is yours and you can export it at any time."

**Supporting points:**
- Data portability: tenant records, leases, and maintenance history can all be exported.
- SA-hosted: data never leaves South African servers.
- The product is live, working, and actively used — this is not vapourware.

**What not to say:**
- Do not over-promise on longevity — you cannot guarantee the future.
- Do not be defensive about being new — being new and focused is a strength at this stage.

## Objection 5 — "WeConnectU (or Prop Data) does everything we need"

**What they really mean:** "We already evaluated the market and chose a platform. Convince me I made the wrong choice."

**Core response:**

> "WeConnectU is solid for large agencies with 200+ units. But for portfolios under 100 units, you're paying enterprise pricing for features you don't use. Klikk offers comparable breadth — lease management, maintenance, owner reporting — at significantly lower cost, with features WeConnectU doesn't have: AI lease generation, a tenant mobile app, an agent mobile app, and a document intelligence tool that can parse your existing lease templates."

**Supporting points:**
- WeConnectU has no AI lease generation. Every lease is manual.
- WeConnectU has no tenant mobile app. Tenants manage everything via email or phone.
- WeConnectU has no agent mobile app. Agents are desk-bound.
- Price comparison: WeConnectU is approximately R150–200/unit/month. Klikk Pro is R2,500/month flat for up to 100 units — better economics at any portfolio under 65 units.

**What not to say:**
- Do not claim WeConnectU is unreliable — it is established. Attack price and missing features, not reliability.
- Do not over-claim on parity for features that are PLANNED (trust accounting, deposit management). Be honest that those are coming.

## Objection 6 — "We're happy with Excel and WhatsApp"

**What they really mean:** "The pain isn't bad enough yet to change, and change has a cost."

**Core response:**

> "That works until it doesn't. A WhatsApp message that gets missed becomes a maintenance complaint. A Word lease that's three years out of date becomes a tribunal hearing. A bank statement you checked once a month becomes an arrears problem you didn't see coming. Klikk doesn't replace your workflow — it organises it. And the free tier means you can run it alongside what you're doing now, with zero disruption, until you're ready to switch fully."

**Supporting points:**
- Most of the cost of the current workflow is invisible: time spent on repetitive tasks, liability from non-compliant leases, lost hours chasing maintenance.
- Buying triggers (see `content/sales/icp.md`): a lease dispute, a maintenance escalation, or a new block of units usually tips the decision.
- Meet them where they are: "Start with just leases. Use Klikk to generate and e-sign your next lease. Keep Excel for everything else for now."

**What not to say:**
- Do not tell them Excel is wrong — they know their own business.
- Do not be condescending about their current tools.

## Objection 7 — "What about trust accounting? We need PPRA-compliant trust accounting."

**What they really mean:** "I need to know if Klikk can replace our current financial workflow before I commit."

**Core response:**

> "Trust accounting is on our roadmap as an optional add-on — it is not yet live in v1. If full PPRA-compliant trust accounting is your primary requirement today, Klikk is not your best option right now — PayProp handles that well and we recommend keeping PayProp for trust and payments. What Klikk does today: lease generation, e-signing, maintenance management, tenant and owner portals, and rent tracking with unique payment references. The trust account module will be added as a paid add-on once it passes PPRA compliance review — we'll notify you when it's live."

**Supporting points:**
- Rent tracking in Klikk today: unique payment references per tenant, receipt generation, arrears flagging. Not a full trust account — be clear about this.
- If they are on PayProp: "Keep PayProp for trust. Use Klikk for everything else. The integration is coming."
- If they need trust accounting today: do not oversell. Honesty here builds credibility.

**What not to say:**
- Do not imply Klikk has PPRA-compliant trust accounting today — it does not.
- Do not promise a timeline for the trust account module.

## Objection 8 — "What about data security and POPIA?"

**What they really mean:** "I'm responsible for my tenants' personal data. I need to know you take that seriously."

**Core response:**

> "All data is hosted in South Africa — it never leaves SA jurisdiction. Encrypted at rest and in transit with 256-bit TLS. Role-based access control means tenants can only see their own data, agents only see their assigned properties, and owners only see their own portfolio. We do not share or sell any data with third parties. JWT authentication with role-based permissions enforced at every API endpoint. POPIA compliance is designed in, not bolted on."

**Supporting points:**
- Free tier data policy: Klikk explicitly does not use or share free tier data with any third party.
- Tenant personal information (ID numbers, banking details) is stored with field-level encryption.
- Data portability: tenants and managers can export their data at any time — POPIA right of access and right to erasure are supported.
- If they are from a registered estate agency: "Your PPRA obligations require you to maintain proper records. Klikk's audit trail — every lease version, every signature, every maintenance ticket — satisfies that requirement."

**What not to say:**
- Do not overclaim on specific certifications (ISO 27001 etc.) unless verified.
- Do not dismiss the concern — POPIA liability is real for property managers.

## Objection 9 — "We need a proper integration with our accounting system (Xero/Sage/Pastel)"

**What they really mean:** "We do not want to do double-entry across systems."

**Core response:**

> "Accounting integrations — Xero, Sage Business Cloud, QuickBooks, and Pastel — are on the roadmap as part of the Trust Account add-on. They are not live yet. What we have today: you can export full transaction records as CSV and PDF for manual import. If seamless accounting write-back is a hard requirement right now, be upfront with your accountant that this will be a manual step until the integration ships."

**Supporting points:**
- The Trust Account add-on (when live) will include automated write-back: rent receipts, disbursements, commissions, VAT-categorised.
- Most small agencies run their accounting monthly — CSV export is workable for now.
- If they are on Xero: "Xero is first in the integration queue. When the Trust Account module launches, Xero write-back will be included."

**What not to say:**
- Do not say the Xero integration is "almost ready" — it is PLANNED, not BUILT.
- Do not promise a timeline.

## Objection 10 — "Can Klikk handle a portfolio of 100+ units?"

**What they really mean:** "Will this product scale with us, or will we outgrow it and have to switch again?"

**Core response:**

> "The Enterprise tier is designed for 100+ unit portfolios. It includes everything in Pro, plus white-labelled portals and tenant app under your agency's brand, on-premises or cloud hosting, POPIA-compliant dedicated data storage, dedicated account manager, and an SLA guarantee. At 100 properties, pricing is R4,000 per month base plus R50 per property above 100. You won't need to switch."

**Supporting points:**
- The platform is multi-tenant by design. Agency principals, agents, owners, tenants, and suppliers all operate in isolated views within the same organisation account.
- White-labelling: T3 agencies can have Klikk under their own brand — tenants see the agency's name, not Klikk's.
- Role-based user add-ons: add as many agents (R90/month each), viewers (R50/month each), or admins (R150/month each) as the team requires.
- REST API and MCP server access on Enterprise: agencies can build custom integrations into their existing systems.

**What not to say:**
- Do not oversell the Enterprise tier's current feature set — trust accounting and accounting integrations are still PLANNED.
- Do not claim on-premises deployment is available today unless confirmed by technical team.
