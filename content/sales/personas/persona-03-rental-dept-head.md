# Persona 03 — Rental Department Head at a Mid-Size Agency

_ICP Tier: T3 — Estate Agency with a Dedicated Rental Department_
_Last updated: 2026-04-22_

---

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name (composite)** | Cindy |
| **Age** | 41 |
| **Role** | Rental Department Head / Senior Property Manager |
| **Agency type** | Independent agency with 22 agents; 6 in the rental department |
| **Portfolio** | 140 residential units under management; 15 new placements/month |
| **Entity** | Agency is a (Pty) Ltd, affiliated with Pam Golding or Seeff franchise |
| **Reports to** | Agency principal / MD |
| **Income** | Salary + performance bonus on department revenue |

---

## Goals

1. Grow the rental book to 200 units by end of year without adding headcount
2. Make the rental department profitable enough that the principal stops treating it as a loss leader
3. Win mandates from landlords who currently use competitors by demonstrating a superior tenant and owner experience
4. Reduce agent overtime — two of her best agents are considering leaving because of admin overload
5. Produce clean owner statements and portfolio reports without manual data assembly

---

## Pains

- **WeConnectU costs ~R21 000/month** for her portfolio (140 units × R150). It goes up every year and she can't justify the cost to the principal at renewal.
- **No mobile app for agents** — agents carry laptops to viewings and fill in forms manually; one agent uses a personal Notion for viewing notes which goes nowhere useful.
- **Lease process bottleneck** — leases go through her for review before sending; she approves 3–5 leases per week and each one takes 20–30 minutes of her time.
- **Tenants call the office, not their agent** — no self-service portal means her team handles every query directly, including basic questions like "what's my reference number?"
- **Owner onboarding is painful** — when a new landlord brings 4 properties, entering them into the current system takes a full day.
- **AI blind spot** — she's heard about AI in property management but has no idea what it actually does. WeConnectU has nothing.

---

## Current Tools

| Tool | Job it does |
|------|------------|
| WeConnectU (or Prop Data) | Core PM platform — lease management, owner statements |
| PayProp | Rent collection and disbursement |
| Microsoft Outlook + Teams | Internal comms and file storage |
| OneDrive / SharePoint | Document storage (inconsistently organised) |
| Excel | Bespoke reports the system can't generate |
| Personal WhatsApp groups | Maintenance coordination (unofficial) |

---

## Objections

| Objection | Root cause |
|-----------|-----------|
| "We're locked into WeConnectU — switching is too risky" | Data migration fear; contract lock-in; change management risk |
| "Can Klikk handle our volume?" | Proof-of-scale concern — address with reference agency + demo at 100+ units |
| "Our agents won't adopt a new tool" | Training resistance; agents are already stretched |
| "We use PayProp, will Klikk replace it?" | No — Klikk complements PayProp. This needs to be explicit upfront. |
| "The principal won't approve the budget" | She needs a ROI calc to take upstairs; provide a savings model (cost vs WeConnectU) |

---

## Decision Criteria

1. Total cost vs current tools — show a like-for-like cost comparison (Klikk vs WeConnectU at 140 units)
2. Can we migrate without disrupting active tenancies?
3. What does agent adoption look like — is there training available?
4. Owner reporting quality — the principal requires owner-facing statements to be polished (agency is a premium brand)
5. PayProp compatibility — this is a hard requirement; any risk to rent collection is a deal-breaker
6. Compliance — RHA and POPIA; the agency has had an audit and is sensitive to this

She does not decide alone. Approval chain: Cindy proposes → principal approves → agency IT (1 person) signs off on integrations. Sales cycle: 4–8 weeks, typically starting in Q1 (budget cycle) or when WeConnectU renewal is due.

---

## Day in the Life

**8:30am** — Team standup. Two agents have viewing clashes. One lease is waiting for a tenant signature for 5 days (chasing manually). A maintenance job from last week has no supplier update.

**10:00am** — A landlord calls asking for a year-to-date statement. Cindy asks her admin to pull it from WeConnectU — it takes 45 minutes because the format needs adjusting in Excel before it can be emailed.

**12:00pm** — New landlord mandate. Three properties. Owner wants digital signing. Cindy's current system doesn't do that — she sends PDF via email, chases for wet signature, or uses a separate DocuSign trial account.

**2:30pm** — An agent is doing a viewing but forgot to print the application form. He takes notes on WhatsApp. Cindy will manually transcribe later.

**4:00pm** — WeConnectU sends the renewal invoice. R22 400 for next quarter. She emails the principal.

**5:30pm** — She's still in the office. Her two agents with the most experience are getting calls from a competitor agency with a signing bonus.

**Insight:** Cindy is not a price buyer — she's a value buyer. She will pay for something that clearly reduces her team's overload and makes the department look better to the principal. The ROI argument is cost vs WeConnectU plus the agent retention angle.

## How to Reach Cindy

**Best channel:** LinkedIn (direct message or InMail), warm referral from a peer agency, or via IEASA events.
**Message hook:** "140 units on WeConnectU at R150/unit. Klikk gives you more features at R39/unit — with an AI lease engine and agent mobile app WeConnectU doesn't have."
**Demo trigger:** Show the WeConnectU cost comparison side-by-side, then run the lease generation demo. Book a team trial.
