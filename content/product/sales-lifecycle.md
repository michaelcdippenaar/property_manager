# Klikk Real Estate — Property Sales Lifecycle

**Selling made simple.**

The complete South African residential property sales journey in 11 steps. Every step references applicable legislation, typical timelines, and regulatory requirements.

**Typical end-to-end timeline:** 8–16 weeks from signed OTP to Deeds Office registration.

---

## Step 1: Prospect

**Find motivated sellers**

Identify sellers through referrals, expired listings, geographic farming (targeting specific neighbourhoods), digital leads (Property24, Facebook ads, agency website), and show day lead capture. Capture seller lead with property details, motivation, and timeline.

FICA verification begins at first contact — per the Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001, estate agents are "accountable institutions" and must verify identity before accepting a mandate.

**Key activities:**
- Geographic area farming (door knocking, local market reports, community events)
- Digital lead generation (portal enquiries, social media, website)
- Referral network cultivation (past clients, attorneys, financial advisors)
- Expired listing and FSBO (For Sale By Owner) outreach
- Initial FICA pre-screening

---

## Step 2: Valuate

**Determine market value**

Conduct a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) using recent comparable sales data (last 6 months, same area). Calculate average price per square metre, adjust for unique features, condition, improvements, and micro-location. Present a formal valuation report with recommended listing price range.

For bank-required valuations, a valuer registered with the South African Council for the Property Valuers Profession (SACPVP) must be appointed. Unregistered persons cannot provide official valuations.

**Key activities:**
- Pull comparable sales data (same suburb, similar size/type, last 6 months)
- Calculate price per m² and adjust for property-specific factors
- Inspect property condition, improvements, and defects
- Present CMA report to seller with pricing recommendation
- Recommend formal SACPVP valuation if required (cost: R2,500–R8,000)

---

## Step 3: Sign Mandate

**Formalise the agency agreement**

Seller and agent sign a mandate agreement defining the terms of engagement. Three mandate types exist in SA:

| Type | Description | Exclusivity |
|---|---|---|
| **Sole Mandate** | One agency, exclusive right to sell for a set period (3–6 months typical) | Full exclusivity |
| **Open Mandate** | Multiple agencies can market simultaneously; whoever sells earns commission | No exclusivity |
| **Dual Mandate** | Sole mandate shared between two agencies | Shared exclusivity |

**Legal requirements:**
- **Property Practitioners Act s67:** Agent may NOT accept a mandate without a completed mandatory property disclosure form from the seller. The form must disclose ALL known defects (roof, electrical, pool, foundations, cracks, seepage, mould, drainage, etc.).
- **Consumer Protection Act s14:** Mandates are limited to a maximum of 24 months and must be for a fixed, specified term. Cancellable with 20 business days' written notice.
- **CPA s14 renewal:** Agent must notify seller 40–80 days before mandate expiry to allow cancellation or renewal.

**Mandate must define:**
- Listing price
- Commission percentage (typically 5–7.5% + 15% VAT)
- Mandate period and type
- Marketing permissions and budget
- Sole/open/dual mandate terms

---

## Step 4: List & Market

**Publish and promote the property**

**Major online portals:**
- Property24 (dominant platform)
- Private Property
- MyProperty, IOL Property

**Marketing channels:**
- Professional photography and virtual tours
- Show days / open houses (typically Sunday afternoons)
- Social media (Facebook, Instagram, targeted ads)
- Signage board on property
- Agent's website and database email campaigns
- Local print media (community newspapers, magazines)
- Video tours and drone footage (higher-value properties)

**Listing must include:**
- Accurate property description with measurements
- Professional photographs
- Disclosure of property condition per mandatory disclosure form
- Correct pricing based on CMA
- Agent's FFC number (Fidelity Fund Certificate — required per PPA)

---

## Step 5: Viewings

**Show the property to qualified buyers**

**Show days:** Traditional in SA, typically Sundays. Agent present on-site during set hours. All visitors register with name and contact details. Show days also generate seller leads from neighbours.

**Private viewings:** Arranged by appointment for serious, pre-qualified buyers. Agent pre-screens for affordability (pre-approval letter or proof of cash) and genuine interest.

**Key activities:**
- Schedule and conduct show days and private viewings
- Register all attendees with full contact details
- Collect buyer feedback and intent after each viewing
- Pre-screen buyers for affordability
- Track lead source for marketing ROI analysis
- Track viewing-to-offer conversion per property

---

## Step 6: Negotiate

**Agree on price and terms**

Buyer submits a written Offer to Purchase (OTP) via the estate agent. The agent is legally obligated to present ALL written offers to the seller. The agent may NOT disclose one buyer's offer details to another buyer.

**Process:**
- Buyer submits written offer
- Agent presents offer to seller (mandatory — agent cannot withhold any written offer)
- Seller accepts, rejects, or counter-offers (all in writing)
- Multiple rounds of negotiation common, mediated by agent
- Negotiate: purchase price, occupation date, deposit amount, suspensive conditions, fixtures included

**Typical negotiation range:** Buyers commonly offer 5–15% below asking price depending on market conditions.

---

## Step 7: Sign OTP

**Execute the Offer to Purchase**

Both parties sign the OTP per the **Alienation of Land Act 68 of 1981 (s2(1))** — the sale of immovable property must be in writing and signed by both parties.

**OTP must contain:**
- Full names, ID numbers, and residential addresses of buyer and seller
- Complete property description (erf/unit number, physical address, exclusive use areas)
- Purchase price and payment method (cash, bond, combination)
- Deposit amount and payment deadline
- Occupation date (specific date or "on registration of transfer")
- Occupational rental amount and terms
- Fixtures and fittings included/excluded
- Compliance certificates required
- Voetstoots clause (sold "as is")
- Breach clause (remedies for non-performance)
- Commission clause (agent name, percentage, when payable)
- VAT inclusion/exclusion and transfer duty responsibility

**Suspensive conditions (with deadlines):**
- Bond approval (typically 21–30 working days)
- Sale of buyer's existing property
- Satisfactory building inspection
- Body corporate or HOA approval (sectional title)

**Mandatory disclosure form** must be attached to the OTP.

If suspensive conditions are not met by deadline, the OTP automatically lapses — no breach, no penalty. Extensions must be agreed in writing.

---

## Step 8: Buyer Documents

**Collect FICA-compliant buyer documentation**

Per the Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001, all parties must be verified.

**Natural persons:**
- Certified copy of SA ID document or passport
- Proof of residential address (utility bill, bank statement, or lease — less than 3 months old)
- SARS tax number / proof of tax registration
- Proof of income (3 months payslips)
- 3 months bank statements
- Marriage certificate / ante-nuptial contract / divorce order (if applicable)
- Source of funds documentation

**Juristic persons (companies/trusts):**
- Company registration documents (CIPC)
- Trust deed
- Resolution authorising the transaction
- Identification of all beneficial owners (25%+ shareholding)
- Identification of all directors/trustees

**Record retention:** All KYC records must be kept for at least 5 years after the transaction completes.

**Suspicious transactions:** Property practitioners must report suspicious or unusual transactions to the Financial Intelligence Centre.

---

## Step 9: Bond Approval

**Secure home loan financing**

**Pre-approval (before OTP):**
- Buyer applies to bank or bond originator (ooba, BetterBond, SA Home Loans, Rawson Finance)
- Bank issues pre-qualification letter indicating approximate affordability
- Valid for 90 days; not a guarantee of formal approval

**Formal application (after OTP signed):**
- Buyer submits formal application with signed OTP attached
- Bank performs NCA (National Credit Act 34 of 2005) affordability assessment
- Rule of thumb: repayments should not exceed ~30% of gross income

**Bank valuation:**
- Bank instructs a SACPVP-registered valuer to inspect the property
- Valuer checks: measurements, structural condition, defects, non-compliant alterations, zoning
- Completed within 3–7 working days
- If valuation is below purchase price, bank lends only up to valuation amount — buyer must fund the shortfall

**Timelines:**
- Application to approval: 5–10 working days (once all documents submitted)
- Bank valuation: 3–7 working days
- Total from OTP to bond approval: 2–4 weeks
- Bond registration (after approval): additional 4–8 weeks via bond attorney

If the bond suspensive condition is not met by the OTP deadline, the offer lapses automatically.

---

## Step 10: Legal & Compliance

**Conveyancing and compliance certificates**

**Conveyancer appointment:**
- The SELLER nominates and appoints the transfer attorney (conveyancer)
- The BUYER typically pays the transfer costs
- The bank appoints a separate bond registration attorney

**Conveyancer's process:**
1. Receives signed OTP from agent
2. Sends introductory letters to buyer and seller
3. Collects FICA documents from both parties
4. Obtains original title deed from seller's bank
5. Requests cancellation figures from seller's existing bondholder
6. Applies for rates clearance certificate from municipality
7. Applies for levy clearance from body corporate/HOA (sectional title)
8. Obtains transfer duty receipt from SARS
9. Prepares transfer documents (deed of transfer, power of attorney)
10. Arranges signing by buyer and seller
11. Requests guarantee from buyer's bank
12. Lodges all documents simultaneously at Deeds Office

**Compliance certificates (seller's responsibility):**

| Certificate | Legislation | Mandatory | Validity | Issued by |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Electrical (ECC) | OHS Act — Electrical Installation Regulations | Yes | 2 years | Registered electrical contractor |
| Gas (GCC) | OHS Act — Pressure Equipment Regulations | If gas installed | 5 years | LPGAS Association installer |
| Electric Fence | OHS Act — Electric Fence Regulations | If fence installed/modified after 1 Oct 2012 | 2 years | Registered electrician |
| Beetle/Wood Borer | No national legislation | If required in OTP | 3–6 months | SA Pest Control Association member |
| Water/Plumbing | Municipal by-laws | Varies by municipality (mandatory in Cape Town) | Until alterations | Registered plumber |

**Rates clearance certificate:**
- Required by law before Deeds Office will register transfer
- Confirms all municipal rates, taxes, and service fees for the preceding 2 years are paid
- Valid for 60 days from date of issue
- Municipality may require advance payment of rates before issuing

**Levy clearance (sectional title):**
- Confirms all body corporate levies and special levies are paid
- Required per STSMA (Sectional Titles Schemes Management Act 8 of 2011)

**Transfer duty (paid by buyer):**

| Property Value (ZAR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| R0 – R1,210,000 | 0% |
| R1,210,001 – R1,663,800 | 3% of value above R1,210,000 |
| R1,663,801 – R2,329,300 | R13,614 + 6% of value above R1,663,800 |
| R2,329,301 – R2,994,800 | R53,544 + 8% of value above R2,329,300 |
| R2,994,801 – R13,310,000 | R106,784 + 11% of value above R2,994,800 |
| R13,310,001+ | R1,241,456 + 13% of value above R13,310,000 |

If the seller is a VAT vendor selling in the course of their enterprise, VAT (15%) applies instead of transfer duty — never both.

---

## Step 11: Transfer

**Deeds Office registration and handover**

**Lodgement:**
All linked matters (transfer, bond registration, bond cancellation) must be lodged simultaneously at the Deeds Office. South Africa has multiple Deeds Offices: Cape Town, Johannesburg, Pretoria, Pietermaritzburg, Bloemfontein, King William's Town, Vryburg, Umtata, Mpumalanga.

**Examination (3 levels of scrutiny):**
1. Junior Examiner — checks basic compliance
2. Senior Examiner — verifies complex details
3. Assistant Registrar / Registrar — final approval

Timeline: 7–10 working days from lodgement to registration. If documents are rejected ("queried"), the conveyancer must correct and re-lodge, restarting the examination period.

**On registration day:**
- Property officially transfers to buyer's name
- Conveyancer distributes funds from trust account:
  - Purchase price to seller (less outstanding bond, agent commission, costs)
  - Commission to estate agent (must hold valid FFC on date OTP was signed)
  - Rates/levies to municipality/body corporate
  - Cancellation figures to seller's bondholder
- Keys and occupation handed to buyer on agreed occupation date

**Occupation vs registration:**
- Registration date: when the Deeds Office registers ownership transfer (unpredictable exact date)
- Occupation date: when buyer physically takes possession (negotiated in OTP)
- If buyer occupies before registration: buyer pays occupational rental to seller
- If seller remains after registration: seller pays occupational rental to buyer
- Typical occupational rental: ~1% of purchase price per month

## Key Legislation Reference

| Act | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Property Practitioners Act 22 of 2019 | Regulates all property practitioners (replaced EAAB Act) |
| Alienation of Land Act 68 of 1981 | Property sales must be in writing, signed by both parties |
| Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 | Cooling-off rights, fixed-term mandates, plain language |
| National Credit Act 34 of 2005 | Bond affordability assessments, responsible lending |
| Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001 | Anti-money laundering, KYC requirements |
| Deeds Registries Act 47 of 1937 | Registration of property transfers |
| Transfer Duty Act 40 of 1949 | Transfer duty rates and payment |
| Sectional Titles Schemes Management Act 8 of 2011 | Body corporate governance, levy clearance |
| Occupational Health and Safety Act | Compliance certificates (electrical, gas, electric fence) |

## Agent Commission

- Not regulated — entirely negotiable
- Typical range: 5–7.5% of sale price + 15% VAT
- Earned on acceptance of OTP and fulfilment of suspensive conditions
- Disbursed by conveyancer from transfer proceeds after Deeds Office registration
- Agent must hold a valid Fidelity Fund Certificate (FFC) on the date the OTP was signed
- If co-broke (different agent brings buyer), commission split is agreed between agencies (commonly 50/50)
- Seller pays commission; it is deducted from sale proceeds
