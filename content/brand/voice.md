# Klikk Brand Voice

## Tone

**Professional but approachable.** We're building serious software for real business problems, but we don't take ourselves too seriously. Think "smart colleague who knows their stuff" — not "corporate consultant" or "startup bro."

## Principles

1. **South African first.** Use SA terminology (not US/UK). Say "deposit" not "security deposit." Say "lease" not "rental agreement" in casual contexts. Reference the RHA, POPIA, and local platforms (Property24, PayProp) by name.

2. **Honest about status.** If a feature is coming soon, say "Coming Soon." Never imply something works if it doesn't. Our 15-stage lifecycle is ambitious — being transparent about what's built vs planned builds trust.

3. **Show, don't tell.** Lead with what the product does, not buzzwords. "Generate an RHA-compliant lease in 3 minutes" beats "AI-powered lease automation solution."

4. **Respect the reader's time.** Short sentences. Clear headlines. No filler paragraphs. Property managers are busy — get to the point.

5. **Technical confidence without jargon.** We can mention Claude AI, WebSockets, and Flutter — our audience includes tech-savvy property managers. But never let tech language replace a clear explanation of the benefit.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "AI generates your lease" | "Leveraging artificial intelligence for lease optimization" |
| "Built for the Rental Housing Act" | "Compliance-first approach to regulatory frameworks" |
| "Free for up to 5 units" | "Freemium tier with limited feature access" |
| "Sign from any device" | "Omnichannel digital signature solution" |
| "4 apps, one platform" | "Unified ecosystem synergy across stakeholders" |

## Keywords to Use

- Property management, rental management
- South Africa, SA, Rental Housing Act, RHA, POPIA
- AI-powered, intelligent, automated
- Lease, tenant, landlord, agent, supplier
- Free, no credit card, start today

## Keywords to Avoid

- Leverage, synergy, disrupt, revolutionize
- Solution, platform (use sparingly), ecosystem (ok in app context)
- Cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, next-generation
- Any American English spelling (favor → favour, organize → organise)
