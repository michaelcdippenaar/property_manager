## Messaging Pillars

These five pillars distil the value-prop ladder into the themes that flow into website copy, ads, email, and sales conversations.

### Pillar 1 — AI That Actually Does the Work

AI in Klikk is not a chatbot bolted onto a dashboard. It drafts your lease from a conversation, parses a PDF lease you upload, classifies a maintenance request, matches the right supplier, and answers tenant questions in real time. Claude AI is the engine inside every workflow — not a premium add-on.

**Hero line:** "AI drafts the lease. You review it. Done."

**Target audience resonance:** Boutique PM firms (T1) and self-managing landlords (T2) who spend hours on lease drafting and maintenance co-ordination.

---

### Pillar 2 — Built for South African Rental Law

Every clause, every workflow, and every compliance prompt is written for the Rental Housing Act and POPIA — not adapted from a UK or US product. Unique payment references, RHA-aligned deposit rules, and POPIA-aware tenant data handling are defaults, not configuration options.

**Hero line:** "Built for the RHA. Not translated from somewhere else."

**Target audience resonance:** All three ICP tiers. Particularly strong for T1 and T3 where compliance liability is a buying trigger.

---

### Pillar 3 — Four Apps, One Platform

Every participant in a tenancy gets a tailored experience:
- Agents manage their full portfolio and pipeline from the admin dashboard and mobile app.
- Tenants report issues, sign documents, and chat via their mobile app.
- Owners see real-time occupancy and income without calling the agent.
- Suppliers receive jobs, submit quotes, and manage their calendar without a login.

No app in this set competes with another. Together they eliminate the WhatsApp/Excel/email patchwork that most SA rental operations run on today.

**Hero line:** "4 apps. One platform. No WhatsApp group required."

**Target audience resonance:** T1 boutique PM firms who are the co-ordination hub between all four parties. Also strong for T3 agencies looking to modernise tenant and owner experience.

---

### Pillar 4 — The Price Makes Sense for Small Portfolios

WeConnectU charges R150–200/unit. A 100-unit portfolio on WeConnectU costs R15,000–R20,000/month. The same portfolio on Klikk Pro is R2,500/month flat — the limit is 50 properties or 100 units, whichever is reached first. Firms managing more than 100 units move to Enterprise at R4,000/month base. The free tier covers up to 5 properties with no credit card required.

**Hero line:** "Free for your first 5 properties. R2,500/month after that — no matter how the units add up."

**Target audience resonance:** T1 and T2 where price is the primary friction. Less important for T3 where business-case ROI language matters more.

---

### Pillar 5 — Built by a Landlord, Not a Software House

The founder manages their own properties. Every feature exists because it solved a real problem. There are no phantom roadmap items to impress investors — just the 15-stage rental lifecycle, built out feature by feature, shipped and used in production on real tenancies.

**Hero line:** "Every feature exists because the founder needed it."

**Target audience resonance:** T2 self-managing landlords (direct empathy). Also useful in T1 sales conversations to establish credibility and counter the "never heard of you" objection.
