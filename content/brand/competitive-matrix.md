## Where Klikk Leads (Differentiated — BUILT features only)

These are Klikk's genuine, shipped points of differentiation. All claims substantiated in `features.yaml`.

### AI-Native Across the Stack

No competitor in the SA rental market has shipped AI into core workflows. WeConnectU and Prop Data are legacy platforms with no AI features. PayProp is a payment engine. Klikk is the only product where AI drafts the lease, parses uploaded contracts, triages maintenance, matches suppliers, and answers tenant questions in real time.

**Axes where Klikk is sole provider:** 1, 5, 6, 7 (entire row is Klikk vs N across all competitors).

---

### Native E-Signing (No Subscription Required)

WeConnectU and Prop Data route e-signing through a DocuSign add-on — additional cost, additional login, additional friction. Klikk includes e-signing natively. Tenants sign via a TLS-encrypted link from any device; no account required.

**Axis 2:** Klikk Y vs all others N or Partial.

---

### Mobile-First for Agents and Tenants

No direct competitor has shipped a dedicated mobile app for both agents and tenants.
- The agent app covers property browsing, viewing scheduling, and prospect capture.
- The tenant app covers issue reporting, real-time chat, document signing, and lease viewing.

HouseME has a tenant-facing web interface but not a native app. PayProp's mobile offering is limited to payment status.

**Axes 3 and 4:** Klikk Y vs all others N or Partial.

---

### Price-to-Capability Ratio

At R2,500/month flat (Pro, up to 100 units), Klikk delivers AI lease generation, e-signing, a tenant app, an agent app, and a full maintenance workflow. That works out to R25/unit at full 100-unit capacity — a derived figure, stated transparently. WeConnectU charges R150–200/unit for a product with none of the AI or mobile capabilities. Klikk's free tier (up to 5 properties) has no real equivalent among the established players.

**Axis 19:** Klikk's flat-rate model is materially cheaper than WeConnectU and Prop Data at any portfolio size above approximately 17 units (where WeConnectU at R150/unit exceeds Klikk Pro's R2,500/month). The advantage widens as portfolio size grows.

## Where Klikk Gaps (Honest Assessment)

These gaps are real and must be acknowledged in sales conversations. Hiding them erodes trust.

### Trust Accounting / Deposit Management (Planned)

PayProp owns this space in SA. WeConnectU and Prop Data also offer trust accounting. Klikk does not have trust accounting in v1 — deposit management is on the roadmap (PLANNED). The correct positioning is: "Klikk complements PayProp — it manages everything before and after the payment. For trust accounting, use PayProp alongside Klikk."

**Risk:** Any ICP prospect whose primary pain is trust accounting is a poor fit for v1. Qualify out early.

---

### Tenant Screening via TPN (Planned)

TPN is the dominant SA tenant screening provider. Klikk's direct TPN integration is PLANNED. In the interim, agents run TPN checks manually and upload reports to the lease file. The correct sales response: "TPN integration is on the roadmap. For now, you do the TPN check in their portal and upload the report directly to the Klikk lease — it takes 2 minutes."

---

### Vacancy Advertising / Multi-Portal Listing (Planned)

Property24 and Private Property syndication is PLANNED. WeConnectU and Prop Data offer this. Until Klikk ships it, agents list vacancies on portals manually and use Klikk's viewing scheduler to manage inbound leads.

---

### Inspections (Incoming and Outgoing) (Planned)

Photo-documented incoming and outgoing inspections are PLANNED. This is a meaningful gap for agencies where deposit disputes are common. Until this ships, agents use manual inspection forms alongside Klikk. Do not compare Klikk on inspection capability until BUILT.

## Competitor-Specific Positioning Responses

These are the most common competitive scenarios in a sales conversation and how to navigate them.

### "We use PayProp"

PayProp does one thing well: rent collection and trust accounting. Klikk does everything else — AI lease generation, maintenance management, tenant app, owner reporting, e-signing. Klikk is designed to sit alongside PayProp: agents stay on PayProp for payments; Klikk handles the full tenancy lifecycle before and after the money moves.

**When to use:** Very common objection. Reframe as complementary, not competitive.

---

### "We're on WeConnectU"

WeConnectU is a comprehensive platform, but it was built before AI and mobile became viable. It has no AI lease generation, no tenant mobile app, and no agent mobile app. It costs R150–200/unit — a 100-unit portfolio runs R15,000–R20,000/month. Klikk Pro covers the same 100 units for R2,500/month flat, and includes all three AI and mobile capabilities. If the WeConnectU contract is up, the business case for switching is direct.

**When to use:** T3 rental department agencies where WeConnectU is the incumbent.

---

### "We use Prop Data / MRI"

Prop Data is an enterprise global product — complex to set up, expensive, and built for large agencies with dedicated operations staff. Klikk is built for SA-specific law and can be set up in a day. For an agency with 50–200 units, Klikk delivers comparable operational coverage with AI advantages and a fraction of the cost.

**When to use:** T3 agencies where Prop Data is the incumbent.

---

### "We've never heard of you"

Klikk is new and SA-focused, built by a landlord who needed it. That means every feature has been used on real tenancies before being shipped. Early adopters get direct access to the founding team, faster feature turnaround, and the opportunity to shape the product. Being early has real advantages.

**When to use:** All tiers. Particularly T1 and T2 where trust is built on personal connection.

---

### "WeConnectU does everything. Why change?"

At what cost, and with what tools? WeConnectU has no AI, no tenant mobile app, and charges R150–200/unit — a 100-unit portfolio costs R15,000–R20,000/month vs R2,500/month on Klikk Pro. The gap between Klikk and WeConnectU on AI and mobile is growing, not shrinking. Agencies that switch now build an AI-native operational model before competitors do.

**When to use:** T1 and T3 where WeConnectU is an active consideration.

---

### "TPN does our tenant screening"

TPN is a screening bureau, not a management platform. It does one step in a 15-step tenancy lifecycle. Klikk manages the other 14. Klikk's TPN integration is on the roadmap so that screening becomes one more workflow inside a single platform rather than a separate tool and manual upload.

**When to use:** When TPN is mentioned as a reason not to switch — it is not a competing product.
