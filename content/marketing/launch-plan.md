# Klikk Rentals v1 — Launch Campaign Plan

_Last updated: 2026-04-22. All feature claims verified against `content/product/features.yaml`. No PLANNED features referenced as current capabilities._

_Dependencies: This plan assumes GTM-003 (homepage live) and GTM-004 (sales assets ready) are done before Week 1 activities begin. Any delay to those tasks shifts the timeline; the relative ordering of activities does not change._

---

## Context and Constraints

**Team reality:** This plan is written for a small, founder-led team (1–3 people executing). Every activity listed is achievable without a dedicated marketing hire. Where external support is noted, it is optional and budget-gated.

**Launch definition:** "Launch" is the date Klikk Rentals v1 is publicly available for sign-up and the homepage is live. It is not a single event — it is a 9-week campaign window (4 pre-launch + launch week + 4 post-launch).

**Primary ICP priority for launch:** Tier 1 (boutique rental agencies, 20–80 units) is the highest-value early adopter. Tier 2 (self-managing landlords, 1–10 units) is the free-tier top-of-funnel. Tier 3 (estate agencies with a rental department) requires a longer sales cycle — initial outreach only in this plan, conversions expected in weeks 5–12.

## Channels

### Direct Outreach (Primary)

The highest-converting channel at launch. The founder has existing relationships in the Stellenbosch/Winelands property market. All outreach is personal — no bulk templates sent cold to strangers.

**Tactics:**
- 1:1 WhatsApp and email messages to known landlords and rental agents (warm network)
- Personalised demo invitations to 20–30 target Tier 1 agencies identified from PPRA registrations and Property24 agency listings in Cape Town, Johannesburg, and Stellenbosch
- Follow-up sequence for non-responders: one message at Day 3, one at Day 10

**Assets required:** sales one-pager (GTM-004), demo booking link (Calendly or equivalent), short video demo (screen recording, 3–5 minutes)

---

### LinkedIn

SA property professionals are active on LinkedIn. Founders with a genuine story perform well here. This channel builds credibility more than it drives direct sign-ups in the first 8 weeks.

**Profile:** Founder's personal LinkedIn account (not a company page initially — company pages without followers get no reach).

**Tactics:**
- Founder posts 2–3 times per week during the campaign window
- Post formats that work: "we built this because…" stories, short demos (native video), feature walkthrough screenshots, questions to SA landlords/agents ("what's your current process for X?")
- Engage actively in SA property and PropTech discussions — comment with substance, not promotion
- 1:1 connection requests to principals at target T1 and T3 agencies after any post engagement

**Who to target:** rental agency principals, property managers, estate agent principals, landlord investors — search by title + SA location

---

### Industry Groups and Professional Bodies

Slower to convert but high credibility. Being visible in the right communities before and after launch builds the authority that converts sceptics.

**SA-specific groups and channels:**

| Group / Body | Audience | Tactic |
|---|---|---|
| EAAB (Estate Agency Affairs Board) — licensed agents | T1 and T3 agents with PPRA registration | Sponsorship of newsletter or webinar (contact: eaab.org.za) |
| SAPOA (SA Property Owners Association) | Commercial-heavy but some residential members | Op-ed pitch: "AI and the future of residential rental management" |
| Rental Housing Tribunal stakeholder networks | T1 and T2 | Educational content framed around RHA compliance |
| RLA (Residential Landlords Association SA) | T2 self-managing landlords | Announcement post, offer free tier, offer to answer compliance questions |
| TPN Tenant Profile Network | T1 and T3 | Not a competitor — introduce Klikk as complementary; explore co-marketing |
| PayProp partner ecosystem | T1 and T3 | Position Klikk as the missing workflow layer around PayProp |

---

### Facebook Groups for SA Landlords

Where self-managing landlords actually spend time. Volume is lower than LinkedIn but intent is high — people post real pain points that are exactly what Klikk solves.

**Target groups:**
- "SA Landlords" (largest SA private landlord FB group, ~25,000+ members)
- "Cape Town Landlords and Property Investors"
- "Johannesburg Rental Properties and Landlords"
- "Winelands Property Investors"
- "South African Property Investment" (broader, some landlords)

**Tactic:** Join as a participant first. Answer questions about RHA compliance, deposit disputes, and lease drafting with genuinely helpful responses — mention Klikk only when directly relevant and only after establishing credibility. Do not post promotional content as opening messages. The goal is inbound interest, not banner-ad style promotion.

**Content type that lands:** "Here's how I handle [specific problem] in practice" posts with screenshots of the product solving it.

---

### PR and Media

One targeted push during launch week. Not a sustained channel in the first 8 weeks. The SA PropTech and property media landscape is small and relationship-driven.

**Targets:**

| Publication / Platform | Audience | Pitch angle |
|---|---|---|
| Property Professional magazine (PPRA-linked) | Registered estate agents | "First SA-built rental platform with AI lease generation — built by a landlord" |
| Business Insider SA (property/tech section) | Broad tech-interested SA business audience | PropTech launch story — AI, SA rental law, founder backstory |
| Property360 (IOL property) | Property investors and agents | Feature: "How AI is changing how SA landlords manage leases" |
| TechCentral (SA tech media) | Tech-adopting business professionals | Short news item: "Klikk launches AI-native rental management for SA market" |
| MyBroadband property/tech threads | Tech-active SA professionals | Founder AMA (Ask Me Anything) thread — genuine community format |

**Timing:** PR outreach goes out 5 days before launch day so stories can publish on or around launch day.

---

### Email Sequences

Three separate sequences, each targeting a different ICP. Sent from the founder's personal email address (or a simple `mc@klikk.co.za` address) — not a mass-mail tool for the first 8 weeks. Volume is low enough that personalised sends outperform templated blasts.

See the content calendar section below for timing. Sequence content is produced as a separate deliverable (GTM-006, if scoped).

---

### Webinar

One live online session during or just after launch week. SA property professionals respond well to live product walkthroughs — it is a lower-commitment entry point than a 1:1 demo.

**Format:** 45-minute Zoom or Google Meet session — 20 minutes product demo, 25 minutes Q&A. Record and share as a follow-up asset.

**Topic:** "AI Lease Generation and the RHA — a live demo for SA rental agents and landlords"

**Promotion channels:** LinkedIn event, Facebook group posts, email to warm contacts, direct personal invitations to T1 targets.

**Target attendance:** 15–30 registrants is a good result for a first webinar with no existing audience.

| Weekly | Personal follow-up on any open demo or trial conversation — one touchpoint, not a chase sequence |

## Email Sequences

Three sequences, described at outline level. Full copy produced separately (GTM-006 if scoped).

### Sequence A — Warm Network (Direct Outreach)

**Audience:** Founder's existing contacts — agents and landlords who already know the founder personally.
**Volume:** 20–30 contacts.
**Tone:** Personal. Written as individual messages, not templates.

| Step | Timing | Message summary |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Week -2 | "I've been building Klikk for the last [X] months — it's live. Here's what it does, and I'd love your honest reaction. Book a 20-minute demo with me directly." |
| Email 2 | Week -2 + 4 days | If no reply: "I know you're busy — just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. One link, no pressure." |
| Email 3 | Launch week | "Klikk is live today. Free tier is up to 5 properties with no credit card. Thought of you specifically because [personalised reason]." |

---

### Sequence B — Webinar Registrants

**Audience:** Anyone who registered for the launch webinar.
**Volume:** 15–50 registrants (target).
**Tone:** Helpful, follow-through. Not a sales chase.

| Step | Timing | Message summary |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmation | On registration | "You're registered for [date/time]. Here's what we'll cover and a quick look at what Klikk does." |
| Reminder | 24 hours before | "Tomorrow at [time]. You can add it to your calendar here: [link]. See you there." |
| Reminder | 2 hours before | "Starting in 2 hours. Join link: [link]." |
| Replay | Day after webinar | "Here's the recording. The most useful bit is [timestamp] where we show the lease being generated. If you want a 1:1 run-through, book here: [link]." |
| Follow-up | Day +7 | "Any questions from the webinar? We've been talking to a few agents about [common question from Q&A] — happy to cover that in a quick call." |

---

### Sequence C — Free Sign-Up Onboarding

**Audience:** Anyone who creates a free Klikk account.
**Volume:** All free sign-ups.
**Tone:** Practical. Get them to their first value moment (AI lease generated) within 48 hours.

| Step | Timing | Message summary |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome | Immediately on sign-up | "Welcome to Klikk. You're on the free tier — up to 5 properties. The fastest way to see what Klikk does: generate your first lease in [link to AI lease builder]." |
| Day 2 check-in | 48 hours after sign-up | "Have you tried the lease builder yet? It takes 3 minutes. Here's a short video showing exactly how it works: [demo video link]." |
| Day 5 prompt | 5 days after sign-up | "If you've added a property or generated a lease, you've seen how it works. If you've got questions, reply to this email — I read everything." |
| Day 10 upgrade nudge | 10 days after sign-up | "You're on the free tier (up to 5 properties). If you manage more, or want unlimited AI leases and the full maintenance workflow, Pro is R2,500/month — no per-unit charges. [Compare plans link]." |

## KPIs

These are the leading and lagging indicators that tell you whether the campaign is working. Review weekly.

### Leading Indicators (Weekly)

| KPI | Target (8-week total) | How to measure |
|---|---|---|
| Demo booking rate from direct outreach | 30% of 20–30 contacts book a demo | Calendly bookings vs outreach sends |
| Webinar registrations | 20+ for launch week session | Webinar registration list |
| LinkedIn post impressions | 1,000+ cumulative by end of Week 2 | LinkedIn analytics |
| LinkedIn post engagement rate | >3% per post (SA PropTech average is ~1–2%) | LinkedIn analytics |
| Facebook group engagement | 3+ substantive replies per post | Manual count |
| Free sign-ups (first 4 weeks post-launch) | 15–30 accounts | Backend registration data |

---

### Lagging Indicators (Campaign Outcome)

| KPI | Target (8-week window) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Demo sessions completed | 10+ | Quality over quantity — T1 prospects preferred |
| Trial activations (account created + property added) | 20+ | "Activated" = added at least 1 property |
| First paid conversions (Pro, R2,500/month) | 2–5 paying customers | 1 T1 agency = meaningful |
| Pipeline for T3 (agencies requiring longer cycle) | 3–5 qualified T3 conversations open | Conversions expected in Weeks 6–12, not W1–8 |
| Net Promoter feedback from first users | 1–2 testimonials or written quotes | Feed into website and LinkedIn |

---

### Conversion Benchmarks (for context)

| Funnel stage | Assumed rate | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Outreach → demo booking | 20–30% | Based on warm network (known contacts) |
| Demo booking → trial sign-up | 60–70% | Demo removes friction; free tier reduces risk |
| Trial sign-up → paid conversion | 10–20% | Conservative for first 8 weeks; improves as social proof builds |
| Webinar registrant → demo booked | 15–25% | Post-webinar follow-up is the key lever |

## Budget Structure

The plan is designed to operate near R0 in external spend for the first 8 weeks. Optional paid line items are flagged below with indicative ranges. The founder sets actual numbers based on available cash.

| Line item | Type | Indicative range | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| Calendly (demo booking) | Tool | Free (basic) or R80/month (paid) | W-4 onwards |
| Webinar platform (Zoom/Meet) | Tool | Free (Meet) or ~R200/month (Zoom Pro) | Launch week |
| LinkedIn Sponsored Content (optional) | Paid media | R2,000–R8,000 per campaign; start small to test | W1–W4 if organic is not working |
| Facebook Group or Meta Ads (optional) | Paid media | R1,500–R5,000 for targeted SA landlord demographics | W2–W4 |
| PR distribution (optional) | Service | R0 (direct pitch) to R3,000–R6,000 for a single press release via a PR service | Launch week |
| EAAB or SAPOA newsletter sponsorship | Sponsorship | R3,000–R8,000 per placement (estimate; get quotes) | W3–W4 |
| Blog/SEO tool (e.g. Ahrefs/Semrush) | Tool | R600–R1,200/month | W2 onwards (optional) |
| Short-form video production (optional) | Service | R2,000–R5,000 for a professionally edited 60-second spot | W2–W4 |

**Total optional spend range for 8 weeks:** R0 (zero paid activation, organic only) to ~R30,000–R45,000 (most optional items activated).

**Recommended approach:** Start with zero paid spend. If organic outreach generates 5+ demo bookings in the first 2 weeks, the product-market fit signal is strong enough to invest in paid. If not, diagnose the conversion problem before spending on awareness.

## What This Plan Does Not Cover

These items are out of scope for this plan. They are either too early for v1 launch or require a separate brief.

- **Paid SEO campaign** — needs 3–6 months runway; not a launch-week lever
- **Affiliate or referral programme** — requires a customer base first; scope in Month 3+
- **Content syndication to Property24 editorial** — longer relationship-building cycle
- **Social media account for Klikk** (separate from founder's LinkedIn) — low ROI at zero-follower base; build founder brand first
- **PLANNED features in any campaign asset** — tenant screening, multi-portal advertising, inspections, deposit management are all PLANNED and must not be referenced as available
