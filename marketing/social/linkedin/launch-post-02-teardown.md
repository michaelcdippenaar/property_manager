## Post copy

I audited 14 residential leases from landlords around Stellenbosch and Cape Town this month.

12 of them were missing clauses required by the Rental Housing Act.

The top 5 mistakes:

**1. No interest on the deposit**
The RHA requires deposits to be held in an interest-bearing account, with interest paid to the tenant on refund. If your lease is silent, you still owe it.

**2. No incoming inspection clause**
Section 5(3) requires a joint inspection before move-in, with a written record. Skip it and you have no baseline for deductions later.

**3. Blank escalation**
"To be agreed" is unenforceable. Specify the % now (8–10% is standard in SA).

**4. "Landlord can terminate at will" clauses**
You can't. PIE Act overrides any lease clause — you need a court order to evict.

**5. No POPIA notice**
If you hold the tenant's ID, payslips, or bank details, you need a lawful basis and a short privacy notice. Missing = compliance risk.
