## Segment rules

1. **Every asset names its segment.** No "property management software" — always "for self-managing landlords" or "for small agencies scaling past 50 units".
2. **Each segment has its own mini-funnel:** own lead magnet, own landing page, own case study (once a beta exists in that lane).
3. **Beta cohort balance:** 1–2 per segment across T1–T3. T4 is warm-intro only. T5 runs on parallel track (PBSA 30-day experiment).
4. **Pricing exception:** T1 free tier stays free for life for first 5 beta users. Their feedback buys their access.
5. **Never skip segments for ICP breadth.** Converting 5 T1 users beats 1 user from each tier.

## When to revisit

- Add a new segment only after converting 3+ paying customers in an existing segment
- Retire a segment if 30-day experiment shows <2% response rate with a cost-effective channel
- Split a segment if two sub-profiles diverge significantly (e.g. T3 "boutique 20-unit" vs T3 "growth-stage 80-unit" may need separate messaging at scale)

## Stage 0 priority (weeks 1–4)

T1 (self-managing landlord) is the **fastest path** because:
- Michael IS one (R120M portfolio, 10 years, 6 units) → unimpeachable credibility
- His peer network maps directly onto this segment
- Product is closest to shipping for their use case
- Conversion cycle shortest (they sign themselves, no board approval)

T2–T4 identification runs in parallel but first 5 paying users should all be T1.
