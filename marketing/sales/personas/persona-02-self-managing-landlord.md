---

## Goals

1. Keep her properties occupied and rent coming in without it consuming evenings and weekends
2. Avoid legal exposure from bad leases or missed compliance steps
3. Build a clean paper trail so she can sell a property without digging through WhatsApp threads
4. Eventually grow to 5–6 units and retire early — but only if the admin doesn't scale linearly

---

## Pains

- **Downloaded a lease template online** in 2021. She has no idea if it's still compliant with the latest RHA amendments. A tenant's friend told him it wasn't.
- **Rent tracking is mental + manual** — she has a standing payment from two tenants and has to check internet banking every month to confirm the third paid.
- **No maintenance record** — when the outgoing tenant damaged the oven, she had no inspection report and couldn't claim the deposit. She lost R6 000.
- **Communication is via her personal WhatsApp** — she worries about POPIA but doesn't know what to do about it.
- **Deposit handling is guesswork** — she holds deposits in her personal account, not an interest-bearing account as required by the RHA. She doesn't know she's non-compliant.
- **Discovery risk** — SARS estate agent audit, a dispute at the Rental Housing Tribunal, or a POPIA complaint would expose all of the above.

## Objections

| Objection | Root cause |
|-----------|-----------|
| "I only have 3 properties, it's probably overkill" | Doesn't see herself as a "business" — needs reframing |
| "I can't afford extra monthly costs" | Risk aversion; hasn't calculated what non-compliance costs |
| "I don't really understand software" | Adoption fear — UX must be frictionless |
| "I already have leases in place" | Existing tenants are mid-lease; upload & import must feel easy |
| "What if my tenant sees my data?" | POPIA concern — address with POPIA-first messaging |

---

## Decision Criteria

1. Is there a free tier or very low cost to start? (R0 for 3 units would remove the barrier entirely)
2. Is it easy to set up without IT skills?
3. Will it make my leases legally compliant?
4. Can I keep my existing tenants in the system?
5. Will it protect me from a Rental Housing Tribunal dispute?

She decides alone, but often consults a friend who is also a landlord or has asked a property attorney before. Sales cycle is short — if the free tier is available and setup is under 30 minutes, she signs up herself without a demo.

---

## Day in the Life

**First Monday of the month** — Zanele checks internet banking before her 9am meeting. Two of three rents are in. She sends a WhatsApp to the third tenant. He pays on Wednesday.

**Random Tuesday** — Tenant 2 messages: "The geyser is making a noise." Zanele forwards to a plumber she knows. Waits. Follows up Thursday. Job done Friday, but no invoice, no record.

**End of tenancy (3 years in)** — Tenant 1 moves out. No inspection report from move-in. Tenant disputes the claim on the deposit. The lease is ambiguous. Zanele settles for R3 000 less than she's owed because she can't prove the damage predates the tenancy.

**When she finally searches Google** — "RHA compliant lease South Africa" or "how to manage rental property South Africa" — this is the acquisition moment. Klikk must appear here.

**Insight:** Zanele doesn't know what she doesn't know. She's not looking for "property management software" — she's looking for peace of mind and protection from risk. The value proposition is risk reduction first, time saving second.
