---

## Goals

1. Grow the portfolio to 80–100 units without hiring another full-time administrator
2. Win new landlord mandates by demonstrating a more professional process than competitors
3. Reduce the time he personally spends on lease creation, maintenance calls, and tenant queries
4. Build a recurring revenue base that is less dependent on new placements
5. Give property owners real-time visibility so they stop phoning him for updates

---

## Pains

- **Lease creation takes 2–3 hours** per new tenant — copy-paste from a Word template, manually fill in 40+ fields, print, scan, and chase for signatures
- **Maintenance is managed on WhatsApp** — no record, no tracking, suppliers disappear, owners ask "what happened with the geyser?"
- **Lease disputes** — he's had two tenants challenge clauses because the template was from 2018 and not RHA-compliant
- **Owner reporting is ad-hoc** — month-end statements are manual; owners with multiple properties ask the same questions repeatedly
- **Tenant calls at 9pm** — tenants have no self-service option and call him directly for things like reading the lease or reporting a dripping tap
- **Scaling fear** — he knows the next 20 units will break the current process, but he can't afford to hire before he has the income

| "I use PayProp, I don't want to change that" | Misunderstanding — Klikk complements PayProp, doesn't replace it |
| "R2,500/month sounds expensive" | Reframe: 45 units = R55/unit/month flat. Less than one hour of his time saved per week. WeConnectU charges R150–200/unit — Klikk is a third of the cost. |

---

## Decision Criteria

1. Does it actually reduce admin? (Demo lease generation end-to-end)
2. Is it SA-compliant? (RHA, POPIA — he's been bitten before)
3. What does the tenant experience look like? (He uses this to pitch landlords)
4. Can he migrate his existing portfolio without starting from scratch?
5. What does it cost, and is there a trial period?

He decides alone — no committee. Typical sales cycle: 1–2 weeks from first demo to sign-up if the product lands.

## Day in the Life

**7:30am** — Wakes up to 4 WhatsApp messages: one tenant reporting a leak, one asking when their lease expires, one owner asking for a payment statement, and a supplier saying they can't make Tuesday.

**8:00am** — Responds to all four manually. Forwards the maintenance job to a different supplier via WhatsApp.

**9:00am** — New tenant application arrives by email — PDF scan of ID and payslip, plus a form filled in on paper. He re-types details into his Word lease template.

**11:30am** — Lease is done. Emails it as a PDF. Waits. Sends a reminder at 3pm. Tenant signs a printed copy and scans it back on day 3.

**2:00pm** — Owner calls about a property he manages. "Has the rent come in?" Ruan checks his spreadsheet, confirms, sends an ad-hoc email update.

**5:30pm** — Month-end approaching. He needs to reconcile 45 payment references by hand and generate 12 owner statements. Blocks out Saturday morning.

**Insight:** Every step in Ruan's day is something Klikk automates. He doesn't need to be convinced there's a problem — he lives it. He needs proof the solution is fast to set up and won't create new problems.
