## How to Read This Document

Three ICP tiers are defined below, ordered by fit and revenue priority. Each tier includes firmographics, portfolio profile, geographic concentration, tech readiness, buying triggers, disqualifiers, and a rough segment size estimate for the South African market.

A persona sheet in `content/sales/personas/` maps to each tier.

## Tier 1 — Boutique Rental Agency / Small Property Management Company

### Firmographics

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| Entity type | CC, (Pty) Ltd, or sole proprietor registered as estate agent |
| Staff | 1–10 people (1–3 dedicated rental staff) |
| Portfolio size | 20–80 residential units under management |
| Property types | Apartments, freehold homes, sectional title units |
| Locations managed | Concentrated in one metro or suburb cluster |
| Operating model | Managing agent for multiple private landlords |
| Revenue model | Management fee (7–10% of monthly rent) + placement fee (0.5–1 month's rent) |

### Geographic Concentration (SA)

Primary markets: Greater Cape Town (Atlantic Seaboard, Southern Suburbs, Winelands), Johannesburg (Sandton, Rosebank, northern suburbs), Pretoria (Menlyn, Waterkloof), Durban (Umhlanga, Ballito).

Rationale: These nodes have the highest density of private residential rentals, highest average rental values, and highest concentration of tech-adopting agents under 40.

Estimated addressable count (SA): ~3 500–5 000 boutique rental agencies and independent PM firms. Source: PPRA registrations (Property Practitioners Regulatory Authority) — approximately 40 000 registered practitioners; roughly 10–12% operate dedicated rental management businesses at this scale.

### Tech Readiness

- Currently uses 2–5 disconnected tools: WhatsApp (tenant comms), Excel or Google Sheets (rent tracking), Acrobat or Word (leases), bank statements (payment reconciliation), and possibly a basic CRM
- Decision maker has a smartphone and is comfortable with SaaS tools
- No existing property management software, OR has outgrown a basic tool like RentMaster
- PayProp may be in use for rent collection — Klikk complements rather than replaces it

### Buying Triggers

1. A recent lease dispute where a non-compliant agreement caused liability
2. A new tenant moved in without a signed lease because the process was too slow
3. The agency just won a new block of units and the manual process no longer scales
4. A competitor agency is pitching professionalism (digital leases, tenant app) as a differentiator to win landlord mandates
5. Owner of the agency wants to step back from day-to-day admin

### Competitive Displacement

- Displaces: Excel + WhatsApp + paper, RentMaster (declining), manual processes
- Competes against: WeConnectU (too expensive for this tier at ~R150–200/unit), Prop Data (enterprise, overkill)
- Wins on: price (Pro R2,500/month flat vs R150+/unit at competitors), AI lease generation, tenant app, agent mobile app, ease of setup

### Segment Size (SA)

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Total addressable firms | ~3 500–5 000 |
| Units per firm (avg) | 35 |
| Serviceable addressable market | ~1 500–2 000 firms (tech-ready, right size) |
| Firms in SAM | ~1 500–2 000 |
| MRR at Pro R2,500/month (SAM capture rate 10%) | ~R375 000–R500 000/month (directional estimate) |

## Tier 2 — Independent Landlord (Self-Managing)

### Firmographics

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| Entity type | Natural person, family trust, or small CC |
| Portfolio size | 1–10 residential units (own properties) |
| Property types | One or two investment properties; often a flat or townhouse in a complex |
| Operating model | Self-managing — no agency involvement |
| Revenue model | Rental income supplementing salary or retirement income |

### Geographic Concentration (SA)

Any SA metro. Slightly over-indexed in Cape Town and Johannesburg where investment property ownership rates are highest (NB: Stats SA Residential Property Report shows ~15% of mortgaged SA homeowners hold at least one investment property). Stellenbosch and Winelands are high-value niche given the founder's network.

Estimated addressable count (SA): ~150 000–250 000 self-managing landlords (derived from: ~1.1m rental households in formal sector per Census 2022; approximately 15–20% managed privately by owner without an agent).

### Tech Readiness

- WhatsApp for everything — tenant comms, maintenance requests, lease sharing
- Bank statements checked manually for rent payment
- Lease either from a template downloaded online (often non-compliant) or from a lawyer (expensive)
- Comfortable with smartphones; may or may not be confident with web-based software
- Decision made quickly and alone — no committee

### Buying Triggers

1. A tenant disputing a lease clause because the document is outdated or non-RHA compliant
2. A maintenance issue that escalated because there was no paper trail
3. A tenant leaving and the landlord has no confidence in their ability to deduct from the deposit
4. Moving from 1 to 2–3 units — complexity increases faster than expected
5. Referred by another landlord or estate agent

### Competitive Displacement

- Displaces: WhatsApp + spreadsheet + Word template
- There is no real software competitor at this tier — the competition is inertia
- Wins on: free tier (up to 5 properties), no setup complexity, SA-compliant leases out of the box

### Segment Size (SA)

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Total addressable landlords | ~150 000–250 000 |
| Likely free-tier users (1–5 properties) | ~80% |
| Likely paying users (6+ properties, Pro R2,500/month) | ~20% |
| Serviceable addressable (tech-ready, any metro) | ~30 000–50 000 |
| MRR potential (10% conversion, R2,500/month flat) | ~R750 000–R1 250 000/month (directional estimate) |

Note: This tier converts at lower ARPU but generates referrals and builds brand trust. It is the top-of-funnel for agents who start as landlords and grow into agency operations.

## Tier 3 — Estate Agency with a Dedicated Rental Department

### Firmographics

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| Entity type | Registered estate agency, franchise or independent |
| Staff | 10–50 staff (3–15 in the rental department) |
| Portfolio size | 50–300 residential units under management |
| Property types | Mix of apartments, cluster homes, freehold |
| Operating model | Rental department inside a broader sales/commercial agency |
| Revenue model | Rental management fees + sales commissions (rentals are often the "cash flow" department) |

### Geographic Concentration (SA)

Franchise branches of Pam Golding, Seeff, Chas Everitt, Rawson, and RE/MAX are the primary targets. Independent boutique agencies in high-value nodes (Atlantic Seaboard, Sandton, Umhlanga).

Estimated addressable count (SA): ~800–1 200 agencies with a meaningful rental book at this scale.

### Tech Readiness

- Likely on Prop Data or WeConnectU already
- Principal agent or operations manager makes the decision — needs a business case
- Longer sales cycle (2–6 weeks) and may require a trial period
- Pays attention to owner reporting quality, as owners are also sales clients

### Buying Triggers

1. Current software contract is up for renewal — cost pressure opens the door
2. Rental department principal wants to modernise to attract younger agents
3. Agency is losing mandates to competitors offering slicker tenant/owner experiences
4. New franchise requirement or compliance audit creates urgency
5. Current software doesn't have a tenant app or mobile agent tools

### Competitive Displacement

- Displaces: WeConnectU (R150–200/unit), Prop Data, or hybrid manual
- Competes against: WeConnectU (feature parity argument), PayProp + Excel
- Wins on: AI features (unique differentiator WeConnectU lacks), tenant app, agent mobile app, Enterprise flat-rate pricing (R4,000/month base — significant saving vs R150–200/unit at 120 units), faster lease turnaround

### Segment Size (SA)

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Total addressable agencies | ~800–1 200 |
| Units per agency (avg) | 120 |
| Serviceable addressable (willing to switch) | ~200–400 agencies |
| Agencies in SAM | ~200–400 |
| MRR at Enterprise R4,000/month (SAM capture rate 10%) | ~R80 000–R160 000/month (directional estimate) |

## Disqualifiers (Universal)

- Commercial-only portfolios (Klikk is residential)
- Portfolios outside SA (SA-law-specific)
- Primary need is trust accounting / PayProp replacement — point them to PayProp, offer integration later
- Needs full ERP / accounting suite — out of scope for v1
