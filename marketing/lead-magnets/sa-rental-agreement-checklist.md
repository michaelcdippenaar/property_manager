# The SA Rental Agreement Checklist

**Everything your lease must contain under the Rental Housing Act (Act 50 of 1999, as amended).**

A one-page reference for landlords, agents, and property managers in South Africa. Use this before you sign.

---

## Why this checklist exists

Section 5(2) of the Rental Housing Act requires every residential lease in South Africa to be in writing and to contain specific information. If it doesn't, the lease is still binding on the landlord — but you lose enforcement leverage when things go wrong (rent arrears, deposit disputes, early termination).

Most Word templates floating around SA miss at least three of these. We've seen it on dozens of leases.

---

## The 12 things every SA lease must include

### Parties and property
- [ ] **1. Full names and ID numbers** of landlord and tenant (natural persons), or registered company details
- [ ] **2. Physical address of the leased property** — including unit/erf number where relevant
- [ ] **3. Landlord's service address** for notices (physical and email)

### Money
- [ ] **4. Monthly rental amount** in Rands, and the date it is due
- [ ] **5. Reasonable escalation** (annual %) stated up front — not added later
- [ ] **6. Deposit amount** — and that it will be held in an interest-bearing account
- [ ] **7. Who pays utilities** — water, electricity, refuse, sewerage (with meter-reading method if split)

### Term
- [ ] **8. Lease start date and end date** (fixed term) OR notice period for month-to-month
- [ ] **9. Renewal terms** — automatic, fixed-term roll, or month-to-month on expiry

### Condition and obligations
- [ ] **10. Incoming inspection** — joint inspection before occupation, with written record of defects (required under s5(3))
- [ ] **11. Outgoing inspection** — joint inspection before handover, within 3 business days of expiry
- [ ] **12. House rules** — pets, smoking, guests, alterations, subletting

---

## The 5 things most templates get wrong

1. **Deposit clause doesn't mention interest.** The RHA requires interest to be paid to the tenant on refund. Silence doesn't waive it.
2. **No incoming inspection clause.** Without it, you have no baseline to justify deductions at move-out.
3. **Escalation left blank or "to be agreed".** Unenforceable. Specify the % or method now.
4. **Termination by landlord is too loose.** Under the Prevention of Illegal Eviction Act (PIE), you cannot evict without a court order — your lease cannot override this.
5. **No POPIA consent clause.** If you hold tenant ID numbers, banking details, or credit-vetting data, you need lawful basis and a short privacy notice.

---

## Before you sign

- Read every clause aloud with the tenant present. Unclear language is interpreted *against* the drafter.
- Sign in wet ink OR via a qualifying electronic signature (ECTA s13). Initial every page.
- Give the tenant a copy. The RHA requires it.
- Keep the signed original for at least 3 years after termination.
