# AI Lease vs Word Template: How We Generate Leases at Klikk

Ask any rental agent in South Africa where their lease template came from. The honest answer is usually one of three things: a Word document their colleague passed around the office three years ago, a PDF their attorney drafted once and they have been editing ever since, or something they found online and have quietly adapted over time.

None of these are wrong, exactly. But all three carry the same risk: the template was created at a point in time, and the law has moved on since then.

---

## What Goes Wrong With Word Templates

The fundamental problem with a Word template is that it does not know what year it is.

The Consumer Protection Act's impact on fixed-term lease cancellations, the Rental Housing Amendment Act's strengthened tenant protections, evolving Rental Housing Tribunal case law on deposits — none of that makes its way into a template automatically. Someone has to go in and update it. In most agencies and small portfolios, that does not happen.

Beyond staleness, Word templates create a second category of problem: editing errors.

When a lease is a dense 12-page Word document, it is easy to change the rent amount on page 1 and miss the reference to the same figure on page 4. Or to delete the landlord's signature block while reformatting. Or to leave in a clause from the previous tenancy — including the previous tenant's name.

These are not hypothetical. They are the kinds of defects that surface at the Rental Housing Tribunal, where a poorly edited document weakens a landlord's position at exactly the wrong moment.

## How Klikk's AI Lease Generation Works

The workflow is three steps.

**Step 1: Tell it the basics.**
You provide the facts that are specific to this tenancy: tenant name, property address, monthly rental, deposit amount, lease start and end date, and any special conditions. Nothing more complicated than filling in a form.

**Step 2: AI generates the lease.**
Klikk uses Claude AI to produce a complete, structured lease document. The output includes all the clauses required by section 5(2) of the Rental Housing Act (Act 50 of 1999), correctly worded deposit investment language, the appropriate notice period provisions, maintenance responsibility clauses, and correctly placed signature blocks with merge fields for each signatory.

The AI does not start from a blank page each time. It works from a foundation of RHA-aligned clause templates that have been structured for legal accuracy, then populates and adjusts them based on the details you provided. Where the tenancy has specific requirements — a furnished property, a pet permission, a specific escalation formula — the AI incorporates those into the relevant clauses.

**Step 3: You review and edit.**
The draft appears in Klikk's template editor. You read it. You check the figures. You make any amendments using the WYSIWYG editor. If there is a clause you want to add or rephrase, you do that here — or ask the AI to generate an alternative version of a specific clause from the built-in clause library.

When you are satisfied, you send it for e-signing. The tenant receives a secure link and signs from any device.

## The "AI Drafts, You Review" Safety Model

There is a reasonable concern about trusting AI with legal documents. It is worth being direct about what the AI does and does not do here.

The AI does the grunt work: pulling the right clauses together, inserting the correct statutory language, populating merge fields, and formatting the document consistently. It removes the risk of accidentally leaving out a required clause, which is the most common way template leases fail.

What the AI does not do is replace your review. You are still responsible for checking that the figures are correct, that any special conditions are accurately captured, and that the document reflects the actual agreement between you and your tenant. If you have a specific legal question — about a complex commercial sub-lease, a lease over an unusual property type, or a dispute that is already in progress — you should involve an attorney.

For the vast majority of standard residential tenancies, the AI produces a complete, accurate lease that you can review, adjust, and send for signature in a fraction of the time it takes to edit a Word template.

That is the practical case for AI lease generation. Not that it is smarter than a lawyer. But that it is faster, more consistent, and less likely to leave out the clause about depositing the deposit in an interest-bearing account.
