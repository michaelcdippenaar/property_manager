6. OCCUPANCY

The Property shall be used for residential purposes only. The maximum number of authorised occupants is {{ max_occupants }}. The Tenant shall not permit any additional persons to reside in the Property without the Landlord's prior written consent.

The Tenant shall not sublet the whole or any part of the Property, nor cede or assign any right under this lease, without the prior written consent of the Landlord. Any purported subletting or cession without consent is void and constitutes a material breach of this lease.

The Tenant shall comply at all times with the body corporate conduct rules and house rules of the {{ property_name }} scheme. Any fine or penalty imposed on the Landlord by the body corporate as a direct result of the Tenant's conduct shall be for the Tenant's account.

No pets may be kept in the Property without the prior written consent of the Landlord and, where required, the body corporate. Any consent given for a specific pet does not extend to additional animals.

7. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

The Landlord shall maintain the Property in a habitable and structurally sound condition and shall attend to all major repairs that are not caused by the Tenant's negligence or wilful act.

The Tenant shall:

  1. Keep the Property clean and in a reasonable state of repair;
  2. Report all maintenance defects to the Landlord in writing promptly upon discovery;
  3. Be responsible for minor day-to-day maintenance, including but not limited to replacement of light bulbs, unblocking of drains caused by the Tenant's use, and pest control arising from the Tenant's conduct;
  4. Not carry out any structural alterations or improvements without the Landlord's prior written consent and, where applicable, body corporate approval;
  5. Restore the Property to its original condition at the termination of the lease, fair wear and tear excepted.

Any damage caused by the Tenant or their visitors beyond fair wear and tear shall be repaired by the Tenant at the Tenant's cost. If the Tenant fails to do so, the Landlord may carry out the repairs and recover the cost from the deposit or as a debt from the Tenant.

8. INSPECTIONS

In compliance with section 5(3)(f) of the Rental Housing Act, the parties shall conduct a joint ingoing inspection of the Property before or on the commencement date of this lease. A written record of the condition of the Property shall be signed by both parties and shall serve as the baseline for assessing damage on termination.

The parties shall conduct a joint outgoing inspection within three (3) days before or after the Tenant vacates the Property. The Landlord shall give the Tenant reasonable written notice of the date and time. If the Tenant fails to attend the outgoing inspection after being duly notified, the Landlord may conduct the inspection without the Tenant and the record shall be binding.

The Landlord or the Landlord's authorised representative may enter the Property at any reasonable time, on not less than twenty-four (24) hours' written notice to the Tenant, for the purposes of inspection, repair, or showing the Property to prospective tenants or purchasers. In a genuine emergency, the Landlord may enter the Property without prior notice.

9. NOTICE AND TERMINATION

At the expiry of the Initial Term, this lease terminates automatically unless both parties agree in writing to renew it or the Tenant holds over with the Landlord's consent (in which case section 3 above applies).

Either party may terminate this lease during the Initial Term only in accordance with section 14 of the Consumer Protection Act, as set out in section 3 above, or on the grounds of material breach as set out below.

If either party commits a material breach of this lease and fails to remedy that breach within twenty (20) business days of written notice by the aggrieved party, the aggrieved party may cancel this lease on written notice, without prejudice to any claim for damages.

All notices under this lease shall be given in writing and shall be delivered by hand, by registered post, or by email to the addresses set out in section 1. A notice sent by email shall be deemed received on the first business day after transmission, subject to confirmation of delivery.

10. CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT

Where the Tenant is a natural person and the Landlord is in the business of letting residential property, this lease is subject to the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 (the "CPA").

In terms of section 14 of the CPA, the Tenant may cancel this fixed-term agreement before its expiry by giving the Landlord twenty (20) business days' written notice. If the Tenant cancels early, the Landlord may impose a reasonable cancellation penalty. The penalty must be proportionate to actual losses sustained by the Landlord and may never exceed the actual loss; a penalty equal to all remaining rent under the term is unlawful.

Nothing in this lease limits, excludes, or undermines any right conferred on the Tenant by the CPA or the Rental Housing Act. In the event of any conflict between the terms of this lease and the CPA or the Rental Housing Act, the statute shall prevail.

11. PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION (POPIA)

The Landlord collects and processes the personal information of the Tenant (including name, identity number, contact details, and banking information) for the following purposes:

  1. Conclusion and administration of this lease agreement (lawful basis: contract — POPIA s11(1)(b));
  2. Compliance with applicable legislation, including the Rental Housing Act and FICA (lawful basis: legal obligation — POPIA s11(1)(c));
  3. Communication in connection with the tenancy (lawful basis: legitimate interest — POPIA s11(1)(f)).

The Tenant's personal information will not be shared with third parties except as required by law or strictly necessary for the administration of the tenancy (for example, with a managing agent, body corporate, or legal representative). The Landlord shall implement reasonable technical and organisational measures to safeguard the Tenant's information against unlawful access, loss, or damage.

The Tenant has the right to request access to their personal information, to request correction of inaccurate information, and to object to processing in certain circumstances, by contacting the Landlord at the address or email set out in section 1. Complaints may be directed to the Information Regulator of South Africa.

Personal information collected in connection with this lease will be retained for the duration of the tenancy and for a period of five (5) years thereafter, or such longer period as may be required by law.

12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Any dispute arising from or in connection with this lease shall first be submitted to the Rental Housing Tribunal having jurisdiction in the area where the Property is situated, in accordance with the Rental Housing Act. The parties acknowledge that the Tribunal has jurisdiction to determine disputes concerning unfair practices, deposit refunds, maintenance obligations, and lease interpretation.

If a dispute falls outside the Tribunal's jurisdiction, or if the Tribunal cannot achieve resolution, either party may refer the dispute to a court of competent jurisdiction. Both parties consent to the jurisdiction of the Magistrate's Court for the district in which the Property is situated, notwithstanding that the amount or value of the relief sought might exceed the Magistrate's Court's monetary jurisdiction.

Nothing in this clause prevents either party from approaching a court of competent jurisdiction for urgent or interim relief.
