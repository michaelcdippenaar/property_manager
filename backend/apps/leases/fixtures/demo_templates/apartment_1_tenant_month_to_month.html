3. LEASE PERIOD

This lease shall commence on {{ lease_start }} and shall continue on a month-to-month basis until terminated in accordance with the notice provisions below (the "Month-to-Month Tenancy").

Either party may terminate this lease by giving not less than one (1) calendar month's written notice to the other party. Notice must be given in writing to the other party's address set out in section 1. The tenancy shall end at the expiry of the last day of the calendar month following the month in which notice is given.

The Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 does not impose fixed cancellation-penalty obligations on month-to-month leases, but all other provisions of the CPA applicable to residential tenancies remain in force.

6. OCCUPANCY

The Property shall be used for residential purposes only. The maximum number of authorised occupants is {{ max_occupants }}. The Tenant shall not permit additional persons to reside in the Property without the Landlord's prior written consent.

The Tenant shall not sublet the whole or any part of the Property, nor cede or assign any right under this lease, without the Landlord's prior written consent. Any purported subletting or cession without consent is void and constitutes a material breach.

The Tenant shall comply at all times with the building's house rules and conduct rules. Any fine imposed on the Landlord by the body corporate or managing agent as a direct result of the Tenant's conduct shall be for the Tenant's account.

No pets may be kept in the Property without the prior written consent of the Landlord and, where applicable, the body corporate.

7. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

The Landlord shall maintain the Property in a habitable and structurally sound condition and shall attend to all major repairs not caused by the Tenant's negligence or wilful act.

The Tenant shall:

  1. Keep the Property clean and in a reasonable state of repair;
  2. Report all maintenance defects to the Landlord in writing promptly upon discovery;
  3. Be responsible for minor day-to-day maintenance, including replacement of light bulbs, unblocking of drains caused by their use, and pest control arising from their conduct;
  4. Not carry out structural alterations or improvements without the Landlord's prior written consent and, where applicable, body corporate approval;
  5. Restore the Property to its original condition at termination, fair wear and tear excepted.

Any damage beyond fair wear and tear shall be repaired by the Tenant at the Tenant's cost. If the Tenant fails to do so, the Landlord may effect repairs and recover the cost from the deposit or as a debt.

8. INSPECTIONS

In compliance with section 5(3)(f) of the Rental Housing Act, the parties shall conduct a joint ingoing inspection before or on the commencement date. A written record of the Property's condition shall be signed by both parties and shall serve as the baseline for assessing damage on termination.

The parties shall conduct a joint outgoing inspection within three (3) days before or after the Tenant vacates. The Landlord shall give reasonable written notice. If the Tenant fails to attend after due notification, the Landlord may conduct the inspection without the Tenant and the record shall be binding.

The Landlord may enter the Property at any reasonable time on not less than twenty-four (24) hours' written notice for inspection, repair, or showing the Property to prospective occupants. In a genuine emergency, no prior notice is required.

9. NOTICE AND TERMINATION

Either party may terminate this month-to-month lease by giving not less than one (1) calendar month's written notice to the other. The notice must specify the last day of the calendar month on which the tenancy will end.

In addition to termination by notice, either party may cancel this lease on grounds of material breach. If either party commits a material breach and fails to remedy it within twenty (20) business days of written notice by the aggrieved party, the aggrieved party may cancel this lease on written notice without prejudice to any claim for damages.

All notices shall be in writing, delivered by hand, registered post, or email to the addresses in section 1. An email notice shall be deemed received on the first business day after transmission, subject to delivery confirmation.

10. CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT

Where the Tenant is a natural person and the Landlord is in the business of letting residential property, this lease is subject to the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008.

As this is a month-to-month agreement, the fixed-term cancellation-penalty provisions of CPA section 14 do not apply. However, all other CPA rights applicable to residential tenancies remain in full force, including the right to fair, honest dealing and the right to safe and quality accommodation.

Nothing in this lease limits, excludes, or undermines any right conferred on the Tenant by the CPA or the Rental Housing Act. In the event of conflict, the statute shall prevail.

11. PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION (POPIA)

The Landlord collects and processes the personal information of the Tenant (including name, identity number, contact details, and banking information) for the following purposes:

  1. Conclusion and administration of this lease (lawful basis: contract — POPIA s11(1)(b));
  2. Compliance with applicable legislation, including the Rental Housing Act and FICA (lawful basis: legal obligation — POPIA s11(1)(c));
  3. Communication in connection with the tenancy (lawful basis: legitimate interest — POPIA s11(1)(f)).

The Tenant's personal information will not be shared with third parties except as required by law or strictly necessary for tenancy administration. The Landlord shall implement reasonable security measures to protect the Tenant's information.

The Tenant has the right to request access to and correction of their personal information by contacting the Landlord at the address in section 1. Complaints may be directed to the Information Regulator of South Africa. Personal information will be retained for five (5) years after termination, or longer if required by law.

12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Any dispute arising from or in connection with this lease shall first be submitted to the Rental Housing Tribunal having jurisdiction in the area where the Property is situated. The Tribunal has jurisdiction over unfair practices, deposit refunds, maintenance obligations, and lease interpretation.

If a dispute falls outside the Tribunal's jurisdiction, either party may refer it to a court of competent jurisdiction. Both parties consent to the jurisdiction of the Magistrate's Court for the district in which the Property is situated.

Nothing in this clause prevents either party from approaching a court for urgent or interim relief.
