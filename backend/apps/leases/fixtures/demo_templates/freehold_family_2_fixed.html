3. LEASE PERIOD

This lease shall commence on {{ lease_start }} and shall expire on {{ lease_end }} (the "Initial Term").

Should the Tenants remain in occupation after the expiry of the Initial Term with the express or tacit consent of the Landlord, the lease shall continue on a month-to-month basis on the same terms and conditions, terminable by either party on not less than one (1) calendar month's written notice.

Where either or both Tenants are natural persons and the Landlord is in the business of letting residential property, the Tenants have the right under section 14 of the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 to cancel this fixed-term lease on twenty (20) business days' written notice. The Landlord may impose a reasonable cancellation penalty proportionate to actual losses — never the full outstanding rental for the remainder of the term.

6. OCCUPANCY

The Property shall be used for residential family purposes only. The maximum number of authorised occupants is {{ max_occupants }}. The Tenants shall not permit additional persons to reside at the Property without the Landlord's prior written consent.

The Tenants shall not sublet the whole or any part of the Property, nor cede or assign any right under this lease, without the Landlord's prior written consent. Any purported subletting or cession without consent is void and constitutes a material breach.

No business activities generating customer traffic, requiring signage, or constituting a change of land use shall be conducted from the Property without the Landlord's prior written consent and local authority approval.

No pets may be kept at the Property without the Landlord's prior written consent.

7. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

The Landlord shall maintain the Property in a habitable and structurally sound condition and shall attend to all major repairs not caused by the Tenants' negligence or wilful act.

The Tenants shall:

  1. Keep the Property, outbuildings, and yard clean and in a reasonable state of repair;
  2. Report all maintenance defects in writing promptly upon discovery;
  3. Be responsible for minor day-to-day maintenance, including light bulb replacement, drain unblocking caused by their use, pest control arising from their conduct, and garden upkeep;
  4. Not carry out structural alterations without the Landlord's prior written consent;
  5. Restore the Property to its original condition at termination, fair wear and tear excepted.

Any damage beyond fair wear and tear shall be repaired by the Tenants at their cost. If they fail to do so, the Landlord may effect repairs and recover the cost from the deposit or as a debt.

8. INSPECTIONS

In compliance with section 5(3)(f) of the Rental Housing Act, the parties shall conduct a joint ingoing inspection before or on the commencement date. A written record signed by all parties shall serve as the baseline for assessing damage on termination.

The parties shall conduct a joint outgoing inspection within three (3) days before or after the Tenants vacate. The Landlord shall give reasonable written notice. If the Tenants fail to attend after due notification, the Landlord may conduct the inspection without them and the record shall be binding.

The Landlord may enter the Property at any reasonable time on not less than twenty-four (24) hours' written notice for inspection, repair, or showing the Property to prospective occupants. In a genuine emergency, no prior notice is required.

9. NOTICE AND TERMINATION

At the expiry of the Initial Term, this lease terminates automatically unless both parties agree in writing to renew or the Tenants hold over with the Landlord's consent.

Either party may terminate during the Initial Term only in accordance with CPA section 14 (see section 3) or on grounds of material breach as set out below.

If any party commits a material breach and fails to remedy it within twenty (20) business days of written notice, the aggrieved party may cancel this lease on written notice without prejudice to any claim for damages.

All notices shall be in writing, delivered by hand, registered post, or email to the addresses in section 1. An email notice shall be deemed received on the first business day after transmission, subject to delivery confirmation.

10. CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT

Where either or both Tenants are natural persons and the Landlord is in the business of letting residential property, this lease is subject to the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008.

In terms of section 14 of the CPA, the Tenants may cancel this fixed-term agreement on twenty (20) business days' written notice. The Landlord may impose a reasonable cancellation penalty proportionate to actual losses; a penalty equal to all remaining rent is unlawful.

Nothing in this lease limits, excludes, or undermines any right conferred on the Tenants by the CPA or the Rental Housing Act. In the event of conflict, the statute shall prevail.

11. PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION (POPIA)

The Landlord collects and processes the personal information of both Tenants (including names, identity numbers, contact details, and banking information) for the following purposes:

  1. Conclusion and administration of this lease (lawful basis: contract — POPIA s11(1)(b));
  2. Compliance with applicable legislation, including the Rental Housing Act and FICA (lawful basis: legal obligation — POPIA s11(1)(c));
  3. Communication in connection with the tenancy (lawful basis: legitimate interest — POPIA s11(1)(f)).

Personal information will not be shared with third parties except as required by law or strictly necessary for tenancy administration. Reasonable security measures will be implemented to protect both Tenants' information.

Each Tenant has the right to request access to and correction of their personal information by contacting the Landlord at the address in section 1. Complaints may be directed to the Information Regulator of South Africa. Personal information will be retained for five (5) years after termination, or longer if required by law.

12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Any dispute arising from or in connection with this lease shall first be submitted to the Rental Housing Tribunal having jurisdiction in the area where the Property is situated. The Tribunal has jurisdiction over unfair practices, deposit refunds, maintenance obligations, and lease interpretation.

If a dispute falls outside the Tribunal's jurisdiction, either party may refer it to a court of competent jurisdiction. Both parties consent to the jurisdiction of the Magistrate's Court for the district in which the Property is situated.

Nothing in this clause prevents either party from approaching a court for urgent or interim relief.
