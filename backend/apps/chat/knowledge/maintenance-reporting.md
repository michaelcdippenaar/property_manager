# Reporting Maintenance Issues

## How do I report a repair or maintenance problem?
You can report a maintenance issue in three ways:

1. **AI chat** — Describe the problem in the Klikk chat. The AI will ask clarifying questions if needed and log a ticket automatically.
2. **Report Issue button** — Tap "Report Issue" in the app to fill in a structured form with title, description, and photos.
3. **Contact your agent directly** — Phone or email your managing agent. Ask them to log the issue in the system so there is a record.

## What information should I provide?
Be as specific as possible:
- What is broken or not working (e.g. "the kitchen mixer tap is dripping")
- Where in the unit (e.g. "main bedroom", "en-suite bathroom")
- When it started
- Whether it is getting worse
- Any immediate safety risk (e.g. sparking socket, flooding)

Photos and videos are very helpful — attach them when you report the issue.

## What happens after I report?
1. A maintenance request is logged and you receive a reference number.
2. Your agent reviews the request and schedules a contractor.
3. You will be notified when a contractor is assigned and when the job is completed.
4. You may be asked to confirm the repair is done before the ticket is closed.

## Emergency repairs
For emergencies (burst pipe, no electricity, gas smell, break-in, flooding), call your agent immediately — do not wait for the app. After the call, log it in the app as well so there is a record.

Emergency hints:
- Burst pipe: shut off the water at the main stopcock (ask your agent where it is).
- Electrical fault: switch off at the DB board if safe to do so.
- Gas smell: open windows and doors, do not use any switches, leave the property, call the fire department.

## Who is responsible for repairs?
The landlord is responsible for maintaining the property in a habitable, waterproof, and structurally sound condition (RHA s13(1)). Tenants are responsible for damage they cause beyond normal wear and tear.

Tenant responsibilities typically include:
- Replacing light bulbs
- Cleaning and unblocking drains
- Minor cosmetic touch-ups they caused

Landlord responsibilities typically include:
- Structural repairs
- Geyser replacement
- Roof leaks
- Electrical faults not caused by the tenant

## Response times
Urgency levels determine response time:
- **Urgent** (danger to life/property): same day
- **High** (serious defect): 1–3 business days
- **Medium** (normal repair): within 7 days
- **Low** (cosmetic): scheduled at convenience

## Disputes about repairs
If the landlord refuses to repair something they are responsible for, approach the Rental Housing Tribunal in your province. You cannot withhold rent unilaterally — always seek formal mediation first.
