# Paying Your Rent — Methods and Due Dates

## When is rent due?
Rent is due on the date stated in your lease, typically the 1st of each month. Late payment may incur interest or penalties as specified in your lease agreement.

## How do I pay rent?
Your lease will specify the accepted payment method. Common methods in South Africa include:

**EFT (Electronic Funds Transfer)**
Most landlords prefer EFT. Your lease or welcome letter will provide the bank name, account holder name, account number, and branch code. Use your name and unit number as the payment reference so the payment is allocated correctly.

**Debit order**
Some managing agents set up a debit order for tenants. If you agreed to a debit order, ensure there are sufficient funds in your account on the debit date.

## What reference should I use?
Always use the reference your agent provided (usually your name + unit number). Using the wrong reference can cause your payment to be unallocated, which may show as arrears even though you paid.

## How do I know if my payment was received?
You can check your rent status in the Klikk tenant portal under the Payments section. If a payment you made is not reflected after 2 business days, contact your agent and send proof of payment (a bank statement or screenshot showing the EFT).

## What if I cannot pay on time?
Contact your agent immediately — before the due date if possible. Discuss a payment arrangement in writing. Eviction proceedings cannot begin without a court order, but arrears accrue interest and can affect your credit record. The Rental Housing Tribunal can also mediate payment disputes.

## Rent receipts
Your agent should provide you with a receipt or written confirmation for each payment. On Klikk, receipts are generated automatically and available in your portal.

## Cash payments
Avoid paying in cash where possible. If cash is the only option, always get a signed receipt immediately.

## Bank account changes
If your managing agent changes the bank account, they must notify you in writing. Be cautious — always verify a new account number with your agent directly (by phone or in person) before making a payment to prevent fraud.
