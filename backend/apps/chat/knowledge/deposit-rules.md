# Deposit Rules — South African Residential Tenancies

## How much deposit is required?
Your lease agreement specifies the deposit amount. By law (Rental Housing Act s5(3)) the deposit may not exceed two months' rent, though many landlords charge one month's rent as deposit.

## Where is my deposit held?
The landlord or managing agent must place your deposit in an interest-bearing bank account separate from their own funds. You are entitled to the name of the bank and account number on request.

## Can the landlord use my deposit during the tenancy?
No. The deposit may only be used after the lease ends to cover unpaid rent or damage beyond normal wear and tear. It cannot be accessed during the tenancy.

## What counts as normal wear and tear?
Normal wear and tear includes things like faded paintwork, minor scuffs on walls, and carpet flattening from regular use. Damage that goes beyond normal use — stains, broken fittings, holes in walls — can be deducted from your deposit.

## When must the deposit be refunded?
The landlord must refund your deposit (plus interest) within 14 days of the lease end date if there is no damage. If an outgoing inspection reveals damage, they have 21 days to complete repairs and then return the balance with a full itemised statement.

## Outgoing inspection
The landlord or agent must conduct a joint outgoing inspection with you before or on the last day of your lease. You have the right to be present. Any damage noted during this inspection justifies a deduction. If the landlord refuses to do a joint inspection, they lose the right to make deductions.

## Receiving the deposit back
The refund should be paid to the bank account you provide. If no account is provided, the landlord must make reasonable efforts to trace you. Interest accrued on the deposit belongs to you.

## Disputes about deposit deductions
If you believe a deduction is unfair, you can approach the Rental Housing Tribunal in your province free of charge. Keep your original lease, the incoming inspection report, and any photos you took when you moved in.

## POPIA — your deposit data
Your deposit amount and bank account details are stored securely and used only for the purpose of managing your tenancy. You can request a copy of your data at any time through the Klikk tenant portal.
