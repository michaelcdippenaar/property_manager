# Moving Out — Notice, Inspections, and Handover

## How much notice must I give?
You must give notice equal to the notice period in your lease (usually 1 month, sometimes 2 months for longer leases). Notice must be given in writing and must be received by the landlord or agent by the required date. If your lease expires on a fixed date, confirm with your agent whether notice is still required.

## When does my notice period start?
Notice typically starts on the first day of the following month if given mid-month, but this depends on your specific lease wording. Read your lease carefully or ask your agent.

## Can I leave before the end of my notice period?
If you leave before your lease or notice period ends, you may be liable for rent for the remaining period and possibly an early termination penalty (as specified in your lease). Your deposit cannot be used to cover future rent while you are still a tenant.

## Outgoing inspection
Before or on your last day, the landlord or agent will conduct a joint inspection of the unit. You have the right to attend. The agent will compare the unit to the incoming inspection report.

Tips for the inspection:
- Clean the unit thoroughly.
- Repair any damage you caused (holes in walls, broken fittings, stains).
- Return all keys, remotes, and access cards.
- Ensure all appliances work and all light fittings have bulbs.

## Returning keys
Return all keys, parking remotes, garage openers, intercom fobs, and access cards on or before your last day. Lost keys may be deducted from your deposit.

## Forwarding your mail
Update your address with SARS, your bank, medical aid, and any other services before you move. The new tenant or owner is not responsible for forwarding your mail.

## Deposit refund timeline
After the outgoing inspection:
- If no damage: deposit (plus interest) must be refunded within 14 days.
- If there is damage: the landlord has 21 days to complete repairs and return the balance with an itemised statement.

## Disputes
If you disagree with deductions, you can approach the Rental Housing Tribunal in your province. Keep your incoming inspection report, lease, and any photos or correspondence as evidence.

## Utilities
Settle your electricity prepaid top-ups and ensure there are no outstanding municipal accounts before you leave. Your lease will state who is responsible for final meter readings.
