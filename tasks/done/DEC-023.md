## Question

Which email provider, sender domain, and from/reply-to addresses should Klikk use for transactional email? What auth records (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) are required?

## Options

**Option A — AWS SES af-south-1 on klikk.co.za apex**
- Pros: matches existing hosting region (af-south-1), consolidates vendors, competitive per-message cost.
- Cons: klikk.co.za apex currently uses Google Workspace for human mailboxes (mc@klikk.co.za); SES auth records must be scoped to a subdomain or isolated carefully.

**Option B — AWS SES af-south-1 on mail.klikk.co.za subdomain**
- Pros: fully isolated from Google Workspace SPF/DKIM on the apex; no risk of breaking mc@klikk.co.za delivery.
- Cons: sender domain is mail.klikk.co.za not klikk.co.za (minor cosmetic difference in headers).

**PM recommendation:** Option B (mail.klikk.co.za subdomain) to keep Google Workspace intact on the apex, consistent with OPS-006 dual-domain design.

## Answer

FINAL (2026-04-24 — MC):

- **Provider:** AWS SES in af-south-1 (matches production hosting region)
- **Sender domain:** klikk.co.za (apex)
- **Transactional from:** no-reply@klikk.co.za
- **Reply-to:** support@klikk.co.za
- **Auth:** DKIM + SPF + DMARC on klikk.co.za

> Note: OPS-006 already specifies a dual-domain setup (`klikk.co.za` apex keeps Google Workspace, `mail.klikk.co.za` carries SES auth). The implementer of OPS-006 must reconcile MC's klikk.co.za apex preference with the need to avoid disrupting `mc@klikk.co.za` Google Workspace delivery. The safest path is: set SES DKIM CNAME records on klikk.co.za (they coexist with Google's DKIM selectors), add SES to the klikk.co.za SPF `include:`, and ensure DMARC on the apex covers both senders. The `mail.klikk.co.za` subdomain in OPS-006 may be simplified or removed accordingly.
