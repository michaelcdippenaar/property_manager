## Question

No attorney-drafted Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Cookie Policy, or Acceptable Use Policy exist yet. Should Klikk delay launch until attorney-drafted versions are ready, or publish template-based drafts with appropriate disclaimers now and engage an attorney post-launch?

## Options

**Option A — Delay launch until attorney-drafted documents are ready**
- Pros: fully reviewed legal pages at launch; no disclaimer needed.
- Cons: unknown attorney timeline blocks the entire launch; disproportionate risk for a soft launch with limited early adopters.

**Option B — Template-based drafts at launch, attorney review post-launch (recommended)**
- Pros: unblocks OPS-004 and the marketing website immediately; POPIA-compliant drafts using klikk-legal-POPIA-RHA skill + SA context (POPIA, RHA, CPA, ECTA); footer disclaimer signals status transparently; attorney review is a defined post-launch item.
- Cons: drafts are not attorney-reviewed at the moment of launch; disclaimer is visible to users.

## Recommendation

Option B. Soft-launch with template-based drafts. The documents must be POPIA-compliant, plain-English, SA-governed, and carry a "Pending attorney review" footer disclaimer. Attorney review is a post-launch ship item (tracked in OPS-024).

## Answer

FINAL (2026-04-24 — MC):

- No attorney-drafted documents exist. Proceed with template-based drafts.
- Pages needed: Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Cookie Policy, Acceptable Use Policy.
- Voice: plain English, SA context (POPIA, RHA, CPA), ZAR, South African law governing.
- Footer disclaimer: "Pending attorney review" must appear on all four legal pages until attorney-reviewed versions are published.
- Attorney review is NOT a v1.0 ship blocker. Post-launch ticket filed: OPS-024.
- Use klikk-legal-POPIA-RHA skill for SA citations and compliance framing when drafting content.
