### 2026-04-22 — rentals-reviewer — Review requested changes

**Verdict: SEND BACK — pricing data does not match `content/product/pricing.yaml`.**

The feature claims and PLANNED/BUILT discipline are good — the gap transparency table is thorough and the matrix correctly marks trust accounting, TPN, advertising, and inspections as (P). However the submission fails on pricing accuracy, which is a hard block because incorrect pricing in positioning.md and competitive-matrix.md will propagate directly into website copy, sales decks, and prospect conversations.

**Required fixes — numbered for tracking:**

1. **`content/brand/positioning.md` line 95 — remove per-unit pricing entirely.**
   Current text: "Klikk charges R49/unit (Pro tier) or R39/unit (Agency tier). A boutique PM firm managing 40 units pays ~R1 960/month"
   Problem: No per-unit pricing model exists in `pricing.yaml`. Pro is R2,500/month flat (up to 50 properties / 100 units). "Agency" is not a tier name in `pricing.yaml` — the R4,000/month tier is named "Enterprise". The R1,960 figure is arithmetically derived from a fictitious per-unit rate.
   Fix: Rewrite Pillar 4 to use actual pricing.yaml values. Example framing: "Pro at R2,500/month covers up to 50 properties or 100 units. WeConnectU charges R150–200/unit — a 50-unit portfolio on WeConnectU costs R7,500–R10,000/month vs R2,500 on Klikk. Free tier covers 5 properties with no credit card." Do not invent per-unit figures.

2. **`content/brand/competitive-matrix.md` axis 19 (line 40) — remove per-unit column.**
   Current text: "R49 (Pro) / R39 (Agency)"
   Problem: Same as above — both figures are fabricated. The "Agency" tier label does not exist in `pricing.yaml`.
   Fix: Replace axis 19 with flat-rate comparison: "R2,500/mo (Pro, up to 100 units) / R4,000/mo (Enterprise, 100+ units)". The competitor per-unit figures for WeConnectU/RentMaster/HouseME may stand but should be flagged as directional.

3. **`content/brand/competitive-matrix.md` lines 79 and 129 — remove "R49/unit" in prose.**
   Line 79: "At R49/unit (Pro)..."
   Line 129: "Klikk offers all three AI and mobile capabilities at R49/unit."
   Fix: Replace with flat-rate equivalent or a cost-per-unit-at-capacity derivation that is transparently labelled as such (e.g. "Pro at R2,500/month across 100 units works out to R25/unit at capacity — vs WeConnectU's R150–200/unit"). If using a derived figure, label it as derived.

4. **`content/brand/competitive-matrix.md` line 40 — remove "Agency" tier name.**
   "R39 (Agency)" cites a tier that does not exist as a key in `pricing.yaml`. Even the header comment in pricing.yaml that says "Agency R4,000" is inconsistent with the actual `enterprise` tier key — a discovery has been filed for PM resolution (see `tasks/discoveries/2026-04-22-pricing-yaml-agency-tier-ghost.md`). Until that discovery is resolved and PM confirms the correct tier name, use "Enterprise (R4,000/mo)" only.

5. **`content/brand/competitive-matrix.md` line 180 — qualify the "70–75% cheaper per unit" claim.**
   This figure is derived from comparing a fictitious per-unit price against WeConnectU's per-unit rate. Once fixes 2–3 are applied, recalculate using a transparent basis: e.g. "R2,500/mo (Klikk Pro, 100-unit capacity) vs R15,000–R20,000/mo (WeConnectU at 100 units × R150–200/unit) — Klikk is 83–87% cheaper at capacity." Use real numbers with a stated basis, or remove the percentage claim entirely.

**What was done well (do not regress):**
- All 14 value-prop ladder rows correctly cite only BUILT features.
- Gap transparency table (positioning.md) is complete and accurate.
- Matrix PLANNED markers are correct — trust accounting, TPN, advertising, inspections all marked (P).
- Competitor-specific sales responses are well-targeted and do not overclaim.
- SA spelling and RHA terminology are consistent throughout.
- The one-liner, positioning statement, and four non-pricing messaging pillars are approved as-is.
