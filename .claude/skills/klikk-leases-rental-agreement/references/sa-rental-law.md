## Rental Housing Act 50 of 1999 (as amended) {#rental-housing-act}

### Key Provisions for Lease Agreements

**Section 4A — Unfair Practices**
- Landlords may not unreasonably refuse to enter into a lease
- Changing locks or cutting services to force eviction is unlawful
- Withholding a written lease is an unfair practice

**Section 5 — Leases**

| Sub-section | Requirement | Impact on Agreement |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| s5(2) | Tenant has right to a written lease | This agreement satisfies this requirement |
| s5(3)(a) | Lease must contain names and addresses of parties | Parties section must be complete |
| s5(3)(b) | Description of dwelling | Premises section required |
| s5(3)(c) | Rental amount and reasonable escalation | Rental section must specify both |
| s5(3)(d) | Term of lease, or if indefinite, notice period (minimum one calendar month under s5(5)) | Notice period must be >= one calendar month |
| s5(3)(e) | Deposit amount | Must be stated explicitly |
| s5(3)(f) ⚠️ LOW-confidence | Obligation to pay deposit into interest-bearing account, interest accrues for tenant | **Mandatory clause** — sub-section letter pending lawyer sign-off; see canonical map |
| s5(3)(g) | Rent due date | Must be stated |
| s5(3)(h) | Top-level listing of landlord and tenant obligations | Detailed duties at s5A (landlord) and s5B (tenant) |
| s5(3)(i) | Rules of the house annexed | Include house rules section |
| s5(3)(j) | Deposit purpose + refund terms | Must be stated in lease |
| s5(3)(k) | Joint ingoing / outgoing inspection requirements | Both inspections required |
| Refund deadlines ⚠️ LOW-confidence | 7 days (no damage), 14 days (damage + repairs), 21 days (tenant absent) | Sub-section letters pending lawyer sign-off; see canonical map |

> ⚠️ **There is no statutory deposit cap in s5(3).** The "2× monthly rent" convention is industry custom, not statute. Deposits disproportionate to rent may be challenged as an **unfair practice under s4A**.

**Section 5A — Obligations of Landlord**
- Maintain premises in habitable condition
- Carry out structural repairs
- Provide receipt for rent payments if requested
- Furnish tenant with copy of lease

**Section 5B — Obligations of Tenant**
- Pay rent on time
- Maintain premises in good condition (fair wear and tear excepted)
- Not alter the premises without consent
- Not use premises for unlawful purposes

**Section 7 — Rental Housing Tribunals (establishment)** and **Section 13 — Tribunal powers / referral**
- Provincial Rental Housing Tribunals are **established under s7** of the RHA
- Their powers, procedure, and the effect of their rulings are set out in **s13** (rulings have the effect of a Magistrate's Court order under s13(12))
- Any dispute should be referred to the provincial Rental Housing Tribunal
- Tribunal services are free of charge
- Tribunal has power to make binding rulings

### Deposit Rules (Detailed)

The deposit regime is one of the most litigated areas. Get these right:

1. **Cap**: The RHA does **not** impose a statutory deposit cap in s5(3). The "2× monthly rental" figure is industry convention. A deposit disproportionate to the rent may be challenged as an **unfair practice under s4A**, with provincial Tribunal jurisprudence applying the "reasonableness" test.
2. **Interest-bearing account**: Landlord must invest the deposit in an interest-bearing account with a financial institution.
3. **Interest accrual**: Interest accrues for the benefit of the tenant.
4. **Inspection**: Landlord must inspect premises within 7 days before or after termination, in the presence of the tenant (or after giving 3 days written notice if tenant is absent).
5. **Refund timing**: Within 14 days of expiry if no damage. Within 21 days if repairs are needed (must provide written quotation within 14 days).
6. **Lawful deductions**: Only for damage beyond fair wear and tear, unpaid rent, or unpaid utilities. Must be itemised.

## Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 {#consumer-protection-act}

The CPA applies to residential leases where the landlord is acting in the ordinary course of business (i.e., renting is a commercial activity). It does NOT apply to once-off private lets between individuals where neither party is in the business of letting.

### Key Sections

**Section 14 — Fixed-Term Agreements**
- Consumer (tenant) may cancel a fixed-term agreement with 20 business days' notice
- Landlord may impose a reasonable cancellation penalty (not the full remaining rent — must be proportionate to actual loss)
- Agreement must not exceed 24 months unless the consumer specifically requests a longer term
- Tenant must be notified 40-80 business days before expiry about renewal options

**Section 16 — Cooling-Off Period**
- For unsolicited agreements (direct marketing), tenant has 5 business days to cancel without penalty
- Does not apply if the tenant initiated contact

**Section 22 — Right to Information in Plain Language**
- Agreement must be in plain, understandable language
- Avoid unnecessary legal jargon

**Section 48 — Unfair Contract Terms**
- Terms that are excessively one-sided are void
- Penalty clauses must be reasonable and proportionate
- Cannot waive tenant's rights under the CPA

**Section 54 — Quality of Services**
- Landlord must deliver premises fit for the purpose for which they are let
- Repairs must be completed within a reasonable time

### CPA Compliance Checklist for Lease Agreements
- [ ] Fixed term does not exceed 24 months (unless tenant requested)
- [ ] Cancellation penalty is reasonable (not full remaining rent)
- [ ] Cooling-off clause included for unsolicited agreements
- [ ] Plain language used throughout
- [ ] No unfair or excessively one-sided terms
- [ ] Renewal notification obligation stated (40-80 business days before expiry)

## Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) {#popia}

### Applicability to Leases
Landlords and agents process personal information (names, ID numbers, financial details) and must comply with POPIA.

### Required Disclosures
1. **Purpose**: Information is collected for lease administration, credit checks, and legal compliance
2. **Recipients**: May be shared with managing agents, Rental Housing Tribunal, credit bureaus, or legal advisors as needed
3. **Retention**: Information retained for the duration of the lease plus the prescription period (3 years for contractual claims)
4. **Rights**: Data subjects may request access to, correction of, or deletion of their personal information (subject to legal retention requirements)

### Consent Clause Requirements
- Must be specific about what data is collected
- Must state the purpose
- Must identify third parties who may receive the data
- Must be voluntary (but may be necessary for lease administration)

## Common Compliance Pitfalls {#common-pitfalls}

These are the most common mistakes in SA rental agreements:

1. **Deposit exceeds 2x rent** — Unenforceable and constitutes an unfair practice
2. **No interest-bearing account clause** — Landlord is in breach of RHA from day one
3. **Notice period less than one calendar month** — Clause is void; one month applies regardless
4. **Penalty equal to remaining rent** — CPA requires reasonable penalty, not full outstanding rent
5. **No joint inspection clause** — Leads to deposit disputes; always include
6. **Waiver of Tribunal rights** — Any clause purporting to exclude Tribunal jurisdiction is void
7. **No escalation clause** — Not illegal, but leads to disputes; always specify
8. **Missing POPIA consent** — Information Regulator may intervene; always include
9. **Sub-letting without clarity** — Default is no sub-letting without consent; make explicit
10. **No domicilium clause** — Makes serving legal notices difficult; always include
