# POPIA Direct Marketing — s69 + Regulation 6

Unsolicited electronic direct marketing is **prohibited by default**. Two narrow gates allow it.

## 1. The rule (s69)

Processing PI for direct marketing by **electronic communication** (SMS, email, automated call, instant message) is **prohibited** unless:

- (a) The data subject has given **consent** — the prescribed **Form 4** (POPIA Regulations), OR
- (b) The data subject is an **existing customer** AND the strict conditions of s69(3) are met.

## 2. Consent route — Form 4 (s69(1)(a) + Reg 6)

- Consent must be sought **only once** per data subject (if refused, don't ask again).
- Must use the **prescribed Form 4** (or substantially similar, with all required info).
- Must be **specific** about the type of marketing and the identity of the marketer.
- Must be **informed** — explain what PI is processed and how.
- Must be **opt-in**, not pre-ticked.
- Must be **revocable** — include unsubscribe mechanism in every message.

Form 4 key contents:
1. Identity of the Responsible Party
2. Nature of the marketing
3. Statement that consent is voluntary
4. How to withdraw consent

## 3. Existing-customer route (s69(3))

You may market **without fresh consent** only if **all** of:

- (a) You obtained the contact details **in the context of the sale** of a product/service
- (b) You are marketing **your own similar products/services**
- (c) The data subject was given a **reasonable opportunity to object** at the time of collection **and in every subsequent communication**, free of charge and without undue effort

**Rental translation**: You can email an existing tenant about renewal offers, rent increases, new units in the same portfolio — but **not** about unrelated services (insurance, solar, moving services) without Form 4 consent.

## 4. What counts as "direct marketing"?

Defined broadly in s1:
> any approach... for the direct or indirect purpose of promoting or offering to supply, in the ordinary course of business, any goods or services to the data subject, or requesting the data subject to make a donation.

**Counts as marketing**:
- Newsletter with property listings
- "Refer a friend" campaigns
- Upsell (insurance, fibre, solar) to tenants
- WhatsApp broadcasts with promotions
- Automated calls or SMS

**Does NOT count as marketing** (operational/contractual):
- Invoices and statements
- Maintenance updates
- Renewal notifications (RHA / CPA s14 required)
- Lease compliance notices
- Security alerts
- Regulatory updates (e.g., load-shedding schedule)

## 5. Automated calls / robo-calls

Section 69 applies equally — no automated marketing calls without consent.

## 6. Channels beyond s69

- **Postal mail marketing**: Not covered by s69 — falls under general s11 (consent or legitimate interest).
- **In-person marketing at viewings**: General s11.
- **Social media ads targeting existing contacts**: Triggers s69 because the audience is derived from PI.

## 7. Cross-statute overlap

| Statute | Rule |
|---|---|
| **CPA s11, s12** | Right to restrict unwanted direct marketing; National Opt-Out Register. |
| **ECTA s45** | Electronic communications marketing — opt-out required, sender identity mandatory. |
| **Regulation of Interception of Communications Act (RICA)** | Recording calls for marketing QA requires consent. |

POPIA is the strictest regime — complying with s69 generally covers the others.

## 8. Regulator enforcement focus

The Information Regulator has issued guidance (2022) flagging:
- Pre-ticked consent checkboxes → invalid
- Blanket "marketing partners" clauses → invalid (must name each marketer)
- No unsubscribe link → automatic s69 breach
- SMS lists bought from third parties → rarely defensible

## 9. Founder quick-check

| Scenario | Allowed? |
|---|---|
| Emailing existing tenants about lease renewal | ✅ Operational, not marketing |
| Emailing existing tenants about a new property in your portfolio | ✅ Existing-customer exception (similar service) |
| SMSing applicants who were rejected with a promo offer | ❌ Not existing customers — needs Form 4 consent |
| Running a Facebook ad targeting your tenant email list | ⚠️ Triggers s69 — needs consent |
| WhatsApp broadcast to a "tenant news" group | ⚠️ Operational updates OK; any promotion needs consent |
| Selling tenant emails to a fibre provider | ❌ Unlawful without specific consent |
| Newsletter with monthly market stats + your listings | ⚠️ Marketing — needs Form 4 |
| Referral campaign: "refer a tenant, get R1,000" sent to existing tenants | ⚠️ Marketing — existing-customer exception is arguable; get Form 4 to be safe |

## 10. Practical template (consent wording) — for reference only

> I consent to [Klikk / Landlord name] sending me marketing communications by email and SMS about:
> ☐ New rental listings in my preferred areas
> ☐ Renewal offers and incentives
> ☐ Affiliated service providers (insurance, fibre, utilities)
> I understand I can withdraw this consent at any time by clicking "unsubscribe" or emailing privacy@klikk.co.za.
> Signed: _______ Date: _______

Do not bundle this with lease signature — marketing consent must be separate and revocable.
