# Tenant Personal Information — What POPIA Protects

Every category of PI a rental agent or landlord typically holds, classified by POPIA tier and sensitivity.

## Who is a "tenant data subject"?

POPIA protects **every identifiable, living natural person** — not only the lead tenant. In a rental context this extends to:

- Lead tenant
- Co-tenants / joint signatories
- Guarantors / sureties
- Spouse / life partner (if disclosed)
- Adult household members
- Children (minors) in the household
- Emergency contacts
- References (previous landlord, employer)

Each is a separate data subject with their own rights. Their PI must be handled with their own lawful basis.

## Protection tiers

| Tier | POPIA category | Examples | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Special PI (s26)** | Processing prohibited unless s27 exception | Race, religion, health, biometrics, sex life, criminal behaviour, trade union, political | Explicit consent or statutory authorisation |
| **Tier 2 — Children's Info (s34)** | Prohibited unless s35 exception | Any PI of a person under 18 | Competent-person consent (parent/guardian) |
| **Tier 3 — Account numbers (s107)** | Criminal offence if procured/disclosed unlawfully | Bank account, credit card, ID numbers | s107 offence — imprisonment up to 10 years |
| **Tier 4 — Ordinary PI (s11)** | Lawful with justification | Name, contact, employment | Lawful basis under s11 |

## 14. What is NOT protected

- **Purely household activity** (s6) — doesn't help commercial landlords
- **De-identified** data (s6) — aggregated analytics OK
- **Already public** data (s7) — but combining public + private data re-triggers POPIA
- **Deceased persons** — no longer data subjects (estate retains some access rights)

## 15. Third-party redaction — the most common DSAR mistake

When producing a tenant's file on DSAR, redact:
- Co-tenant IDs, bank accounts, payslips
- Guarantor details
- Previous landlord's personal contact info beyond what the tenant already knows
- Other tenants' names appearing in scheme-wide records
- Internal notes naming other tenants

Failure = breach of those third parties' PI → separate complaint + fine exposure.
