# POPIA Special Personal Information & Children — ss26–35

Two categories get **extra protection**: Special PI (s26) and Children's Info (s34). Both are prohibited by default; you need a statutory exception to process them.

## 1. Special Personal Information (s26)

**Section 26 prohibits processing** of PI concerning:

| Category | Rental examples |
|---|---|
| **Religion or philosophical beliefs** | "No work on Sabbath" requests, faith-based house rules |
| **Race or ethnic origin** | Demographic forms, BEE reporting |
| **Trade union membership** | Employer verification sometimes reveals this |
| **Political persuasion** | Never collect in rentals |
| **Health or sex life** | Disability accommodations, service animals, medical conditions affecting tenancy |
| **Biometric information** | Fingerprint or face access, signature image |
| **Criminal behaviour** — alleged offences or proceedings, sentence outcomes | Criminal record checks on applicants |

## 2. Exceptions allowing processing of Special PI (s27)

You may process special PI only if:

- (a) **Data subject consents** — must be explicit and specific
- (b) **Necessary for rights or obligations in law**
- (c) **Complies with international public law obligation**
- (d) **For historical, statistical or research purposes** (with safeguards)
- (e) **PI has been deliberately made public** by the data subject
- (f) **Provided in terms of ss28–33** (category-specific exceptions)

## 3. Category-specific exceptions (ss28–33)

| Section | Category | Key exception |
|---|---|---|
| s28 | Religion | Allowed for religious organisations about their members |
| s29 | Race / ethnicity | Allowed for compliance with employment equity / BEE laws |
| s30 | Trade union | Allowed for the union about its members |
| s31 | Political persuasion | Allowed for political parties about members/supporters |
| s32 | **Health** | Allowed by medical professionals, insurers, schools, employers — limited to purpose |
| s32 | **Sex life** | Very narrow — mostly medical / social work |
| s33 | **Criminal behaviour / biometrics** | Allowed for bodies charged by law, or with consent, or where necessary |

## 4. Biometric data in rentals (s33 + s26)

Face/fingerprint access control in complexes and estates is **widespread** and **high-risk** under POPIA.

**Required safeguards**:
1. **Explicit written consent** naming biometric processing specifically.
2. **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** — document why a less intrusive method (key card, PIN) won't work.
3. **Purpose limitation** — only for access; not for time-and-attendance, marketing, or behaviour profiling.
4. **Retention** — delete biometric template when tenancy ends.
5. **Security** — encrypted storage, no raw image retention, template only.
6. **Alternative offered** — tenant who refuses biometric must be given a working alternative.

**Regulator stance**: The Information Regulator has signalled biometric access in residential complexes is a **compliance priority** (see Enforcement Notices issued to security estates 2023–2024).

## 5. Criminal record checks on tenants (s33)

- Allowed with **specific consent** (s26(a)) + written POPIA consent form.
- Use accredited bureaus (e.g. MIE, LexisNexis Risk).
- **Purpose must be proportionate** — serious offences relevant to the tenancy (violence, fraud, drug offences), not historic petty matters.
- **Automated decisioning**: Refusing a tenant solely on a criminal hit triggers **s71** — must allow human review.
- **Retention**: Delete the check result once the tenancy decision is made; keep the fact of the decision, not the raw record.

## 6. Children's personal information (ss34–35)

Section 34 prohibits processing PI of a **child** — anyone under 18 years.

### Exceptions (s35)

Processing allowed if:

- (a) Done with **prior consent of a competent person** (parent/legal guardian)
- (b) **Necessary for establishing, exercising or defending a right or obligation in law**
- (c) **Complies with international public law**
- (d) **For historical/statistical/research** with appropriate safeguards
- (e) **PI has been deliberately made public** by the child (with competent-person consent)

### Rental implications

Common collection of children's PI in rentals:
- **Names and ages of minor household members** on the application (affordability, occupancy limits)
- **Photos** of family during inspections or marketing
- **Schools attended** (sometimes asked for relocation context)

**Founder rule**:
1. Collect only **name and age** — not school, ID, DoB, or photos, unless strictly needed.
2. Get **parent/guardian consent** in writing on the application form.
3. Do **not** use children's info for marketing, profiling, or sharing with third parties.
4. **Remove** children's info from inspection photos/videos where possible (blur faces).

## 7. Health information (s32)

- Generally only processable by **medical professionals** or with **consent**.
- Landlord exception: if a tenant discloses a **disability** for accommodation purposes (wheelchair ramp, service animal), you can process **only what's necessary** to provide the accommodation.
- **Do not record diagnosis details** — record only "accessibility requirement" as a flag.

## 8. Penalties for Special PI breaches

- **Administrative fine** up to **R10 million** (s109).
- **Criminal offence** under s107(a) — processing special PI without lawful basis → imprisonment up to **10 years** or fine.
- **Civil damages** under s99 — strict liability, no fault required.

## 9. Founder quick-check

| Scenario | Allowed? |
|---|---|
| Asking for race on application form | ❌ No — unless employment-equity reporting obligation applies |
| Asking "do you have pets" | ✅ Yes — not special PI |
| Asking if tenant has a service animal | ⚠️ Triggers health/disability — collect minimum only |
| Fingerprint access to complex | ⚠️ Special PI — needs consent, DPIA, alternative, minimum retention |
| Criminal background check on applicant | ⚠️ Special PI — needs explicit written consent + proportionate purpose |
| Keeping photos of tenant's children from inspections | ❌ Risk — blur faces or get parental consent |
| Asking religion for dietary/holiday reasons | ❌ No — not proportionate |
| Recording tenant's disability for ramp installation | ⚠️ Yes — minimum info only, no diagnosis |
