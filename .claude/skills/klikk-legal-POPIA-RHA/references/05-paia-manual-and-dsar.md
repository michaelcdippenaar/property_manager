# PAIA — Promotion of Access to Information Act 2 of 2000

Gives effect to Constitution s32 (access to information). Amended by POPIA in 2021 to fuse with data subject access rights.

## 1. Purpose

- Public access to records held by **public bodies** (s11).
- Private access to records held by **private bodies** (s50) — but **only** where the record is **required for the exercise or protection of any right**.
- Data subjects have **additional** rights under POPIA s23 (DSAR).

## 2. Who must comply

**All private bodies**, including sole proprietor landlords and agencies. The small-business exemption (previously carved out by the Minister) **lapsed on 30 June 2021**. Every rental business now needs a PAIA Manual.

## 3. Section 51 Manual — the cornerstone document

Every private body must **compile, maintain, and publish** a PAIA Manual containing:

1. **Contact details** of the head of the private body / Information Officer
2. **Guide on how to use PAIA** (reference to the SAHRC Guide)
3. **Notice under s52(2)** — categories of records automatically available
4. **Description of records held** — organised by subject and category
5. **Subjects on which the body holds records**
6. **Sufficient detail** to facilitate requests
7. **Other relevant information** (fees, forms, appeals)
8. Information on **POPIA** — purposes, categories of PI, recipients, retention, security (integrated per s17 POPIA)

**Where to publish**: Website (prominent link, e.g. footer "PAIA Manual"), and on paper at head office. Updated annually.

**Templates**: Available from the Information Regulator (`inforegulator.org.za`) and the SA Human Rights Commission (SAHRC).

## 4. Information Officer

- **Registered** via the Regulator's online portal.
- Receives PAIA requests (Form 2) and POPIA DSARs.
- In a one-person agency, this is the principal.

## 5. PAIA Forms

| Form | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Form 2** | Request for access (private body) — requester fills in |
| **Form 3** | Third-party notification — sent by IO to third parties whose PI is in the record |
| **Form 4** | Outcome of request (grant / refusal) — response to requester |
| **Form 5** | Internal appeal (public bodies only, not private) |

(Note: POPIA Regulations introduced additional forms — POPIA Form 1 objection, POPIA Form 2 correction, POPIA Form 3 third-party notification, POPIA Form 4 direct marketing consent. These are distinct from PAIA forms and sit under the POPIA Regulations.)

## 6. DSAR procedure — receiving and handling a request

### Step 1: Intake
- Request must be in writing on **Form 2** (or substantially similar).
- Check **identity** of requester (protects against impostors).
- Confirm the **right** for which access is sought (PAIA s50 requirement for private bodies).
- POPIA DSARs under s23 do not require showing a "right" — access to one's own PI is itself the right.

### Step 2: Acknowledge and log
- Acknowledge within a few days.
- Log in the **DSAR Register** (date, requester, scope, deadline).

### Step 3: Decide timeframe
- **30 days** from receipt to decide (PAIA s56).
- Extendable by **30 days** once, with notice (s57), for reasonable grounds (volume, third-party consultation).

### Step 4: Identify third parties and notify (s71)
- If record contains PI of third parties, send **Form 3** giving them **21 days** to object.
- Apply objections when deciding release.

### Step 5: Decide — grant, partially grant, or refuse

**Mandatory refusal grounds** (Chapter 4 Part 3):
| Section | Ground |
|---|---|
| s63 | Mandatory protection of third-party privacy |
| s64 | Mandatory protection of commercial information of third party |
| s65 | Mandatory protection of confidential information |
| s66 | Mandatory protection of safety of individuals / property |
| s67 | Mandatory protection of records privileged from production |
| s68 | Commercial information of the private body |
| s69 | Research information |

**Discretionary grant**: s70 — public interest override.

### Step 6: Send outcome (Form 4)
- Grant: provide the record in the form requested (electronic, copy, inspection).
- Refuse: give reasons citing the specific section relied on.
- Fees: Prescribed fees apply — request fee + access/reproduction fee (Regulation 11).

### Step 7: Appeal / Regulator complaint
- No internal appeal for private bodies (s74 applies to public bodies).
- Requester can complain to the Information Regulator under POPIA s74, or approach the High Court under PAIA s78.

## 7. POPIA s23 DSAR — the main everyday request

Data subjects can request:
- Whether their PI is held
- A record or description of the PI
- Identity of third parties who have or have had access
- **Free of charge** for the fact of holding; **reasonable fee** for copies

Timeframe: **reasonable time** (POPIA does not prescribe 30 days like PAIA, but Regulator practice is 30 days).

## 8. Fees (PAIA Regulation 11)

| Fee | Amount (approximate, per GN 187 of 2021) |
|---|---|
| Request fee | R50 (private body) |
| Access fee — per A4 page | ~R1.10 |
| Copies of computer-readable records | Actual cost |
| Search and preparation | ~R30 per hour |
| Travel / postage | Actual |

## 9. Common rental DSAR scenarios

| Scenario | Response |
|---|---|
| Ex-tenant requests "everything you hold about me" | Full file — application, lease, payments, inspections, correspondence — with third-party redaction |
| Tenant requests credit check report | Provide the report; note bureau data + dispute rights |
| Tenant requests WhatsApp chat history | In-scope PI — must include |
| Tenant requests internal notes/"tenant rating" | In-scope — draft internal notes as if subject will read them |
| Landlord requests sub-agent's file on their property | In-scope for landlord's own PI; redact tenant PI without tenant consent |
| Third party requests tenant's info | Refusal under s63 unless requester shows a right (e.g., attorney with subpoena) |

## 10. Record-keeping

Maintain:
- **DSAR Register** — every request, status, outcome
- **Refusal log** — with sections relied on (defensible at Regulator)
- **Third-party notification log** — Form 3s sent, responses received
- **Extensions log** — with reasons
