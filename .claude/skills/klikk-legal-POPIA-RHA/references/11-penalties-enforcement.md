# Penalties & Enforcement — POPIA, PAIA & RHA

## 1. POPIA — who enforces

**Information Regulator of South Africa** (est. under s39 POPIA).

- Website: `inforegulator.org.za`
- Address: JD House, 27 Stiemens Street, Braamfontein, Johannesburg
- Chair: Adv. Pansy Tlakula

### Powers (Chapter 5)

- Receive complaints (POPIA s74)
- Conduct investigations (s80)
- Issue **Enforcement Notices** (s95) requiring corrective action
- Impose **Administrative Fines** (s109) up to **R10 million**
- Refer matters for **criminal prosecution** (s107)
- Approve codes of conduct (s60)
- Publish findings + guidance

## 2. POPIA — penalties

### Administrative fines (s109)

Up to **R10 million** per breach, considering:
- Nature, gravity, duration
- Whether intentional or negligent
- Prior breaches
- Degree of co-operation
- Categories of PI affected
- Mitigation steps

### Criminal offences (s100–106 + s107)

| Section | Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|---|
| s100 | Obstructing the Regulator | Up to R10m fine / 10 years imprisonment |
| s101 | Breach of confidentiality | Up to R10m / 10 years |
| s102 | Obstructing execution of warrant | Up to R10m / 10 years |
| s103 | Failure to comply with enforcement notice | Up to R10m / 10 years |
| s104 | Offences by witnesses | Fine / 12 months |
| s105 | Unlawful acts re account numbers | Up to R10m / 10 years |
| s106 | Unlawful acts re account numbers (others) | Up to R1m / 12 months |
| s107 | General offence (catch-all) | Up to R10m / 10 years |

**s107 is the one that bites**: unlawful processing of **special PI** or **account numbers** is a specific criminal offence.

### Civil liability (s99)

- Data subjects can **sue the Responsible Party** for damages in High Court.
- **Strict liability** — no fault required, only causation.
- Damages may include: patrimonial loss, aggravated damages for contumelia, costs, plus **punitive damages** where gross disregard is shown.

### Regulator enforcement to date (illustrative)

- **Dis-Chem fine** (2022) — R500,000 for failure to comply with Enforcement Notice over a breach notification delay.
- **Department of Justice** — R5 million Enforcement Notice for ransomware breach.
- Real-estate sector flagged as **high-priority** by Regulator in 2024 compliance plan.

## 3. PAIA — penalties

PAIA has its own offences (s90):
- Destroying, altering, concealing records to frustrate a request: **up to R10,000 / 2 years imprisonment**
- Information Officer failing to comply with PAIA duties: administrative consequences + civil remedies

Regulator now handles PAIA enforcement alongside POPIA (since 2021).

## 4. RHA — penalties & Tribunal enforcement

### Tribunal rulings (s13)

Tribunal rulings have the effect of a **Magistrate's Court order** (s13(12)) — directly enforceable by sheriff.

### Fines & imprisonment for RHA contraventions (s16)

Offences include:
- Failing to comply with a Tribunal ruling
- Obstructing Tribunal investigations
- Making false statements to Tribunal
- Unfair practices where regulations criminalise conduct

**Penalty**: Fine up to **R10,000** and/or imprisonment up to **2 years** (s16).

### Common Tribunal orders

| Order | Example |
|---|---|
| Refund of deposit + interest | R25,000 + interest |
| Repairs within 30 days | Specific performance |
| Reinstate tenant after lockout | Mandamus |
| Pay arrear rent | Judgment for amount |
| Stop harassment / interference | Interdict |
| Cease blacklisting without cause | Order of restoration |

### Civil alternative

Parties can bypass Tribunal and sue in Magistrate's Court directly — slower, more expensive, but allows general damages.

## 5. Unlawful eviction — a separate tort

Evicting a tenant without a **court order** is:
- A **criminal offence** under PIE s8
- A civil wrong (spoliation) — remedy: **mandament van spolie** (immediate restoration)
- An **unfair practice** under RHA s4A
- Potentially a breach of Constitution s26 (right to housing)

Penalty under PIE s8: fine / imprisonment up to **2 years**, and restoration.

## 6. FICA — penalties

- Non-compliance with CDD or record-keeping: administrative sanctions up to **R10 million** (FIC Act s45C–E)
- Criminal offences: imprisonment up to **15 years**

FIC and Information Regulator share jurisdiction where POPIA-FICA overlaps.

## 7. NCA — penalties for unlawful credit checks

- NCR (National Credit Regulator) can fine up to **10% of annual turnover** for systemic breaches.
- Individual complaints resolved via NCR / Credit Industry Tribunal.

## 8. Risk tiers — how founders should prioritise

| Tier | Risk | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Criminal (10y imprisonment)** | POPIA s107 | Unlawful processing of special PI (biometrics without consent), unlawful use of account numbers |
| **Major fine (R10m)** | POPIA s109, FIC | Ignored Enforcement Notice, major breach without notification |
| **Civil liability (open-ended)** | POPIA s99 | Class-action-style tenant claim after breach |
| **Tribunal orders** | RHA s4A | Deposit refund, lockout restoration, habitability orders |
| **Court orders + costs** | PIE, CPA | Unlawful eviction, unfair contract terms |
| **Reputational** | Media + Regulator publication | All of the above |

## 9. Mitigating factors

Things that reduce exposure if an incident occurs:

- Registered Information Officer
- Published PAIA Manual
- Current RoPA + retention schedule
- Evidence of training
- Signed operator DPAs
- Prompt breach notification (within 72 hours)
- Co-operation with Regulator
- Remediation plan in place

## 10. Founder priority list

If resources are limited, fix these first — they give the biggest risk reduction per hour of work:

1. **Register IO + publish PAIA Manual** — absence is prima facie non-compliance.
2. **Privacy notice at every collection point** — eliminates half of likely complaints.
3. **Signed DPAs with credit bureaux, cloud, and managing agent** — without these, s21 breach is presumed.
4. **Deposit interest-bearing account + monthly interest statement** — the most common Tribunal complaint.
5. **Joint inspections + signed reports** — essential for defending deposit deductions.
6. **Breach response plan + template letters** — readiness cuts 72-hour risk.
7. **Special PI consent for biometric access / criminal checks** — s107 criminal risk if skipped.
8. **CPA s14 renewal notification workflow** — stops auto-continuation and leasing disputes.
