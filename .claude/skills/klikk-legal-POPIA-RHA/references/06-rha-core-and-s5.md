# Rental Housing Act — RHA 50 of 1999 (as amended by Act 35 of 2014)

> **Citation grounding.** Authoritative reference for which RHA sub-section we cite for each concept is `content/cto/rha-citation-canonical-map.md`. Sub-section letters for the deposit interest-bearing account and the 7 / 14 / 21-day refund deadlines are flagged LOW-confidence and require SA-admitted-attorney sign-off before any externally-facing lease text relies on them.

The primary statute for residential rentals in SA. Provincial **Rental Housing Tribunals** are established under **s7**, with powers in **s13**. The Act defines landlord and tenant duties, and regulates deposits, inspections, and written leases.

**Amendment Act 35 of 2014** introduced **s4A** (unfair practices) and tightened s5. Commencement of some s4A provisions was staggered — as of 2025 most are in force.

## 1. Scope

- Applies to **residential leases** in SA.
- Does **not** apply to commercial leases, hotels, hospitals, student residences run as institutions.
- Applies to **both written and oral** leases, but s5(2) creates a right to a written lease.

## 2. Section 4 — General principles

| s4(1) | No discrimination on grounds of race, gender, sex, pregnancy, marital status, sexual orientation, ethnicity, colour, age, disability, religion, conscience, belief, culture, language, birth |
| s4(2) | Tenant's right to privacy — no arbitrary search, no seizure of property |
| s4(3) | Prohibits unfair discrimination by landlord in leasing practices |
| s4(5) | Landlord must provide a dwelling in habitable condition |

## 3. Section 4A — Unfair practices (see separate reference)

See `07-rha-s4a-unfair-practices.md` for detail. Section 4A lists practices deemed automatically unfair — including:
- Withholding a deposit without justification
- Arbitrary eviction
- Failure to maintain habitability
- Charging unauthorised fees

## 4. Section 5 — Lease provisions

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| (a) | Names of tenant and landlord, and their addresses for legal service |
| (b) | Description of the dwelling |
| (c) | Rental amount and reasonable escalation |
| (d) | Term of the lease, or if indefinite, notice period |
| (e) | Deposit amount (if any) |
| (f) ⚠️ LOW-confidence | Obligation to invest deposit in an interest-bearing account, with interest rate at least equivalent to standard savings account (sub-section letter pending lawyer sign-off — see canonical map) |
| (g) | Date on which rental is due |

| (i) | Rules of the house in writing (annexed) |
| (j) | Amount, purpose, and refund terms of the deposit |

### s5(4) — Plain language
Lease must be in plain, understandable language (complements CPA s22).

### s5(5) — Notice period
If no period is specified, either party may cancel on **not less than one month's written notice**, given at least one calendar month in advance.

### s5(6) — Renewal / continuation
On expiry of a lease, if the tenant remains in occupation with the landlord's express or tacit consent, the lease continues on the same terms (subject to CPA s14 for fixed-term consumer leases).

## 5. Section 5(3)(f)–(i) — Deposit rules

### The deposit must:
- Be placed in an **interest-bearing account** with a financial institution
- Earn interest at a rate **not less than a standard savings account**
- **Interest accrues for the tenant**
- Landlord must, on written request, provide **proof of the interest accrued**

### Refund timelines ⚠️ LOW-confidence sub-section letters — pending lawyer sign-off

| Scenario | Deadline |
|---|---|
| No damage, lease terminated normally, joint inspection done | **7 days** after expiration of the lease |
| Damage exists — repairs required | **14 days** after repairs completed, against written repair invoices |
| Tenant failed to attend outgoing inspection | **21 days** from expiration of the lease |

> The deadlines themselves (7 / 14 / 21 days) are well established. The specific RHA s5(3) sub-section letters that house each deadline are pending SA-admitted-attorney verification — see `content/cto/rha-citation-canonical-map.md` items #17–19.

### Inspections (s5(3)(k))
- **Ingoing joint inspection** — within 7 days before occupation
- **Outgoing joint inspection** — within 3 days before expiration (or at expiration)
- **Consequence of no joint inspection by landlord**: Landlord forfeits the right to deduct damages. The deposit plus interest must be refunded on termination.

## 6. Section 5A — Duties of the landlord

Added by Act 35 of 2014. Landlord must:

| (a) | Provide a receipt for rent on request |
| (b) | Maintain the dwelling in a habitable condition |
| (c) | Comply with health, safety, building, zoning, and housing laws |
| (d) | Make emergency repairs without delay |
| (e) | Not interfere with the tenant's right to use and enjoy the property |
| (f) | Ensure access rights for inspection are exercised reasonably |

## 7. Section 5B — Duties of the tenant

| (a) | Pay rental on the due date |
| (b) | Keep the dwelling clean and hygienic |
| (c) | Notify the landlord of any faults needing repair |
| (d) | Comply with house rules |
| (e) | Upon termination, restore the dwelling in the same condition (fair wear and tear excepted) |

## 8. Deposit cap — current position

**The RHA does not cap the deposit amount.** The "2× monthly rent" convention is industry custom, not statute.

Some provincial Tribunals have found deposits > 2× rent to be **unfair practice** under s4A where disproportionate. Standard practice: **1–2 months' rent**.

## 9. Eviction

RHA does not govern eviction — that falls under the **Prevention of Illegal Eviction from and Unlawful Occupation of Land Act 19 of 1998 (PIE)**. See `klikk-rental-master` for the PIE process.

Landlord may **never**:
- Lock out the tenant
- Disconnect utilities
- Remove the tenant's possessions
- Use force

All evictions require a **court order** under PIE.

## 10. Habitability standard (s4(5) + s5A)

Landlord must provide a dwelling that:
- Is structurally sound
- Has working plumbing, water, electricity connections
- Is free of health hazards
- Complies with municipal bylaws and building regulations

**Tenant remedies** for uninhabitable conditions:
- Refer to the Tribunal under s4A
- Apply for rent reduction / abatement
- In extreme cases, cancel the lease for material breach

## 11. Rent escalation

RHA is silent on escalation caps. Standard market escalation is **6–10%** per annum. Escalation must be agreed in writing under s5(3)(c) and cannot be unilaterally imposed.

The **Consumer Protection Act s48** (unreasonable terms) and s14 (fixed-term cancellation) apply where tenant is a natural person.

## 12. Prescription / limitation

- Breach-of-lease claims: **3 years** (Prescription Act 68 of 1969).
- Tribunal complaints: No hard limit, but stale complaints may be dismissed.
