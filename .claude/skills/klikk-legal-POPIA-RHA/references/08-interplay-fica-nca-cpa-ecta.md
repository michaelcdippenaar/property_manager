# Interplay — FICA, NCA, CPA, ECTA, RICA, Prescription Act

POPIA and the RHA do not operate in isolation. A rental business touches half a dozen statutes simultaneously. This reference maps the overlaps.

## 1. FICA — Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001

### What it requires
- Agents who **manage trust funds** (deposits, rent) are **accountable institutions** under Schedule 1 Item 19.
- Must do **Customer Due Diligence (CDD)** — identify and verify tenant and landlord using ID + proof of address.
- Keep CDD records for **5 years** after the relationship ends.

### Interplay with POPIA
- FICA **requires** processing of PI → lawful basis under POPIA s11(1)(c) (legal obligation).
- FICA retention (**5 years**) overrides POPIA s14 minimisation for that period.
- Must still meet POPIA s13 purpose specification — inform tenant that FICA is the basis.

### Founder practice
- Collect ID + address proof **once** at onboarding, store securely, delete 5 years after tenancy ends.
- Do **not** re-use FICA docs for marketing or third-party sharing.

## 2. NCA — National Credit Act 34 of 2005

### When it applies to rentals
- Rentals are **generally not** credit agreements under the NCA.
- But **deferred rent plans, instalments on arrears, or rent-to-buy** may qualify as credit agreements.
- Credit bureau checks are governed by **NCA ss68–72** + the **Credit Industry Code**.

### Interplay with POPIA
- Tenant must give **written consent** for credit bureau pull (NCA s68).
- Consent must be **specific** — cannot be bundled with generic "we may run checks."
- POPIA s11(1)(a) consent + NCA s68 consent typically combined in one form.

### Founder practice
- Separate consent line on application form: "I consent to a credit check via [TPN / Experian / XDS]."
- Keep the credit report only as long as needed for the decision; destroy after decision made.
- Inform the applicant of the outcome and their right to dispute under NCA s72.

## 3. CPA — Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008

### When it applies
- Applies where the tenant is a **natural person** and the landlord is operating in the **ordinary course of business**.
- Small individual landlords may be outside the CPA; agencies and portfolio landlords are **in**.

### Key sections for rentals

| Section | Rule |
|---|---|
| **s14** | Fixed-term consumer agreements — max 24 months (unless demonstrable financial benefit); **20 business days** to cancel on material breach; **40–80 business days** before expiry — landlord must notify of expiry + renewal terms |
| **s22** | Plain language — lease must be understandable to average consumer |
| **s48** | Unfair, unreasonable, or unjust contract terms prohibited |
| **s49** | Specific attention drawn to unusual terms (penalty, indemnity, exclusion) |
| **s51** | Prohibits waivers of consumer rights |

### Interplay with RHA
- s5(4) RHA and s22 CPA together require **plain language leases**.
- CPA s14 complements RHA s5(5) on notice periods.
- Termination for breach: 20 business days notice under CPA s14(1)(b).

### Founder practice
- Send CPA s14 renewal notification **40–80 business days** before lease expiry — usually around month 8–9 of a 12-month lease.
- Highlight and initial any clauses on penalties, deposits, or limitations.
- Keep leases under 15 pages, in clear SA English.

## 4. ECTA — Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002

### What it allows
- **Electronic signatures** on leases (s13) — valid if demonstrably linked to the signatory and reliable.
- **Advanced electronic signatures** (AES) for specific regulated transactions — not required for residential leases.
- Electronic transmission, storage, and retention of records.

### Interplay with RHA
- RHA requires the lease to be in writing on request — "writing" includes electronic per ECTA.
- ECTA s13(3): electronic signature effective if:
  - Method identifies signatory
  - Indicates their approval
  - Is reliable in context

### Interplay with POPIA
- E-sign audit trail (IP, timestamp, device) is **PI** → secure per s19.
- Signer consent to the e-sign process should be recorded.

### Founder practice
- Klikk's native signing via Gotenberg + signed audit PDFs satisfies ECTA s13 — retain the audit trail.
- For esign disputes, produce the audit PDF, sign-in logs, and OTP/SMS verification.

## 5. RICA — Regulation of Interception of Communications Act 70 of 2002

### Relevance
- Recording calls (e.g., maintenance calls, sales calls to prospects) requires **at least one-party consent**.
- Monitoring tenant communications without consent → unlawful interception.

### Founder practice
- Add to outgoing line: "This call may be recorded for quality and training."
- Do not monitor tenant WhatsApp, email, or voice communications on property.

## 6. Prescription Act 68 of 1969

- **3-year prescription** for contractual claims (unpaid rent, damages).
- Interrupted by written acknowledgement or legal action.
- Sets natural upper bound for record retention of dispute-relevant documents (plus a buffer).

## 7. Sectional Titles Schemes Management Act (STSMA) + CSOS Act

### Relevance
- If the property is in a **sectional title scheme**, body corporate **conduct rules** bind the tenant.
- **Community Schemes Ombud Service (CSOS)** handles disputes.

### Founder practice
- Annex body corporate conduct rules to the lease (RHA s5(3)(i)).
- Tenant's PI may need to be shared with managing agent of the scheme → POPIA s11 + written operator agreement.

## 8. PIE Act — Prevention of Illegal Eviction 19 of 1998

### Relevance
- Eviction requires a **Magistrate's Court order**.
- Applies even after lease termination — once a tenant is an "unlawful occupier," PIE protects them.
- Landlord gives notice, tenant responds, court considers all relevant circumstances including vulnerability.

### Interplay with POPIA
- Sharing tenant info with attorneys / sheriff requires lawful basis (s11(1)(f) legitimate interest) — document in the privacy notice.

## 9. SARS / Income Tax Act

- Rental income is taxable; expenses deductible (s11(a)).
- Records retained **5 years** (Income Tax Act s29).
- POPIA s11(1)(c) legal obligation basis.

## 10. Cross-statute retention matrix

| Record | Statute | Period |
|---|---|---|
| FICA CDD records | FICA s42 | 5 years after relationship ends |
| Tax records | Tax Admin Act | 5 years |
| Lease agreement | RHA / Prescription | 3 years after termination (minimum), 5 years recommended |
| Deposit ledger | RHA | 3 years after refund |
| Inspection reports | RHA | 3 years after termination |
| Credit bureau reports | NCA / POPIA | Only for decision; destroy promptly |
| Rent receipts | RHA / SARS | 5 years |
| POPIA consent forms | POPIA | Duration of relationship + 3 years (evidentiary) |

**Rule**: Apply the **longest** applicable retention period, then delete.

## 11. Founder quick-map

| Situation | Primary law | Secondary laws |
|---|---|---|
| Tenant screening | POPIA + NCA | FICA |
| Lease drafting | RHA + CPA | ECTA (e-sign) |
| Deposit handling | RHA s5(3)(f) | FICA (if trust a/c) |
| Rent collection | RHA + CPA | SARS |
| Utility disconnection dispute | RHA s4A | Municipal bylaws |
| Eviction | PIE | RHA (breach basis) |
| Marketing to past tenants | POPIA s69 | CPA s11–12 |
| Cross-border cloud storage | POPIA s72 | Sectoral (none) |
| Body corporate dispute | STSMA / CSOS | RHA (if affects lease) |
| Tenant DSAR | POPIA s23 + PAIA | RHA (records to produce) |
