# Common Lease Addendums

Standard addendums used in South African residential lease agreements. Each addendum is attached as an annexure to the main lease and signed by both parties.

---

## 1. Pet Addendum

**When to use**: Whenever the landlord permits pets on the property.

### Key Clauses

| Clause | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Pets permitted** | Specify species, breed, size, and number (e.g., "1 small dog under 10kg") |
| **Pet deposit** | Separate refundable deposit (typically 1 month's rent) for pet-related damage |
| **Body corporate rules** | If sectional title, BC conduct rules on pets override lease terms |
| **Vaccination and sterilisation** | Require proof of up-to-date vaccinations; may require sterilisation |
| **Control** | Pets must be on leash and under direct control outside the unit |
| **Nuisance clause** | Excessive barking, aggression, or complaints = landlord may require pet removal |
| **Damage liability** | Tenant liable for all pet-related damage (carpets, doors, garden, fencing) |
| **Removal on breach** | If pet causes persistent nuisance or damage, landlord may require removal with written notice |
| **No breeding** | Commercial breeding typically prohibited in residential properties |
| **Insurance** | Tenant may be required to carry liability insurance for the pet |

**Note**: Pet deposit is separate from the security deposit and has the same interest-bearing and refund requirements.

## 2. Garden Maintenance Addendum

**When to use**: Properties with private gardens, especially stand-alone houses or ground-floor units with exclusive use gardens.

### Key Clauses

| Clause | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Maintenance responsibility** | Tenant responsible for: mowing, edging, weeding, watering, general upkeep |
| **Landlord's contractor** | Alternative: landlord arranges gardener at tenant's cost (specify monthly amount) |
| **Watering** | Tenant must water regularly; comply with any municipal water restrictions |
| **Landscaping changes** | No removal, alteration, or planting of trees/shrubs without written landlord consent |
| **Tree trimming** | Minor pruning: tenant; major tree work (above roof line, affecting structure): landlord |
| **Irrigation system** | Tenant responsible for routine use; landlord responsible for system repairs |
| **Chemicals/fertiliser** | Tenant may apply standard garden products; no herbicides near trees without consent |
| **Condition at vacating** | Garden must be in reasonable condition consistent with seasonal expectations |
| **Inspection** | Landlord may inspect garden condition with reasonable notice (24 hours minimum) |

**Water restriction note**: In drought-affected areas (Western Cape, parts of Gauteng), include specific clauses about compliance with municipal water restrictions and responsibility for fines.

## 3. Pool/Jacuzzi Maintenance Addendum

**When to use**: Properties with swimming pools, jacuzzis, or hot tubs.

### Key Clauses

| Clause | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Chemical maintenance** | Tenant responsible for regular chemical treatment (chlorine, pH balance, algaecide) |
| **Cleaning** | Tenant responsible for: skimming, vacuuming, filter cleaning, basket emptying |
| **Equipment** | Landlord provides: pool pump, filter, creepy-crawly. Landlord repairs/replaces equipment failures |
| **Electrical costs** | Pool pump electricity cost borne by tenant (utility consumption) |
| **Safety compliance** | Pool must comply with National Building Regulations (pool fencing, self-closing gate) |
| **Safety responsibility** | Landlord ensures compliant fencing; tenant must not remove or disable safety barriers |
| **Winterising** | Tenant responsible for winter maintenance or covering as directed |
| **Damage** | Tenant liable for damage from neglect (green pool requiring acid wash, pump damage from no water) |
| **Liability** | Landlord not liable for accidents; tenant responsible for supervising guests/children |
| **Professional service** | If pool requires professional maintenance, specify who arranges and pays |

**Safety note**: National Building Regulations require pool fencing with self-closing, self-latching gates for pools deeper than 300mm. Non-compliance = landlord's liability.

## 4. Parking Addendum

**When to use**: Properties with allocated parking bays, especially in sectional title complexes.

### Key Clauses

| Clause | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Allocated bay(s)** | Specify bay number(s), location (basement level, carport, open), exclusive use area reference |
| **Number of vehicles** | Specify maximum vehicles permitted |
| **Vehicle type** | Residential vehicles only; no commercial vehicles, caravans, trailers, or boats (unless agreed) |
| **Visitor parking** | Rules for guest parking (if available); no overnight parking in visitor bays |
| **Mechanical work** | No vehicle repairs, oil changes, or car washing in parking areas |
| **Storage** | No storage of items in parking bays beyond the vehicle |
| **Security** | Security measures available (boom gates, CCTV); landlord not liable for theft or damage |
| **Additional bays** | If additional parking is available at extra cost, specify terms |
| **Body corporate rules** | Sectional title schemes may have additional parking rules — include by reference |

## 5. Home Office Addendum

**When to use**: Tenant operates a business or works from home within the rental property.

### Key Clauses

| Clause | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Permitted use** | Specify type of business/work (e.g., "professional consulting, no walk-in clients") |
| **Zoning compliance** | Tenant responsible for ensuring compliance with municipal zoning bylaws |
| **Client visits** | Specify whether clients may visit the property; limit frequency if permitted |
| **Signage** | No external business signage unless approved by landlord (and body corporate if sectional title) |
| **Additional utilities** | Tenant bears any increased utility costs from business use |
| **Insurance** | Tenant must carry own business insurance; landlord's insurance does not cover business equipment or liability |
| **Nuisance** | Business must not cause noise, increased traffic, or disturbance to neighbours |
| **Employees** | Specify whether employees may work from the property |
| **Subletting prohibition** | Home office does not permit subletting rooms as office space to third parties |
| **Tax implications** | Tenant responsible for own tax affairs regarding home office deductions |

**Zoning note**: Many residential zones in SA municipalities allow home offices for professional services without rezoning, but manufacturing, retail, or high-traffic businesses may require consent use or rezoning approval.

## 6. Furnished Property Addendum

**When to use**: Properties let with furniture, appliances, or other landlord-provided contents.

### Key Clauses

| Clause | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Inventory schedule** | Detailed list of every item: description, brand/model, condition, quantity, estimated value |
| **Condition at move-in** | Photographs of each item; signed inventory by both parties |
| **Tenant obligations** | Use items with reasonable care; report damage immediately |
| **Replacement liability** | Tenant liable for replacement at current market value if damaged beyond repair |
| **Fair wear and tear** | Normal depreciation is landlord's cost (mattress wear, faded upholstery from sunlight) |
| **No removal** | Tenant may not remove furnished items from the property |
| **Landlord's insurance** | Landlord insures contents against fire, theft, natural disaster |
| **Tenant's insurance** | Tenant insures own personal belongings |
| **Maintenance** | Appliance servicing: landlord (major repairs, breakdown); tenant (cleaning, filters, minor care) |
| **Return condition** | Items must be returned in the same condition as received, fair wear and tear excepted |
| **Inspection** | Inventory checked against original at move-out; differences documented |

**Depreciation**: Landlords can claim wear-and-tear deductions on furnished items for tax purposes (see `07-rental-income-tax.md`).

## 7. Shared Accommodation Addendum

**When to use**: Houses or large units let to multiple tenants sharing common areas.

### Key Clauses

| Clause | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Individual room allocation** | Specify which room is allocated to each tenant |
| **Common area responsibilities** | Kitchen, bathroom, lounge cleaning schedule or shared responsibility |
| **Utility splitting** | How utilities are split (equal shares, sub-metered, included in rent) |
| **Guest policy** | Overnight guest limits and notification requirements |
| **Noise** | Quiet hours and mutual respect provisions |
| **Personal belongings** | Each tenant responsible for their own items; no communal claims |
| **Joint and several liability** | If all tenants on one lease, each is liable for the full rental amount |
| **Individual leases** | Alternative: each tenant signs a separate lease for their room |
| **Notice** | Each tenant's notice period and replacement procedure |
| **Conflict resolution** | Internal dispute mechanism before escalating to landlord |
| **Security deposit** | Per-person deposit or shared deposit (specify clearly) |

---

## General Addendum Rules

1. **All addendums must be signed** by both landlord and tenant to be enforceable
2. **Attach as annexures** to the main lease — reference the addendum in the lease body
3. **Number and date** each addendum
4. **Consistent with main lease** — if there's a conflict, specify which document prevails (typically the addendum as it's more specific)
5. **Body corporate compliance** — in sectional title, addendum terms cannot override conduct rules
6. **Plain language** — CPA s22 applies to addendums just as it does to the main lease
