# Body Corporate and Sectional Title

Rules governing rental properties in sectional title schemes, HOAs, and community schemes in South Africa.

---

## Key Legislation

| Act | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Sectional Titles Schemes Management Act 8 of 2011 (STSMA)** | Governs body corporate operations, levies, rules, and management |
| **Sectional Titles Act 95 of 1986** | Defines sectional title ownership, common property, exclusive use areas |
| **Community Schemes Ombud Service Act 9 of 2011 (CSOS Act)** | Dispute resolution for community scheme governance disputes |

---

## Body Corporate vs HOA

| Feature | Body Corporate (Sectional Title) | HOA (Freehold/Estate) |
|---|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | STSMA — statutory body, automatically created | Constitution — voluntary association (may be compulsory per title deed) |
| **Ownership** | Individual sections + undivided share in common property | Full freehold ownership of stand |
| **Levies** | Mandatory — statutory obligation | Usually mandatory per HOA constitution or title deed condition |
| **Rules** | Management rules + conduct rules (prescribed by STSMA) | HOA constitution and rules |
| **Dispute resolution** | CSOS | CSOS (if registered community scheme) or courts |

---

## Levies

### Who Pays
- **Owner (landlord) is liable** for all levies — this is a statutory obligation under STSMA
- Tenant is NOT directly liable to the body corporate for levies
- Lease may require tenant to reimburse levy costs to landlord, but the landlord remains liable to the body corporate

### Levy Components
| Component | Covers |
|---|---|
| Monthly levy | Common area maintenance, insurance (sectional title policy), management fees, reserve fund |
| Special levy | Major repairs, improvements, or unforeseen expenses — approved at general meeting |
| Exclusive use levy | Additional charge for exclusive use areas (if applicable) |

### Non-Payment
- Body corporate can institute legal action against the **owner** for arrears
- Can obtain judgment and attach the unit
- Interest accrues on overdue levies
- **CSOS power**: Can order tenant to pay rent directly to body corporate to clear owner's levy arrears

## Rules That Affect Tenants

### Conduct Rules (STSMA Prescribed Management Rule 68)
Tenants are bound by conduct rules, which typically cover:

| Rule area | Common provisions |
|---|---|
| **Noise** | Quiet hours (typically 22:00–06:00), restrictions on loud music, construction noise |
| **Pets** | May prohibit or restrict pets by type, size, number; may require written trustee approval |
| **Parking** | Designated bays, visitor parking, no commercial vehicles, no car washing in parking areas |
| **Common areas** | Pool hours, gym rules, braai area bookings, laundry facilities |
| **Waste** | Designated disposal areas, recycling requirements, no dumping in common areas |
| **Appearance** | Balcony restrictions (no laundry hanging), window treatments, signage |
| **Security** | Gate/access procedures, visitor registration, key/remote management |
| **Smoking** | Designated areas, may prohibit in units if smoke affects neighbours |

### Key Rule: Body Corporate Rules Override Lease
If the lease allows pets but body corporate conduct rules prohibit them, **the conduct rules prevail**. Landlords must ensure lease terms are consistent with scheme rules.

### Management Rules
Govern body corporate operations — voting, meetings, finances, insurance. Owners participate, not tenants.

## Exclusive Use Areas

Defined in the sectional plan and allocated to specific units:

| Type | Examples | Who maintains |
|---|---|---|
| Garden (exclusive use) | Private garden attached to ground-floor unit | Tenant (per lease), but structural elements are body corporate |
| Parking bay | Numbered bay in basement or carport | Body corporate maintains structure; tenant keeps clean |
| Storeroom | Allocated storage unit | Body corporate maintains structure |
| Balcony/patio | Private outdoor space | Tenant maintains; structural repairs by body corporate |

**Important**: Exclusive use areas remain **common property** — the owner has exclusive rights to use them, but they are not part of the section.

## Alterations in Sectional Title

Any alteration requires **two approvals**:

1. **Landlord's written consent** (as per lease)
2. **Body corporate approval** — trustees' written consent for minor alterations; special resolution at general meeting for alterations affecting common property

Examples:
- Installing a satellite dish → trustee approval needed
- Tiling a balcony → may need special resolution (affects common property)
- Adding security gates → trustee approval needed
- Painting exterior → must conform to scheme's colour scheme

## CSOS — Community Schemes Ombud Service

### Jurisdiction
Disputes involving:
- Body corporate governance and operations
- Trustee conduct and duties
- Levy disputes between owners and body corporate
- Management rule interpretation
- Common property use and maintenance
- Financial management of the scheme

### NOT in CSOS Jurisdiction
- Landlord-tenant disputes → **Rental Housing Tribunal**
- Evictions → **PIE Act court application**

### Process
1. **Application**: File with CSOS (online or paper)
2. **Conciliation**: CSOS attempts to mediate
3. **Adjudication**: If conciliation fails, formal adjudication
4. **Order**: Binding adjudication order issued
5. **Enforcement**: Non-compliance → Magistrate's Court enforcement

### Fees
- Application fee: R50 (individual) / R100 (body corporate)
- Significantly cheaper than court action

## Landlord Obligations in Sectional Title

| Obligation | Detail |
|---|---|
| Pay levies on time | Statutory obligation; cannot pass liability to tenant vis-à-vis body corporate |
| Ensure tenant compliance | Responsible for tenant's adherence to conduct rules |
| Attend meetings or appoint proxy | Vote on special levies, rule changes, management appointments |
| Maintain exclusive use areas | Or delegate to tenant via lease |
| Insure contents | Sectional title policy covers structure and common property, NOT contents |
| Notify body corporate of tenancy | Some schemes require landlord to register tenant details with managing agent |

---

## Insurance in Sectional Title

| What | Covered by | Who pays |
|---|---|---|
| Building structure | Sectional title insurance policy (body corporate) | Body corporate (via levies) |
| Common property | Same policy | Body corporate (via levies) |
| Public liability (common areas) | Same policy | Body corporate (via levies) |
| Unit contents (landlord's fixtures) | Landlord's insurance | Landlord |
| Tenant's personal belongings | Tenant's household contents insurance | Tenant |
| Improvements by owner | May need additional cover | Landlord |

## Practical Tips

**For landlords letting in sectional title:**
- Read the scheme's conduct rules BEFORE signing a lease — ensure lease terms are compatible
- Register tenant details with managing agent (if required by scheme rules)
- Attend AGMs or appoint a proxy — special levies can be costly and unexpected
- Ensure tenant has a copy of conduct rules (attach as annexure to lease)
- Budget for annual levy increases (typically 8–15% per year)
- Special levies are the owner's responsibility — do not pass to tenant unless specifically agreed

**For tenants in sectional title:**
- Request a copy of conduct rules before signing the lease
- Understand pet, parking, noise, and visitor rules — these are enforceable
- Body corporate cannot evict you — only your landlord can
- If body corporate harasses you directly, refer them to your landlord
- Your household contents are NOT covered by the building's insurance — get your own policy
