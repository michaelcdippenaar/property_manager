# Parties and Roles in SA Residential Rentals

All stakeholders in the South African rental ecosystem, their obligations, and how they interact.

---

## 1. Landlord (Property Owner)

The registered owner of the property who lets it to a tenant.

**Obligations (RHA s5A):**
- Maintain premises in habitable condition
- Carry out structural and major repairs
- Provide a written lease on request (s5(2))
- Place deposit in interest-bearing account (s5(3)(f))
- Refund deposit within prescribed timelines (s5(3)(h))
- Conduct joint inspections at move-in and move-out
- Issue rent receipts on request
- Not cut utilities or change locks (s4A — unfair practice)
- Comply with POPIA when handling tenant personal data

**Tax obligations:**
- Declare rental income to SARS on ITR12
- Register as provisional taxpayer if rental income > R30,000/year (not from employment)
- Retain records for 5 years

**Liability:**
- Ultimately liable for municipal rates and services on the property
- Liable for body corporate levies (sectional title) regardless of tenant non-payment
- Vicariously responsible for ensuring tenant complies with body corporate conduct rules

---

## 2. Tenant (Lessee)

The person who occupies the property under a lease agreement.

**Rights:**
- Right to a written lease (RHA s5(2))
- Right to habitable premises (RHA s5A)
- Right to privacy — landlord must give reasonable notice before access (minimum 24 hours)
- Right to deposit refund with interest within prescribed timelines
- Right to refer disputes to the Rental Housing Tribunal (free, binding)
- Right to cancel fixed-term lease with 20 business days' notice (CPA s14)
- Protection from illegal eviction (PIE Act)

**Obligations (RHA s5B):**
- Pay rent on the agreed date
- Maintain premises in good condition (fair wear and tear excepted)
- Not alter premises without landlord's written consent
- Not use premises for unlawful purposes
- Not sub-let without written consent
- Attend joint inspections
- Give proper written notice before vacating

---

## 3. Estate Agent / Rental Agent (Property Practitioner)

A professional who markets the property and procures tenants on the landlord's behalf.

**Regulatory framework:** Property Practitioners Act 22 of 2019 (replaced Estate Agency Affairs Act)

**Requirements:**
- Must hold a valid **Fidelity Fund Certificate (FFC)** — cannot trade without one
- Must maintain separate **trust account** with a registered bank for holding deposits and rent
- Must comply with FICA (Financial Intelligence Centre Act) — accountable institution
- Must display FFC at place of business

**Obligations:**
- Collect and verify FICA documents from all parties (ID, proof of address)
- Conduct tenant screening (credit, employment, references)
- Draft or facilitate lease agreement
- Hand over deposit to landlord or invest per landlord's instruction
- Retain FICA records for 5 years

**Fidelity Fund:** Protects consumers against theft of trust money by property practitioners

**Mandate types:**
- **Sole mandate**: Only this agent may market the property
- **Open mandate**: Multiple agents may market simultaneously
- **Procurement mandate**: Agent finds tenants only; does not manage

---

## 4. Managing Agent

A property practitioner or company that manages the property on an ongoing basis after the lease is signed.

**Scope of authority** (defined by management agreement):
- Tenant screening and onboarding
- Rent collection and arrears management
- Maintenance coordination (receiving requests, appointing contractors, approving quotes)
- Property inspections (periodic, move-in, move-out)
- Deposit management
- Lease renewals and terminations
- Serving notices on behalf of landlord
- Financial reporting to landlord

**Fiduciary duties:**
- Must act in the landlord's best interest
- Must maintain proper financial records
- Must hold tenant deposits in trust or interest-bearing account
- Must provide regular financial statements

**Fee structure:** Typically 8–12% of monthly rental (varies by region and services)

---

## 5. Rental Housing Tribunal

An independent provincial body established under RHA s13 to resolve landlord-tenant disputes.

**Key features:**
- **Free service** — no cost to either party
- Available in all 9 provinces
- Rulings are **binding** and carry the weight of a Magistrate's Court order

**Jurisdiction:**
- Unfair practices by landlords or tenants (RHA s4A)
- Deposit disputes
- Maintenance failures
- Lease interpretation disagreements
- Rent increases and escalation disputes
- Any dispute arising from the landlord-tenant relationship

**Limitations:**
- **Cannot order evictions** — landlord must apply to court under PIE Act
- Cannot hear commercial lease disputes (residential only)

**Process:**
1. Complainant files prescribed forms with supporting documents
2. Tribunal investigates and opens a case file
3. Mediation attempt (informal resolution)
4. If mediation fails → formal hearing
5. Tribunal issues binding ruling
6. Non-compliance → fines or imprisonment
7. Ruling may be taken on review to the High Court

**Resolution timeframe:** Typically within 90 days

**Provincial contacts:** See `lease-rental-agreement/references/sa-rental-law.md` for tribunal contact table

## 6. Municipality

Local government responsible for rates, utilities, and service delivery.

**Role in rentals:**
- Issues municipal accounts (rates, water, electricity, refuse, sewerage)
- Account must be in the **registered property owner's name**
- Owner is ultimately liable for municipal debt on the property (regardless of tenant non-payment)
- Issues clearance certificates for property transfers
- May install or manage prepaid meters

**Landlord's exposure:**
- If tenant doesn't pay municipal accounts, the municipality pursues the **owner**
- Outstanding municipal debt can lead to service disconnection
- Municipal debt attaches to the property and transfers to new owners

---

## 7. SARS (South African Revenue Service)

**Role in rentals:**
- Collects income tax on rental income
- Landlords must declare all rental income on ITR12
- Provisional tax registration required if rental income > R30,000/year and not from employment
- Conducts audits on rental income declarations
- See `07-rental-income-tax.md` for full details on deductions and obligations

---

## 8. Body Corporate / HOA

Governs sectional title schemes and homeowners' associations.

**Role in rentals:**
- Sets and enforces conduct rules (noise, pets, parking, common areas)
- Levies monthly contributions from owners (not tenants)
- Cannot evict tenants — only the landlord can
- Can hold the owner liable for tenant breaches of conduct rules
- May impose special levies for major repairs
- See `09-body-corporate-sectional-title.md` for full details

## Relationship Map

```
Landlord ←──management agreement──→ Managing Agent
    │                                      │
    ├── lease agreement ──→ Tenant         ├── rent collection
    │                          │           ├── maintenance coordination
    │                          │           └── inspections
    ├── mandate ──→ Estate Agent
    │              (tenant procurement)
    │
    ├── levy obligation ──→ Body Corporate/HOA
    │
    ├── rates account ──→ Municipality
    │
    └── tax return ──→ SARS

Tenant ←── dispute ──→ Rental Housing Tribunal (free, binding)
Landlord ←── dispute ──→ Rental Housing Tribunal

Eviction: Landlord → Court (PIE Act) → Sheriff → Tenant
         (Tribunal CANNOT order evictions)
```
