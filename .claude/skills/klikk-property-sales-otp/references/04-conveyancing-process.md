# Conveyancing Process — OTP to Registration

## 1. Ownership Only Passes at Registration

Under the **Deeds Registries Act 47 of 1937, s16**:

> *"The ownership of land may be conveyed from one person to another only by means of a
> deed of transfer executed or attested by the registrar."*

Until registration, the seller remains the legal owner even if the buyer has paid the
full purchase price and taken occupation. The buyer has a personal right against the
seller (based on the OTP) but no real right until registration.

---

## 2. Who Appoints the Conveyancer?

| Conveyancer Role | Appointed By |
|---|---|
| **Transfer conveyancer** (prepares and lodges the transfer) | The **seller** (or seller's agent) — long-standing SA custom |
| **Bond registration conveyancer** (registers the new bond) | The **bank** granting the bond |
| **Bond cancellation conveyancer** (cancels seller's existing bond) | The **bank whose bond is being cancelled** |

Buyers cannot insist on their own conveyancer for the transfer, but may appoint an
advisory attorney to protect their interests. The PPA (s58) prohibits agents from
compelling a party to use a specific conveyancer.

---

## 3. Full Timeline: OTP to Registration

**Typical total time: 8–12 weeks** for a straightforward freehold transfer. Complex
transactions (sectional title, municipal arrears, disputed transfers) can take
**4–6 months**.

### Phase 1: OTP Signed → Conveyancer Appointment (Week 1)

- Seller/agent instructs the transfer conveyancer
- Conveyancer issues FICA requests to buyer and seller
- Conveyancer obtains existing title deed from seller's bank and confirms bond
  cancellation requirements

### Phase 2: Bond Application and Approval (Weeks 1–5, runs in parallel)

| Step | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| Formal application to conditional approval | 5–10 working days |
| Bank valuation of property | 3–7 working days |
| Final unconditional grant | 1–3 working days after valuation |
| Bond attorneys appointed by bank | 1–3 days after grant |

### Phase 3: Transfer Document Preparation (Weeks 4–6)

- Transfer conveyancer prepares transfer documents once bond approval confirmed and
  FICA completed
- Buyer and seller sign transfer documents (power of attorney, declarations)
- Buyer pays deposit into trust (if not already done)
- Buyer pays transfer duty to SARS via conveyancer — SARS issues receipt within
  5–10 working days of eFiling

### Phase 4: Clearance Certificates (Weeks 4–8 — most common bottleneck)

These run in parallel with Phase 3 but are frequently the cause of delays:

| Certificate | Issued By | Typical Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Municipal rates clearance** | Local municipality | Cape Town: 10–15 working days; Johannesburg: 4–8 weeks; eThekwini: 3–6 weeks |
| **Levy clearance certificate** (sectional title) | Body corporate | 1–2 weeks once amounts settled |
| **HOA clearance** (gated estates) | Homeowners association | 1–3 weeks |
| **Electrical compliance certificate (COC)** | ECSA-registered electrician | 1–2 weeks (may require remediation) |
| **Gas compliance certificate** | LPGAS-registered installer | 1–2 weeks (if applicable) |
| **Plumbing certificate** | Where required by municipality | 1–2 weeks |
| **Electric fence compliance** | Approved installer | Few days (if applicable) |
| **Beetle/borer inspection** | Certified inspector | 1 week (typically Western Cape only) |

**Seller's obligations:** Electrical COC, gas, electric fence, and beetle certificates
are typically the seller's responsibility. The municipality decides which compliance
certificates are required for a given property type.

**Rates clearance detail:** The conveyancer applies to the municipality. The
municipality issues a "figures" demand requiring payment of current arrears plus a
pre-payment of typically 2–4 months of rates in advance (to cover until transfer).
This advance payment is typically the **buyer's cost** (recovered via the conveyancing
pro-rata on transfer).

### Phase 5: Simultaneous Lodgement at the Deeds Office (Weeks 8–11)

**Linked transactions** must all be lodged simultaneously:
- Transfer documents (transfer conveyancer)
- New bond documents (bond registration conveyancer)
- Bond cancellation documents (bond cancellation conveyancer)

All three attorneys coordinate. If any document packet is rejected by the Deeds Office,
the **entire lodgement** is rejected and must be corrected and re-lodged (resetting
the timeline).

**Deeds Office examination process:**

| Step | Duration |
|---|---|
| Junior examiner (technical check) | 1–2 working days |
| Senior examiner (substantive legal check) | 2–3 working days |
| Registrar (final approval and registration) | 1–2 working days |
| **Total examination** | **8–10 working days** normal load; up to 3–4 weeks during peak periods |

**Peak periods** (avoid if possible): January–February, September–October, and the
festive season. Different Deeds Offices have different backlogs — Cape Town is
generally faster than Johannesburg.

### Phase 6: Registration and Financial Settlement (Day of Registration)

- Transfer registered in the name of the buyer in the Deeds Registry — **ownership
  passes at this moment**
- Bond registered simultaneously against the title
- Seller's bond cancelled simultaneously
- Proceeds released to seller: purchase price less bond payout, agent commission,
  transfer attorney fees, rates advance, and other deductions
- Keys handed to buyer

## 4. FICA Obligations

Both conveyancers and estate agents are **accountable institutions** under the
Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001 (FICA). Each independently must
verify the parties — so a buyer and seller in a typical transaction may undergo
FICA verification four times (with the agent, transfer attorney, bond attorney,
and bond cancellation attorney).

### Documents Required (FICA KYC)

**Natural persons:**
- Certified copy of South African ID or passport
- Proof of residential address (bank statement, utility bill, or lease — not older
  than 3 months)
- Source of funds declaration (for material transactions)

**Companies/Close Corporations:**
- COR14/CoR21 (CIPC registration certificate)
- Latest CIPC confirmation of directors/members
- Resolution authorising the transaction and signatories
- FICA documents for all directors with >25% interest

**Trusts:**
- Trust deed
- Letter of authority from the Master of the High Court
- FICA documents for all trustees and beneficiaries

### FICA Reporting Obligations

| Report | Threshold / Trigger | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Cash Transaction Report (CTR) | Cash transactions of R50,000 or more | Within 3 days |
| Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) | Any suspicious transaction, regardless of amount | Without delay, within 15 days of awareness |
| Targeted Financial Sanctions | Client matches UN/SA sanctions list | Immediately — do not proceed |

**Penalties for non-compliance:** Fines up to R50 million per institution; criminal
liability up to 15 years imprisonment.

## 5. Common Causes of Delay

| Cause | Impact |
|---|---|
| Municipality slow to issue rates clearance | 4–8 weeks in Johannesburg — the single most common delay source |
| Seller fails to provide FICA documents | Blocks all proceedings |
| Seller's bond cancellation takes longer than expected | Delays linked lodgement |
| Deeds Office rejection of documents | Full re-lodgement required; adds 2–4 weeks |
| Outstanding compliance certificates | Electrician/remediation time unpredictable |
| Body corporate levy arrears | Must be settled before clearance issued |
| SARS transfer duty assessment delay | 5–10 working days normally; eFiling backlogs possible |
| Seller cannot locate original title deed | Requires replacement application; adds weeks |

---

## 6. Obligations Summary by Party

| Party | Key Obligations |
|---|---|
| **Seller** | Complete s67 mandatory disclosure form; disclose known defects; settle levy/rate arrears; sign power of attorney for conveyancer; obtain compliance certificates; cooperate with FICA |
| **Buyer** | Pay deposit per OTP; cooperate with FICA; pay transfer duty via conveyancer; secure bond finance; pay transfer attorney fees, bond registration costs |
| **Transfer Conveyancer** | Prepare transfer documents (DRA s15); verify FICA; obtain SARS receipt, rates clearance, levy clearance; coordinate simultaneous lodgement; release proceeds on registration |
| **Agent** | Obtain valid FFC; obtain s67 disclosure form; attach form to OTP; use written mandate; maintain trust account; not compel use of specific conveyancer |
