# Property Practitioners Act — Agents, Mandates, Disclosure & PPRA

## 1. Property Practitioners Act 22 of 2019 (PPA)

The PPA replaced the **Estate Agency Affairs Act 112 of 1976** with effect from
**1 February 2022**. It established the **Property Practitioners Regulatory Authority
(PPRA)** as the new sector regulator (replacing the EAAB).

"Property practitioner" is broadly defined to include:
- Estate agents (residential and commercial)
- Managing agents
- Commercial property brokers
- Auctioneers of immovable property
- Mortgage originators
- Property developers involved in sales on behalf of others
- Bond originators where they advise on property transactions

---

## 2. Fidelity Fund Certificate (FFC) — PPA ss47–50

### The Fundamental Rule

**s47:** No person may carry on business as a property practitioner without a valid FFC
issued by the PPRA.

**s48(1):** Where a practitioner employs another practitioner, both must hold valid FFCs.
For companies, CCs, trusts, and partnerships — every director, member, trustee, or
partner must individually hold an FFC.

**s48(4):** Any commission earned while an FFC was not held **must be repaid**. This
is non-negotiable and has been enforced by courts against agents who sold properties
while their FFC had lapsed.

### Disqualification Grounds (s50)

An FFC may be refused or withdrawn for:
- Non-citizenship or unlawful residence
- Prior convictions under this or similar Acts (5-year lookback)
- Findings of fraud or dishonesty
- Insolvency
- SARS tax non-compliance
- Failure to submit BEE compliance documentation
- Failure to meet training/experience standards

**Practical advice:** Always verify an agent's FFC status on the PPRA website
(theppra.org.za) before signing a mandate. An agent without a valid FFC has no right
to claim commission and faces criminal prosecution.

### Penalties for Acting Without FFC

- Criminal liability under PPA s71 — fines or imprisonment up to **10 years**
- Loss of right to claim commission
- Mandatory repayment of any commission already received (s48(4))

## 3. Mandate Types

The PPA and its Code of Conduct (Regulation 33) recognise three mandate types.
**All mandates must be in writing.**

| Type | Definition | Commission Entitlement |
|---|---|---|
| **Sole/Exclusive Mandate** | Only one agent/agency authorised to sell the property | Agent earns commission whether or not they personally introduced the buyer — including if the seller finds a buyer directly (check exact mandate wording) |
| **Open Mandate** | Multiple agents authorised simultaneously | Only the agent who is the **"effective cause"** of the sale earns commission |
| **Joint/Multi-Listing** | Multiple agencies share a single listing | Commission split per agreed formula between participating agencies |

### Key Mandate Rules

- **CPA s14** restricts fixed-term mandates to a maximum of **24 months**
- **Regulation 33.3:** A non-principal practitioner (less than 6 months registered)
  cannot enter any mandate or sale agreement unless co-signed by a qualified principal
  practitioner holding an FFC. An OTP signed by such a practitioner alone may be void
- **Regulation 37:** All agreements must contain a FFC warranty clause
- **"Effective cause" doctrine** applies to open mandates — the agent must prove they
  were the proximate cause of the sale, not merely one of several agents who showed
  the property

## 4. Mandatory Disclosure Form — PPA s67 (Regulation 36)

This is the most operationally significant change under the PPA for OTP transactions.

### Agent Obligations (s67(1))

The property practitioner must:
1. Obtain a **fully completed and signed mandatory disclosure form** from the seller
   **before accepting any mandate**
2. Provide a copy to any prospective purchaser **before they make an offer**
3. **Attach the form to the OTP/sale agreement as an integral part thereof**

### Form Requirements

- Prescribed template per **Regulation 36** — deviations require PPRA Exemption
  Committee approval; agents cannot use their own templates
- Must be signed by the seller
- Must be initialled on each page by both parties once annexed to the OTP

### Consequences of Non-Compliance (s67(2)–(3))

If the form is not completed, signed, or attached to the OTP:
- *"The agreement must be interpreted as if **no defects or deficiencies** of the
  property were disclosed."*
- This dramatically shifts risk to the seller (and potentially the agent)
- The property practitioner may be held **personally liable** by an affected consumer
  for costs arising from undisclosed defects discovered post-transfer
- Fine: **R15,000** per contravention (minor contravention under PPA)
- Records must be retained for **5 years** (s55)

### What the Form Covers (Physical Condition Questions Include)

- Damp / moisture penetration
- Roof leaks and condition
- Electrical compliance status
- Plumbing and geyser condition
- Structural defects
- Boundary/encroachment issues
- Asbestos (presence known)
- Swimming pool compliance certificate status
- Outstanding compliance certificates

**Note:** The disclosure form is not a warranty by the agent — only that the agent
obtained and distributed it. The **seller** provides the representations on the form.

### Interaction with Voetstoots and CPA

- **Private seller + voetstoots:** The disclosure form creates a written record.
  If the seller signs indicating no known defects but a defect later emerges, this
  is evidence of fraudulent concealment — undermining the voetstoots defence.
- **Developer seller + CPA applies:** The disclosure form must still be completed,
  but the voetstoots clause is void regardless.

## 5. Trust Accounts — PPA s54

- All practitioners must maintain separate trust accounts at a registered bank
- Deposits (earnest money, proceeds held in trust) must be deposited and held until
  the practitioner is lawfully entitled to the funds or receives written instructions
  to transfer
- Audited trust account reports due within 6 months of the financial year-end
- **Misappropriation of trust funds is a criminal offence**

---

## 6. Service Provider Restriction — PPA s58

An agent **may not compel** a consumer to use any particular service provider,
including:
- A specific conveyancer
- A specific bond originator
- A specific property inspector

Violation results in loss of remuneration rights and potential criminal liability.

## 7. Commission

### How Commission Is Calculated

Commission is a percentage of the purchase price, negotiated between the seller and
the agent (no legislated fixed rate since the Competition Commission's intervention
in 2003). Market norms:
- Residential: **5–7.5% + VAT** of the purchase price
- Commercial/industrial: varies, often 5–7% + VAT

### When Commission Is Earned

| Scenario | Commission Owed? |
|---|---|
| Sole mandate: buyer found by seller directly | Yes — if mandate wording is clear |
| Sole mandate: buyer found by different agency | Generally yes — check mandate wording |
| Open mandate: agent was not the effective cause | No |
| Agent's FFC lapsed at time of sale | No — must be repaid |
| Buyer defaults and OTP falls away | Generally no commission (sale not completed) — unless the mandate specifically addresses this |

### Commission from the Seller's Proceeds

Commission is paid by the seller from the proceeds of the sale, at registration,
by the transfer conveyancer. The buyer does not directly pay the agent's commission,
though it is implicitly factored into the agreed sale price.

## 8. PPRA Complaints / Ombud Process (PPA ss28–30)

**How to complain:**
- Online: theppra.org.za/complaint-online
- PPRA's IMA (Investigation Mediation and Adjudication) department

**Process:**
1. Complaint lodged online with supporting documentation
2. PPRA contacts complainant within **14 working days** with a reference number
3. Investigation → Mediation (s29) → Adjudication (s30) if unresolved
4. Adjudicators may impose fines up to Magistrates' Court limits; proceeds fund the Fidelity Fund
5. Full investigations typically take **3+ months**

**Fidelity Fund Claims (ss34–35):**
Consumers may claim against the Fidelity Fund for theft by a practitioner holding a
valid FFC (e.g., misappropriation of a deposit). Notice of claim must be given within
**3 years** of the loss.

## 9. Common Agent Pitfalls — Summary

| Pitfall | Risk |
|---|---|
| Acting without valid FFC | Forfeiture of commission + criminal liability |
| Not obtaining s67 disclosure form before mandate | R15,000 fine + personal liability for undisclosed defects |
| Obliging buyer to use specific conveyancer | Loss of commission + criminal liability (PPA s58) |
| Non-principal signing mandate without principal co-signature | Mandate/agreement potentially void (Regulation 33.3) |
| Earning commission without FFC | Must repay commission (s48(4)) |
| Not attaching disclosure form to OTP | Treated as if no defects disclosed |
| Not retaining records for 5 years | Regulatory breach; limits ability to defend complaints |
