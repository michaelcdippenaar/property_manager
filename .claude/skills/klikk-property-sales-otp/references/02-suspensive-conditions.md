# Suspensive Conditions in SA Property OTPs

## 1. What Is a Suspensive Condition?

A suspensive condition (condition precedent) is a clause that suspends the legal effect
of an OTP until a specified event occurs. Until every condition is either fulfilled or
waived:

- **No perfected sale exists** — the contract is entirely "suspended"
- Neither party can sue for breach of the sale itself
- If the condition lapses unmet, the agreement automatically falls away — no breach,
  no remedy, no damages between parties (unless one party deliberately prevented
  fulfilment)

**Extensions must be in writing, signed by both parties, and executed BEFORE the
original deadline expires.** A verbal extension or a written extension signed after
the deadline cannot revive a lapsed agreement — courts apply no "substantial
compliance" doctrine to suspensive conditions.

## 2. Bond / Finance Condition (Most Common)

### What It Is

The OTP is subject to the buyer obtaining a home loan of a specified amount within a
specified period. This is the primary exit mechanism for most residential buyers.

### NCA-Compliant Drafting (Critical)

Under the National Credit Act 34 of 2005, a bond suspensive condition is only
**fulfilled** when two things happen:

1. The bank issues a **pre-agreement statement and quotation** (NCA s92, Form 20)
   specifying: principal debt, interest rate, credit costs, total cost of credit,
   monthly instalments
2. The purchaser **accepts** that quotation in writing

**A bond "approval in principle" or AIP letter does NOT fulfil the condition.**

**Key case:** *Basson v Remini 1992(2) SA 322(N)* — condition only fulfilled on
quotation acceptance, not approval in principle. Confirmed recently in *Setso Property
Fund (Pty) Ltd v Manama (JHC, June 2024)*.

### NCA-Compliant Bond Clause (Best Practice Wording)

> *"This agreement is subject to the purchaser being issued with a pre-agreement
> statement and quotation (as contemplated in section 92 of the National Credit Act 34
> of 2005) by a financial institution for a loan of not less than R[___], secured by
> mortgage bond over the property, and the purchaser accepting such quotation, within
> [X] days of the date of this agreement."*

### NCA Bond Condition Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Risk |
|---|---|
| OTP says "bond approval" without specifying NCA quote acceptance | Condition fulfilled too early (mere AIP letter) — creates dispute |
| Purchaser deliberately refuses to accept a valid NCA quote | Seller can argue **fictional fulfilment** — condition deemed fulfilled by courts |
| Suspensive condition period too short (e.g., 14 days) | NCA quote-to-acceptance alone takes 5+ business days; process impossible in the window |
| No extension clause | Condition lapses automatically — sale void |
| Incorrect bond amount specified | *Marais v Kovacs Investments*: approval for a lesser amount means the condition failed — partial bond cannot be compelled |

### Buyer Obligations Under the Bond Condition

The buyer must apply for the bond promptly and in good faith. Failure to apply is a
**breach of the OTP**, not a failed condition. The seller can claim damages if the
buyer never applied.

### Bond Origination Process (OOBA / BetterBond)

| Stage | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| AIP (pre-qualification, before OTP) | 48–72 hours |
| Formal application to conditional approval | 5–10 working days |
| Bank valuation of property | 3–7 working days |
| Final unconditional grant | 1–3 working days after valuation |

**Recommended bond suspensive condition period:** 30 days (industry standard) — allows
for the full NCA process including any complications. 21 days is used for cash-strong
buyers; 60 days is sometimes negotiated.

## 3. Subject-to-Sale Condition (72-Hour Clause)

### What It Is

The buyer's offer is conditional on selling their current property within a specified
period (e.g., 30–90 days). This protects a buyer who cannot afford two bonds
simultaneously.

### The 72-Hour Clause (Seller Protection)

Where the OTP includes a subject-to-sale condition, the seller may continue marketing
the property. If the seller receives another acceptable offer:

1. Seller gives the first buyer **written notice** of 72 hours (3 days)
2. The 72-hour clock starts on **receipt** of the notice
3. Within 72 hours, the first buyer must either:
   - **Waive** the subject-to-sale condition (proceed without it), or
   - **Accept that the OTP falls away**
4. If the buyer waives, the sale proceeds unconditionally; if not, the seller accepts
   the better offer

**Critical drafting note:** The 72-hour clause must be expressly included in the OTP
alongside the subject-to-sale condition. An OTP without it leaves the seller unable to
accept better offers during the waiting period — potentially locking them out of the
market for months.

## 4. Inspection Condition

### What It Is

The buyer has a specified number of days to commission a professional property
inspection. If material defects are found, the buyer may cancel (or negotiate a price
reduction).

### Drafting Requirements

Vague inspection conditions create disputes. The OTP must specify:

- **Who bears inspection costs** (typically the buyer)
- **What qualifies as "material"** — a monetary threshold (e.g., defects costing more
  than R25,000 to remedy) or specific categories
- **The remedy available** — cancellation only, or cancellation plus negotiation of
  price reduction?
- **The timeframe** — typically 5–10 business days from OTP acceptance

"Subject to a satisfactory inspection" (purely subjective) has been disputed in courts.
Objective triggers are safer.

## 5. General Rules for All Suspensive Conditions

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Must be in writing | Part of the OTP (ALA s2(1) applies to the whole agreement) |
| Must have a deadline | A condition without a time limit creates indefinite uncertainty |
| Extensions must be written and pre-deadline | Verbal or post-deadline extensions do not revive a lapsed condition |
| Waiver must be in writing | A party wishing to waive a condition for their benefit must do so in writing |
| Cannot be imposed unilaterally | The other party must agree to any change in the condition |
| Lapsed condition = void OTP | The agreement falls away automatically; no breach, no damages (unless one party deliberately prevented fulfilment) |

### Fictional Fulfilment Doctrine

If a party deliberately causes the failure of a condition that was in the other party's
interest, courts may deem the condition fulfilled despite the failure. Example: a buyer
who deliberately refuses to accept a valid NCA bond quote to escape the OTP — the
seller can argue fictional fulfilment and hold the buyer to the sale.
