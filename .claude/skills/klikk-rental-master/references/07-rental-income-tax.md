# Rental Income Tax — SARS Obligations for Landlords

South African landlords must declare all rental income to SARS. This reference covers tax obligations, allowable deductions, and record-keeping requirements.

---

## Taxable Rental Income

**All rental income is taxable** — declared on the ITR12 annual tax return under "Rental income from the letting of fixed property."

Taxable rental income includes:
- Monthly rent received
- Lease premiums (lump sums paid for entering a lease) — taxable in the year received/accrued
- Key money or goodwill payments
- Income from short-term/holiday lets (Airbnb, booking platforms)
- Rental income from rooms, granny flats, outbuildings

**Deposits**: Not initially taxable if an obligation to refund exists. Become taxable only when applied to unpaid rent or damages (i.e., when the obligation to refund ceases).

---

## Provisional Tax

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Rental income > R30,000/year AND not subject to PAYE | Must register as **provisional taxpayer** |
| Submit IRP6 returns | Twice per year (August and February) with optional third submission |
| Pay estimated tax in advance | Based on projected annual rental profit |

**Penalty for non-registration**: SARS may impose penalties and interest on late provisional tax payments.

---

## Allowable Deductions

Landlords may deduct expenses **actually incurred in the production of rental income** (Income Tax Act s11).

| Deduction | What qualifies | What does NOT qualify |
|---|---|---|
| **Bond interest** | Interest portion of mortgage repayments | Capital repayment portion |
| **Rates and taxes** | Municipal property rates | — |
| **Body corporate levies** | Monthly levies | Special levies for capital improvements |
| **Insurance** | Building insurance, landlord's insurance | Tenant's household contents insurance |
| **Repairs and maintenance** | Restoring to original condition: painting, plumbing fixes, replacing broken items | Improvements that enhance the property |
| **Managing agent fees** | Monthly management fees, letting commissions | — |
| **Advertising** | Costs to find tenants: portal listings, signage, marketing | — |
| **Security** | Armed response, alarm monitoring, security upgrades | — |
| **Accounting fees** | Fees for rental income tax preparation | — |
| **Legal fees** | Lease drafting, eviction proceedings, debt collection | — |
| **Garden services** | If landlord pays for garden maintenance | — |
| **Pest control** | Regular treatments and once-off pest removal | — |
| **Wear and tear (depreciation)** | Furniture and appliances in furnished lets (SARS rates apply) | Land and buildings (no depreciation for individuals) |
| **Travel** | Reasonable travel to inspect/manage the property | Daily commute, personal travel |
| **Home office** (if applicable) | Proportionate costs if dedicated space used for property management | Only if strict SARS criteria met |

## Repairs vs Improvements — Critical Distinction

| Repairs (deductible) | Improvements (NOT deductible — capital expenditure) |
|---|---|
| Fixing a leaking roof | Adding a new room |
| Replacing broken tiles | Installing new flooring where none existed |
| Repainting walls | Building a pool or carport |
| Fixing plumbing | Upgrading kitchen to higher spec |
| Replacing a broken geyser with equivalent | Installing solar panels |
| Patching cracked walls | Renovating bathroom |

**Rule**: Repairs **restore** the property to its original condition. Improvements **enhance** or add new features.

**Capital improvements** are added to the **base cost** of the property, reducing Capital Gains Tax on eventual sale.

---

## Apportionment (Partial Use)

If only part of a property is rented:
- Expenses must be **apportioned** based on the rented area as a percentage of total area
- Example: If you rent out 40% of a property, you may deduct 40% of shared expenses (rates, insurance, etc.)

If the property is only rented for part of the year:
- Apportion expenses based on the rental period as a proportion of the full year

---

## Capital Gains Tax (CGT) on Property Disposal

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary residence exclusion** | Does NOT apply to rental properties (only owner-occupied) |
| **Inclusion rate** | 40% for individuals (i.e., 40% of the capital gain is added to taxable income) |
| **Base cost** | Purchase price + transfer costs + capital improvements (not repairs) |
| **Proceeds** | Sale price minus agent commission and transfer costs |
| **Annual exclusion** | R40,000 per individual per year (across all capital gains) |

## Record Keeping

| Record | Retention period | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| Rental income records | **5 years** from date of assessment | Tax Administration Act s29 |
| Expense invoices and receipts | **5 years** | Tax Administration Act s29 |
| Lease agreements | **5 years** after lease ends | Tax + prescription period |
| Bank statements (rental account) | **5 years** | Tax Administration Act |
| Bond statements | **5 years** | Tax Administration Act |
| Improvement/renovation invoices | **Indefinitely** (needed for CGT base cost) | Best practice |

---

## Common SARS Audit Triggers

| Trigger | Why it's flagged |
|---|---|
| Rental income not declared but property registered in name | SARS cross-references Deeds Office records |
| Large deductions relative to rental income | Consistent rental losses may indicate inflated deductions |
| Repairs claimed in year of purchase | SARS may query whether these are actually improvements |
| No provisional tax registration with rental income > R30,000 | Automatic compliance flag |
| Inconsistent declarations year-on-year | Sudden changes in income or expenses |

## Practical Example

**Property**: 2-bedroom apartment rented at R10,000/month

| Item | Annual amount |
|---|---|
| **Gross rental income** | R120,000 |
| Less: Bond interest | (R48,000) |
| Less: Levies | (R18,000) |
| Less: Insurance | (R4,800) |
| Less: Agent fees (10%) | (R12,000) |
| Less: Repairs | (R6,000) |
| Less: Rates and taxes | (R8,400) |
| **Net rental income (taxable)** | **R22,800** |

This R22,800 is added to the landlord's other taxable income and taxed at their marginal rate.

---

## Multiple Properties

- Each property's income and expenses are calculated separately
- A **loss** on one property can be offset against **profit** on another rental property
- Net rental loss can be offset against other income (salary, business income)
- SARS may query persistent losses — ensure all deductions are legitimate and documented
