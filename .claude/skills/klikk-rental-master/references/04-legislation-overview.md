# South African Rental Legislation Overview

Seven statutes governing residential property rentals. For detailed RHA/CPA/POPIA section-by-section analysis, see also `lease-rental-agreement/references/sa-rental-law.md`.

---

## 1. Rental Housing Act 50 of 1999 (as amended 2014)

**Purpose**: Primary statute governing residential landlord-tenant relationships in South Africa.

**Key sections for rentals:**

| Section | Subject | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| s4A | Unfair practices | Lists prohibited landlord and tenant conduct |
| s5(2) | Written lease | Tenant has right to a written lease |
| s5(3) | Lease contents | Mandatory provisions: parties, premises, term, rent, deposit, inspections |
| s5(5) | House rules | Must be annexed to lease; after expiry, lease continues month-to-month |
| s5A | Landlord obligations | Habitability, structural repairs, receipts, lease copy |
| s5B | Tenant obligations | Pay rent, maintain premises, no unlawful use, no unauthorised alterations |
| s13 | Rental Housing Tribunal | Provincial dispute resolution body; free, binding rulings |

**Cross-reference**: See `lease-rental-agreement/references/sa-rental-law.md` for full section-by-section breakdown including deposit rules, CPA checklist, and tribunal contacts.

## 2. Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 (CPA)

**Purpose**: Protects tenants as consumers in fixed-term lease agreements where the landlord lets property in the ordinary course of business.

**Applicability**: Applies when landlord is in the business of letting (owns multiple properties, uses an agent, earns rental income commercially). Does NOT apply to once-off private lets between individuals.

**Key sections:**

| Section | Subject | Impact on rentals |
|---|---|---|
| s14 | Fixed-term agreements | Max 24 months; tenant can cancel with 20 business days' notice; landlord must notify 40–80 business days before expiry |
| s16 | Cooling-off | 5 business days to cancel unsolicited (direct marketing) agreements |
| s22 | Plain language | Lease must be in plain, understandable language |
| s48 | Unfair terms | Excessively one-sided clauses are void |
| s54 | Quality of service | Premises must be fit for purpose |

**Critical rule**: Cancellation penalty must be **reasonable and proportionate** — not the full remaining rent.

## 3. Prevention of Illegal Eviction Act 19 of 1998 (PIE Act)

**Purpose**: Protects ALL occupiers (tenants, unlawful occupiers, former owners) from illegal eviction. Requires court order for any eviction.

**Key sections:**

| Section | Subject | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| s1 | Definitions | "Unlawful occupier" = person occupying without consent or right |
| s4 | Eviction of unlawful occupiers | Court application required; 14 days' notice; municipality must be served; "just and equitable" test |
| s4(6)–(7) | Vulnerable persons | Court must consider elderly, children, disabled, female-headed households |
| s5 | Urgent proceedings | Available for recent occupation (< 6 months) or imminent danger |
| s8 | Prohibition of self-help | Changing locks, cutting services, removing belongings = criminal offence |

**Critical rule**: A landlord may NEVER evict without a court order. Self-help eviction is a criminal offence.

**See**: `06-eviction-and-pie-act.md` for full eviction process and timelines.

## 4. Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA)

**Purpose**: Governs collection, storage, use, and sharing of personal information — applies to all tenant data handling.

**Key requirements for landlords/agents:**

| Requirement | POPIA section | Rental application |
|---|---|---|
| Lawful processing | s9–12 | Must have consent or contractual necessity for collecting tenant data |
| Purpose limitation | s13 | Data collected only for lease administration, not other purposes |
| Minimisation | s10 | Collect only what is necessary (don't over-collect) |
| Security safeguards | s19 | Secure storage of ID numbers, bank details, FICA documents |
| Data subject rights | s23–25 | Tenants can request access to, correction of, or deletion of their data |
| Breach notification | s22 | Must notify Information Regulator and affected tenants of data breaches |
| Cross-border transfer | s72 | Sending tenant data overseas (e.g., cloud services) requires adequate protection |

**Penalties**: Up to R10 million fine and/or imprisonment for serious non-compliance.

**Retention**: Tenant records should be kept for duration of lease + 3 years (prescription period for contractual claims).

## 5. Sectional Titles Schemes Management Act 8 of 2011 (STSMA)

**Purpose**: Governs the management of sectional title schemes (apartments, townhouse complexes) by body corporates.

**Relevance to rentals:**

| Aspect | Rule |
|---|---|
| Levies | Owner (landlord) is liable for levies — NOT the tenant |
| Conduct rules | Tenants are bound by scheme conduct rules (noise, pets, parking, common areas) |
| Management rules | Govern body corporate operations — owners vote, not tenants |
| Exclusive use areas | Parking bays, gardens, storerooms — defined in sectional plan |
| Alterations | Need body corporate AND landlord approval |
| Owner liability | Body corporate can hold owner liable for tenant's breach of rules |

**Critical rule**: Body corporate cannot evict a tenant — only the landlord can, via PIE Act.

**See**: `09-body-corporate-sectional-title.md` for full details.

## 6. Community Schemes Ombud Service Act 9 of 2011 (CSOS Act)

**Purpose**: Provides alternative dispute resolution for community scheme disputes (sectional title, HOA, share block).

**Jurisdiction**: Disputes between:
- Owners and body corporate
- Body corporate and managing agent
- Owners within a scheme
- **NOT** landlord-tenant disputes (those go to Rental Housing Tribunal)

**Powers:**
- Mediate and adjudicate scheme governance disputes
- Order scheme compliance
- Order tenants to pay rent directly to scheme administrators to clear landlord's levy arrears

**Process:**
1. Application filed with CSOS
2. Conciliation attempted
3. If unresolved → adjudication
4. Binding order issued

**Key distinction**: CSOS handles scheme governance disputes; Rental Housing Tribunal handles landlord-tenant disputes.

## 7. Property Practitioners Act 22 of 2019 (PPA)

**Purpose**: Replaced the Estate Agency Affairs Act. Regulates all property practitioners including estate agents, managing agents, and auctioneers.

**Key requirements:**

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Fidelity Fund Certificate (FFC) | Mandatory — cannot trade, charge fees, or advertise without one |
| Trust accounts | Must maintain separate trust account(s) with registered bank |
| Trust account audits | Must appoint independent auditor; submit audit reports to PPRA |
| FICA compliance | Property practitioners are accountable institutions under FIC Act |
| Disclosure | Must disclose FFC status, any conflicts of interest, commission structure |
| Record keeping | Transaction records, trust account records, FICA documents |

**Fidelity Fund**: Reimburses persons who suffer loss due to theft of trust money by a practitioner holding a valid FFC.

**Regulatory body**: Property Practitioners Regulatory Authority (PPRA) — issues FFCs, investigates complaints, enforces compliance.

**Disqualification grounds**: Convicted of fraud/dishonesty, unrehabilitated insolvent, lacking tax clearance, lacking BEE certificate, insufficient training.

## Legislation Interaction Map

```
RHA ──── Primary landlord-tenant statute
 │
 ├── CPA supplements (fixed-term agreements, consumer protection)
 ├── PIE Act governs (eviction — court order mandatory)
 ├── POPIA governs (tenant data handling)
 │
 └── For sectional title properties:
      ├── STSMA governs (body corporate, levies, rules)
      └── CSOS Act governs (scheme disputes)

PPA ──── Regulates estate agents and managing agents
 │
 └── FICA requires (identity verification by practitioners)
```

**Which Act applies when:**

| Situation | Primary Act |
|---|---|
| Drafting a lease | RHA + CPA |
| Deposit dispute | RHA → Rental Housing Tribunal |
| Evicting a tenant | PIE Act → Court |
| Handling tenant data | POPIA |
| Body corporate dispute | STSMA + CSOS Act |
| Agent misconduct | PPA → PPRA |
| Unfair lease terms | CPA s48 |
| Utility disconnection by landlord | RHA s4A (unfair practice) + PIE s8 |
