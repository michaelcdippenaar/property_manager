# Deposit Rules — Complete Lifecycle

Everything about rental security deposits in South Africa: caps, accounts, inspections, refund timelines, deductions, and disputes.

---

## Deposit Cap

- **Maximum**: Cannot exceed the amount prescribed by the Minister — currently **2× monthly rental** per RHA regulations
- Exceeding this cap is an **unfair practice** under RHA s4A
- The deposit is negotiable (can be less than 2×) but landlords typically request 1–2 months' rent

---

## Interest-Bearing Account Requirement

| Rule | Detail | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| Account type | Must be an **interest-bearing account** with a financial institution | RHA s5(3)(f) |
| Interest rate | Must be **no less than** the standard savings account rate | RHA regulations |
| Interest ownership | All interest accrued belongs to the **tenant** | RHA s5(3)(f) |
| Proof | Tenant may request written proof of accrued interest **at any time** | RHA s5(3)(f) |
| Commingling | Landlord may NOT mix deposit funds with personal funds | Best practice / trust principles |

**Managing agents**: Must hold deposits in a **trust account** (PPA 22/2019 trust account requirements apply).

## Deposit Lifecycle Flowchart

Mapped to the 15-stage circular rental lifecycle (see `01-rental-workflow.md`).

```
STAGE 7: DEPOSIT PAYMENT (during previous tenant's notice period)
   Tenant pays deposit (max 2× rent) + first month's rent
        │
   LODGING (within 3-7 days of receipt)
   Landlord places in interest-bearing account
   Provides receipt to tenant with bank details
   ⚠️ Interest accrues from DATE OF LODGING, not lease start date
        │
STAGE 10: MOVE-IN INSPECTION (morning of lease start)
   Joint inspection → signed report → baseline established
        │
STAGES 12-13: ACTIVE TENANCY (12 months)
   Deposit held securely; not available for rent offset
   Interest continues accruing for tenant's benefit
   Tenant may request proof of interest at any time
        │
STAGE 8: MOVE-OUT INSPECTION (afternoon of last day)
   Joint inspection → comparison to ingoing report
   Determine: fair wear and tear vs tenant damage
        │
STAGE 15: DEPOSIT REFUND
   Deposit + ALL accrued interest − lawful deductions = refund amount
   Timeline depends on scenario (see below)
```

## Refund Timelines

| Scenario | Timeline | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| **No damage**, no unpaid amounts | **7 days** after lease termination | Both parties attended outgoing inspection |
| **Damage found**, repairs needed | **14 days** after repairs completed | Landlord must provide written quotation within 14 days of inspection |
| **Tenant absent** from outgoing inspection | **21 days** from lease expiration | Landlord must inspect within 7 days of expiry; refund within 21 days regardless of repair status |

**Interest**: Must always be included in the refund amount.

---

## Lawful Deductions

Landlord may **only** deduct for:

| Deduction | Requirement | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Damage beyond fair wear and tear | Must have invoices or written quotations | Holes in walls, broken fixtures, cigarette burns |
| Unpaid rent | Verified amounts owed per lease | Outstanding month(s) of rent |
| Unpaid utilities | Documented amounts owed | Outstanding water/electricity bills |
| Professional cleaning | Only if property is excessively soiled | Not normal end-of-tenancy cleaning |

**Every deduction must be:**
- Itemised in writing
- Supported by an invoice or quotation from a service provider
- Reasonable and proportionate to actual cost

## Fair Wear and Tear vs Tenant Damage

| Fair wear and tear (landlord's cost) | Tenant damage (deductible from deposit) |
|---|---|
| Faded or slightly discoloured paint | Crayon marks, stains, or graffiti on walls |
| Minor scuff marks on floors | Deep scratches, gouges, or burn marks |
| Worn carpet from normal foot traffic | Stains, tears, or pet damage to carpet |
| Loose door handles from normal use | Broken doors, hinges, or locks |
| Cracked window from foundation movement | Broken window from impact |
| Faded curtains from sunlight | Torn or missing curtains |
| Minor plaster cracks from settling | Large holes from removed fixtures |
| Worn kitchen countertop | Burn marks or cuts on countertop |

**Key principle**: Fair wear and tear is the natural deterioration from ordinary, reasonable use of the premises. Anything beyond this is tenant damage.

## Inspection Requirements for Deposit Claims

### Ingoing Inspection
- **Mandatory** — must be conducted jointly by landlord and tenant
- **Timing**: Before or on the day tenant takes occupation
- **Documentation**: Room-by-room condition, date/time-stamped photos, both parties sign
- **Consequence of skipping**: Landlord loses the right to claim damages from the deposit

### Outgoing Inspection
- **Should be joint** — both parties attend
- **Timing**: Within 3 days before or 7 days after lease termination
- **If tenant absent**: Landlord gives 3 days written notice, inspects alone, has 21 days to refund
- **Documentation**: Comparison to ingoing report, date/time-stamped photos, both parties sign

## Dispute Resolution

### Step 1: Direct Communication
- Tenant writes to landlord requesting refund with calculation
- Allow 7 days for response

### Step 2: Rental Housing Tribunal
If direct communication fails:

1. **File complaint** — prescribed forms available from provincial Tribunal
2. **Supporting documents required**:
   - Copy of lease agreement
   - Ingoing inspection report
   - Outgoing inspection report
   - Deposit receipt
   - Proof of rent payments
   - Any correspondence about the deposit
   - ID copies
3. **Tribunal process**: Investigation → mediation → hearing (if needed) → binding ruling
4. **Cost**: Free to both parties
5. **Timeline**: Typically resolved within 90 days
6. **Ruling**: Carries weight of Magistrate's Court order

### Step 3: Court Action
- If Tribunal ruling is not complied with, enforce via Magistrate's Court
- Alternatively, bypass Tribunal and go directly to Small Claims Court (claims up to R20,000) or Magistrate's Court

## Common Landlord Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Not placing deposit in interest-bearing account | Breach of RHA; Tribunal will order refund with market-rate interest |
| No ingoing inspection | Cannot claim damages — must refund full deposit |
| Deducting without invoices | Tribunal rejects unsupported deductions |
| Exceeding refund timeline | Tribunal may award additional damages |
| Deducting for fair wear and tear | Unlawful deduction — Tribunal orders refund |
| Commingling with personal funds | Trust violation; potential PPRA disciplinary action (if agent) |
| Using deposit as last month's rent | Not permitted unless lease specifically allows it |
| Not paying interest to tenant | Breach of RHA; interest belongs to tenant |

## Practical Tips

**For landlords:**
- Open a dedicated savings account for each deposit (or use a managing agent's trust account)
- Always conduct ingoing and outgoing inspections with the tenant present
- Take extensive dated photos at both inspections
- Get written quotations for repairs before making deductions
- Process refunds within the prescribed timelines — delays invite Tribunal complaints

**For tenants:**
- Attend both inspections — your absence weakens your position
- Keep a copy of the ingoing inspection report and all photos
- Request proof of interest-bearing account and accrued interest
- Document the property condition when you leave (your own photos)
- If deposit is not refunded on time, file immediately with the Tribunal
